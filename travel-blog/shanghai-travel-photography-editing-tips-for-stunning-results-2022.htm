Shanghai Travel Photography: Editing Tips for Stunning Results

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shanghai is a photographer’s dream—where futuristic skyscrapers meet historic alleyways, neon lights dance along the Huangpu River, and the energy of the city pulses through every frame. But capturing the essence of Shanghai is only half the battle. The real magic happens in post-processing, where you can transform good shots into breathtaking masterpieces. Whether you’re editing shots of The Bund, Yu Garden, or the vibrant streets of Tianzifang, these tips will help you elevate your travel photography.

Essential Editing Tools for Shanghai Photography

Before diving into techniques, let’s talk about the tools you’ll need. While professional software like Adobe Lightroom and Photoshop offer the most control, mobile apps like Snapseed, VSCO, and Lightroom Mobile are great for quick edits on the go.

1. Lightroom: The Powerhouse for Travel Photos

Lightroom’s non-destructive editing and presets make it ideal for travel photography. Key features to master:
- Presets: Save time by creating or downloading presets tailored to Shanghai’s unique lighting (e.g., neon glow, misty mornings).
- Graduated Filters: Enhance skies over Lujiazui or add drama to sunrise shots at The Bund.
- HSL Sliders: Adjust hues to make Shanghai’s neon signs pop or soften the greens in Fuxing Park.

2. Snapseed: Quick & Powerful Mobile Edits

For smartphone photographers, Snapseed’s selective adjustments and healing tools are perfect for fixing reflections in glass skyscrapers or removing crowds from Nanjing Road.

Editing Techniques for Iconic Shanghai Scenes

The Bund: Balancing Lights and Shadows

The Bund’s skyline is a mix of historic architecture and modern towers, often shot at golden hour or night.

Enhancing Night Shots

  • Reduce Highlights: Prevent blown-out lights from skyscrapers.
  • Boost Shadows: Reveal details in darker areas of colonial buildings.
  • Add Clarity: Emphasize the textures of stone facades.

Golden Hour Magic

  • Warm Tones: Increase temperature slightly for a rich, golden glow.
  • Dehaze: Cut through Shanghai’s occasional mist for sharper shots.

Yu Garden: Vintage Charm with a Modern Twist

This Ming Dynasty garden contrasts with the city’s modernity.

Bringing Out Traditional Details

  • Vibrance Over Saturation: Avoid oversaturating the red lanterns and greenery.
  • Dodge & Burn: Highlight intricate carvings on pavilions.

Creating a Dreamy Effect

  • Subtle Vignette: Draw focus to the center of classical bridges.
  • Soft Contrast: Mimic the softness of traditional Chinese paintings.

Tianzifang & French Concession: Street Photography Edits

These artsy districts are full of graffiti, boutique cafés, and narrow lanes.

Color Grading for Hipster Vibes

  • Teal & Orange Tones: A popular combo for urban shots.
  • Grain Effect: Add film-like texture to alleyway photos.

Removing Distractions

  • Spot Healing: Erase stray tourists or modern signage.
  • Perspective Correction: Fix leaning walls in tight shots.

Advanced Tricks for Next-Level Edits

Sky Replacements for Moody Skies

Shanghai’s weather can be unpredictable. Use Photoshop or Luminar AI to swap dull skies with dramatic clouds, especially for shots of the Oriental Pearl Tower.

Selective Color Pop

Make one element stand out—like a red lantern in a monochrome scene—by desaturating other colors.

HDR Blending for High-Contrast Scenes

Merge multiple exposures to capture details in both shadows (like underpasses in People’s Square) and highlights (glass facades in Pudong).

Final Checks Before Exporting

  • Sharpening: Apply selectively to avoid noise in night shots.
  • Crop for Composition: Use the rule of thirds to balance skyscrapers and waterways.
  • Watermarking: Protect your work subtly if sharing online.

Shanghai’s dynamic landscapes deserve edits that match their energy. With these techniques, your photos won’t just document the city—they’ll tell its story.
