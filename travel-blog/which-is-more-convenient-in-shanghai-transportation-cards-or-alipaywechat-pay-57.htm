Which is More Convenient in Shanghai: Transportation Cards or Alipay/WeChat Pay?

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Navigating Shanghai’s vast transportation network requires efficiency and the right payment tools. The city offers two primary options for travelers and locals alike: the traditional Shanghai Public Transportation Card (commonly known as the jiaotong card) and modern mobile payment methods like Alipay and WeChat Pay. Each option has its own merits and drawbacks, depending on your needs, familiarity with technology, and the duration of your stay. Let’s explore how these two options compare in convenience, usability, and accessibility.

Overview of Shanghai Public Transportation Card

What is the Transportation Card?

The Shanghai Public Transportation Card is a prepaid, contactless card that can be used across various modes of transport, including buses, subways, ferries, and even taxis. The card is a staple for commuters and a reliable choice for tourists.

How to Get It

You can purchase the card at:

  • Metro stations
  • Designated convenience stores like FamilyMart or Lawson
  • Customer service centers across the city

The card requires a deposit (usually 20 RMB) and can be loaded with credit starting from as little as 10 RMB.

Benefits of the Transportation Card

  1. Wide Acceptance: The card is accepted on nearly all forms of public transport.
  2. Discounts: Frequent users enjoy discounted fares, such as a 10% discount after the first fare within a 90-minute window on buses and subways.
  3. Ease of Use: Simply tap the card on the reader when entering and exiting the subway or bus.
  4. No Dependence on Internet: Unlike mobile payment options, the card functions independently of any internet connection.

Drawbacks of the Transportation Card

  1. Reloading Challenges: If you run out of credit, you’ll need to find a reloading station, which might not always be nearby.
  2. Physical Card: Losing or forgetting the card can cause inconvenience, especially if you’re far from a purchasing center.

Alipay and WeChat Pay for Transportation

What Are Alipay and WeChat Pay?

Alipay and WeChat Pay are mobile payment platforms that have revolutionized the way people pay for goods and services in China, including public transportation. They allow users to link their bank accounts or credit cards to their mobile wallets for seamless, cashless transactions.

How They Work for Public Transport

  1. Metro: Scan a QR code at the subway gates. The fare is deducted automatically from your linked wallet.
  2. Bus: Present a QR code to the scanner near the driver.
  3. Taxis and Shared Bikes: Many taxis and bike-sharing apps like HelloBike and Meituan also accept payments via these platforms.

Benefits of Alipay and WeChat Pay

  1. Convenience: No need to carry a separate card. Everything is on your smartphone.
  2. No Preloading Required: Unlike transportation cards, you don’t need to preload credit. Payments are deducted directly from your bank account.
  3. Transaction History: Keep track of your expenses easily within the app.
  4. Multi-Purpose: Besides transport, the apps can be used for shopping, dining, and other activities.

Drawbacks of Alipay and WeChat Pay

  1. Internet Dependency: A stable internet connection is required to generate QR codes for payment.
  2. Setup Requirements: International visitors may face challenges setting up these apps if they don’t have a Chinese bank account or phone number.
  3. Battery Life: If your phone dies, you’ll lose access to both payment methods.

Comparing the Two Options

Accessibility

  • Transportation Card: Widely available at metro stations and convenient for immediate use.
  • Alipay/WeChat Pay: Requires a smartphone, internet connection, and initial setup, which may be a barrier for short-term visitors.

Cost Efficiency

  • Transportation Card: Offers small discounts for frequent users, making it more cost-effective for daily commuters.
  • Alipay/WeChat Pay: No additional discounts but eliminates the need for preloading credit.

Usability for Tourists

  • Transportation Card: Ideal for tourists staying a few days. Easy to get, use, and return for a refund.
  • Alipay/WeChat Pay: Great for tech-savvy visitors with access to the required setup. Offers the flexibility to pay for other services like bike rentals and shopping.

Speed

  • Transportation Card: Faster during peak hours since tapping is often quicker than scanning QR codes.
  • Alipay/WeChat Pay: QR code scanning may cause slight delays if internet speed is slow.

Practical Scenarios

Short Stay or Limited Phone Access

For a tourist visiting Shanghai for a few days, the transportation card is a straightforward option. It requires no setup, works offline, and can be refunded before leaving the city.

Long-Term Stay

Expats or long-term visitors might prefer Alipay or WeChat Pay for their versatility and seamless integration with other services. After the initial setup, these apps provide a modern, all-in-one payment solution.

Language Barriers

The transportation card eliminates language concerns as it only requires a simple tap. Alipay and WeChat Pay might involve more interaction during setup, which could be daunting without Mandarin proficiency.

Tips for Using Either Option

Transportation Card

  1. Keep the card in a separate compartment to avoid interference with other cards.
  2. Check your remaining balance periodically to avoid being caught without enough credit.
  3. Return the card at designated counters to get your deposit back.

Alipay/WeChat Pay

  1. Set up your wallet before arriving in Shanghai to avoid connectivity issues.
  2. Keep a power bank handy to ensure your phone is always charged.
  3. Bookmark the transportation QR code for easy access in crowded metro stations.

Conclusion

Both the Shanghai Public Transportation Card and Alipay/WeChat Pay offer unique advantages, catering to different types of travelers. Choosing between them depends on your familiarity with technology, the duration of your stay, and personal preferences. Regardless of the option you choose, Shanghai’s efficient and well-connected transportation system ensures a smooth travel experience across this vibrant city.
