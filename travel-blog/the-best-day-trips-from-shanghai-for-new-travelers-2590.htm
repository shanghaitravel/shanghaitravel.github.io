The Best Day Trips from Shanghai for New Travelers

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shanghai is a dazzling metropolis with endless skyscrapers, bustling markets, and a vibrant nightlife. But sometimes, travelers crave a break from the urban jungle to explore the natural beauty, historic towns, and cultural gems just outside the city. If you're new to Shanghai and looking for the best day trips, here’s a curated list of must-visit destinations that are easy to reach and perfect for a quick escape.

1. Hangzhou: The Serene West Lake Escape

Why Visit?

Hangzhou, just an hour away by high-speed train, is famous for its breathtaking West Lake (Xi Hu), a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The city blends natural beauty with rich history, making it a top choice for a relaxing day trip.

Top Attractions

  • West Lake: Rent a bike or take a leisurely boat ride to admire the scenic views, pagodas, and willow-lined pathways.
  • Lingyin Temple: One of China’s most significant Buddhist temples, featuring intricate carvings and peaceful surroundings.
  • Longjing Tea Plantations: Sample some of China’s finest green tea while learning about traditional tea-making.

Getting There

  • By Train: High-speed trains from Shanghai Hongqiao Station take about 1 hour.
  • By Car: Around 2.5 hours via the G60 expressway.

2. Suzhou: The Venice of the East

Why Visit?

Known for its classical gardens, canals, and silk production, Suzhou is a charming city that offers a glimpse into China’s imperial past.

Top Attractions

  • Humble Administrator’s Garden: A masterpiece of Chinese landscape design with pavilions, ponds, and rockeries.
  • Tiger Hill: Home to the iconic Leaning Tower of Suzhou and ancient legends.
  • Pingjiang Road: A historic street lined with traditional tea houses, silk shops, and snack stalls.

Getting There

  • By Train: High-speed trains take 30 minutes from Shanghai.
  • By Bus: Around 1.5 hours via express coaches.

3. Zhujiajiao: Shanghai’s Ancient Water Town

Why Visit?

If you don’t have time for a long trip, Zhujiajiao is the perfect alternative—a well-preserved water town just an hour from Shanghai.

Top Attractions

  • Fangsheng Bridge: A stunning stone bridge offering picturesque canal views.
  • Kezhi Garden: A tranquil classical garden with pavilions and lotus ponds.
  • Boat Rides: Glide through narrow waterways on a traditional wooden boat.

Getting There

  • By Metro: Take Line 17 to Zhujiajiao Station (~1 hour).
  • By Taxi: Around 45 minutes from downtown Shanghai.

4. Moganshan: A Mountain Retreat

Why Visit?

For nature lovers, Moganshan is a lush bamboo-covered mountain area ideal for hiking, biking, and unwinding in boutique lodges.

Top Attractions

  • Bamboo Forests: Walk or bike through serene bamboo trails.
  • Jian Lake: A peaceful spot for picnics and short hikes.
  • Historic Villas: Explore early 20th-century European-style summer homes.

Getting There

  • By Car: Around 2.5 hours from Shanghai.
  • By Bus: Direct buses take about 3 hours.

5. Nanjing: A Blend of History and Modernity

Why Visit?

As a former capital of China, Nanjing is packed with historical landmarks, from ancient city walls to poignant memorials.

Top Attractions

  • Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum: A grand tribute to the founding father of modern China.
  • Confucius Temple (Fuzimiao): A lively area with street food and traditional architecture.
  • Xuanwu Lake: A scenic park perfect for a relaxing stroll.

Getting There

  • By Train: High-speed trains take 1.5 hours from Shanghai.

6. Tongli: Another Charming Water Town

Why Visit?

Less touristy than Zhujiajiao, Tongli offers an authentic glimpse into ancient canal life.

Top Attractions

  • Tuisi Garden: A beautifully preserved classical garden.
  • Three Bridges: Iconic stone bridges symbolizing luck and prosperity.
  • Ming & Qing Street: A historic lane with traditional shops and teahouses.

Getting There

  • By Bus: Around 2 hours from Shanghai.

7. Shaoxing: The Town of Scholars and Rice Wine

Why Visit?

Famous for its literary heritage and Shaoxing wine, this town is a cultural delight.

Top Attractions

  • Lu Xun’s Former Residence: Explore the home of China’s most famous modern writer.
  • East Lake: A scenic quarry-turned-park with boat tours.
  • Shaoxing Wine Tasting: Sample the region’s signature fermented rice wine.

Getting There

  • By Train: High-speed trains take about 2 hours.

8. Qiandao Lake: The Thousand-Island Paradise

Why Visit?

Known for its crystal-clear waters and forested islands, Qiandao Lake is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts.

Top Attractions

  • Island Hopping: Visit themed islands like Bird Island and Monkey Island.
  • Water Sports: Try kayaking, paddleboarding, or cycling around the lake.

Getting There

  • By Bus: Around 4 hours from Shanghai.

Final Tips for Day Trippers

  • Book Train Tickets Early: High-speed trains can sell out quickly, especially on weekends.
  • Pack Light: Most trips require minimal luggage—just essentials like water, snacks, and a camera.
  • Check Weather Forecasts: Some destinations, like Moganshan, are best enjoyed in clear weather.

Whether you're into history, nature, or just a peaceful escape, these day trips from Shanghai offer something for every traveler. So pack your bag, hop on a train, and discover the beauty beyond the city!
