The Ultimate 3-Day Shanghai Trip for Solo Travelers

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shanghai, the "Paris of the East," is a dream destination for solo travelers. With its futuristic skyline, rich history, and vibrant street life, the city offers endless opportunities for adventure. Whether you're into food, culture, or nightlife, this 3-day itinerary will help you make the most of your solo trip.

Day 1: Exploring the Heart of Shanghai

Morning: The Bund & Nanjing Road

Start your day at The Bund, Shanghai’s iconic waterfront. The skyline across the Huangpu River is breathtaking, especially in the morning light. Snap some photos, then stroll along Nanjing Road, one of the world’s busiest shopping streets. Even if shopping isn’t your thing, the energy here is contagious.

Pro Tip: Grab a coffee from %Arabica (a local favorite) and people-watch from their Bund location.

Afternoon: Yu Garden & Old Shanghai

Head to Yu Garden, a 16th-century classical Chinese garden. Wander through its pavilions, rockeries, and koi ponds—it’s a peaceful escape from the city buzz. Right outside, Chenghuangmiao (Old Street) is packed with souvenir shops and street food stalls. Try xiaolongbao (soup dumplings) at Nanxiang Steamed Bun Restaurant—they’re legendary.

Evening: Huangpu River Cruise & Lujiazui

As the sun sets, take a Huangpu River cruise for stunning views of both The Bund and Lujiazui, Shanghai’s futuristic financial district. Afterward, head to Lujiazui to visit the Shanghai Tower (the world’s second-tallest building). The observation deck offers a jaw-dropping 360° view.

Nightlife Option: If you’re up for it, check out Bar Rouge for cocktails with a view.

Day 2: Culture & Hidden Gems

Morning: Tianzifang & French Concession

Start your day in Tianzifang, a labyrinth of artsy alleyways filled with boutiques, cafés, and galleries. It’s perfect for solo explorers who love quirky finds. Then, wander into the French Concession, where tree-lined streets and colonial architecture create a European vibe. Don’t miss Fuxing Park, where locals practice tai chi and ballroom dancing.

Food Stop: Try shengjianbao (pan-fried pork buns) at Yang’s Fry Dumplings.

Afternoon: Shanghai Museum & People’s Square

Dive into history at the Shanghai Museum (free entry!). Its ancient Chinese art collection is world-class. Nearby, People’s Square is a great spot to relax. If you’re lucky, you might catch the weekend marriage market—a fascinating local tradition where parents advertise their single children.

Evening: Jing’an Temple & Night Markets

Visit Jing’an Temple, a stunning golden Buddhist temple surrounded by skyscrapers. Then, head to Shouning Road Night Market for street food galore. Try chuanr (spicy skewers) and jianbing (savory crepes).

Alternative: If markets aren’t your scene, Found 158 is a trendy underground bar hub.

Day 3: Modern Shanghai & Day Trips

Morning: M50 Creative Park

Art lovers will adore M50, a converted factory complex filled with avant-garde galleries and street art. It’s low-key compared to other tourist spots, making it ideal for solo reflection.

Afternoon: Zhujiajiao Water Town (Optional Day Trip)

If you have extra time, take a 1-hour trip to Zhujiajiao, the "Venice of Shanghai." This ancient water town features canals, stone bridges, and tea houses. It’s a serene contrast to the city’s hustle.

No Time? Stay in Shanghai and explore Xintiandi, a chic dining and shopping district with a mix of old and new architecture.

Evening: Final Night Out in Shanghai

End your trip with a rooftop drink at The Nest or live music at JZ Club. If you’re feeling bold, hit Taxx, one of Shanghai’s biggest nightclubs.

Solo Traveler Hack: Use Didi (China’s Uber) for easy transport, and download WeChat for payments—cash is rarely used here.

Shanghai is a solo traveler’s playground. From sky-high bars to hidden alleyways, every corner has a story. Pack your curiosity, and let the city surprise you.
