What Are Some Unique Coffee Shops in Shanghai?

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|

Shanghai is not only a hub of commerce and culture but also a city where café culture thrives. With its mix of historical charm and modern innovation, Shanghai offers a wide variety of coffee shops that cater to every kind of coffee lover. From artsy spaces to themed cafés and hidden gems, here’s a guide to some of the city’s most distinctive coffee spots.

1. Seesaw Coffee

Seesaw Coffee is a well-known name in Shanghai’s specialty coffee scene. It prides itself on offering locally sourced, high-quality coffee beans with a focus on innovative brewing techniques. The ambiance is chic yet relaxed, making it a popular spot for both work and leisure.

Highlights

  • Seasonal coffee blends featuring unique flavor profiles.
  • Workshops for coffee enthusiasts interested in learning brewing skills.
  • Modern interiors with a minimalist aesthetic.

Where to Find

Multiple locations, including Taikoo Hui Mall and Jing’an Kerry Center, ensure easy access for coffee lovers across the city.

2. Mellower Coffee

Famous for its creative coffee presentations, Mellower Coffee is a must-visit for anyone seeking a unique coffee-drinking experience. Its signature "Sweet Little Rain" coffee, where a cloud of cotton candy melts into your cup, is both a visual and flavorful delight.

Highlights

  • Instagram-worthy coffee creations.
  • A wide selection of beverages, including unique lattes and cold brews.
  • Cozy seating areas perfect for long chats or solo moments.

Where to Find

With several outlets across Shanghai, Mellower Coffee can be found in key districts such as Xuhui and Lujiazui.

3. % Arabica

This internationally acclaimed coffee chain has made its mark in Shanghai with its minimalist design and focus on quality. Known for its excellent espresso-based drinks, % Arabica is a favorite among coffee purists.

Highlights

  • High-end coffee machines and single-origin beans.
  • Stunning interiors featuring clean lines and natural light.
  • Beautifully presented coffee, perfect for photography.

Where to Find

The Shanghai location near the Bund offers breathtaking views of the city skyline, making it an ideal stop for tourists.

4. The Press by Inno Coffee

Housed in a former newspaper office, The Press combines history and modernity in its design and offerings. The café exudes old-world charm with a contemporary twist, making it a unique spot for coffee and conversation.

Highlights

  • Signature drinks inspired by classic newspaper themes.
  • Spacious interiors with vintage décor elements.
  • A menu that includes coffee cocktails and fusion snacks.

Where to Find

Located in the Jing’an District, it’s a great spot for exploring nearby attractions after your coffee break.

5. Café del Volcán

Tucked away in the charming French Concession, Café del Volcán is a haven for coffee aficionados. This cozy café focuses on sourcing high-quality beans and roasting them in-house to ensure freshness.

Highlights

  • A rotating selection of single-origin beans.
  • Intimate atmosphere with personalized service.
  • The option to purchase freshly roasted beans to take home.

Where to Find

Situated on Yongkang Road, it’s perfect for a relaxing coffee break after strolling through the tree-lined streets.

6. Sumerian

Sumerian is a staple in Shanghai’s coffee scene, offering expertly brewed coffee and a relaxed vibe. Known for its bagels as much as its coffee, this café is a favorite among locals and expats alike.

Highlights

  • A variety of bagel sandwiches and pastries.
  • In-house roasted coffee beans available for purchase.
  • A dog-friendly atmosphere, ideal for pet owners.

Where to Find

The café is located on North Shaanxi Road, a short walk from major attractions in Jing’an.

7. Wagas Coffee Corner

While Wagas is primarily known for its healthy food offerings, its coffee corner concept provides a cozy and accessible space for enjoying a quick cup of coffee. Their lattes and flat whites are particularly popular.

Highlights

  • Affordable yet high-quality coffee options.
  • A focus on sustainability, with eco-friendly packaging.
  • Comfortable seating areas perfect for casual meetups.

Where to Find

You’ll find Wagas outlets scattered throughout Shanghai, making it a convenient choice for travelers.

8. Rumors Coffee

Rumors Coffee is a hidden gem that combines vintage aesthetics with a passion for quality coffee. The intimate setting and curated music playlist create a relaxing environment for customers.

Highlights

  • Signature drinks like rose lattes and cinnamon cappuccinos.
  • Antique furniture and quirky decorations.
  • A small but carefully curated menu of snacks.

Where to Find

Located on Changle Road, it’s a perfect spot to unwind after exploring the nearby French Concession.

9. Tims Coffee House

Tims, a Shanghai-based café with Canadian roots, offers a blend of Western coffee culture and local flavors. Known for its affordability and wide menu selection, it’s a popular choice for students and professionals.

Highlights

  • Classic coffee options paired with comfort food like muffins and sandwiches.
  • Spacious seating, ideal for group gatherings.
  • A friendly atmosphere with attentive service.

Where to Find

Branches are spread throughout Shanghai, including high-traffic areas like People’s Square and Lujiazui.

10. Fisheye Café

Fisheye Café is a concept café that combines photography and coffee. The walls are adorned with stunning photo displays, and the café often hosts photography exhibitions and events.

Highlights

  • A creative menu inspired by photography themes.
  • Specialty drinks like charcoal lattes and matcha coffee.
  • Opportunities to meet and network with local artists.

Where to Find

Located in the artsy M50 Creative Park, it’s a must-visit for art and coffee lovers alike.

11. MOJO Café

MOJO Café stands out for its Scandinavian-inspired design and commitment to sustainability. The café’s minimalist décor and organic menu items attract eco-conscious customers.

Highlights

  • Specialty coffee brewed with precision and care.
  • A selection of vegan and gluten-free snacks.
  • A serene environment perfect for reading or studying.

Where to Find

Nestled in the Xintiandi area, it’s an excellent choice for a quiet escape from the bustling city.

12. Little Nap Coffee Stand

Inspired by Tokyo’s coffee stands, Little Nap is a compact yet stylish café that focuses on delivering exceptional coffee. Its small size and minimalist design create an intimate coffee experience.

Highlights

  • Quick and efficient service, ideal for grab-and-go.
  • High-quality espresso drinks with bold flavors.
  • A cult following among coffee enthusiasts.

Where to Find

Located in Huangpu District, it’s a great option for a quick caffeine fix while exploring the city.

13. Farine Café

Farine is a French-inspired bakery café that pairs artisanal bread and pastries with expertly brewed coffee. Its rustic charm and high-quality offerings make it a favorite among locals.

Highlights

  • A wide selection of fresh-baked goods.
  • Excellent cappuccinos and flat whites.
  • A cozy and inviting atmosphere.

Where to Find

Located in the French Concession, it’s an ideal stop for a leisurely brunch or afternoon coffee.

Shanghai’s coffee scene is as dynamic and diverse as the city itself. Whether you’re a fan of artisanal brews, creative presentations, or simply a cozy corner to sip your latte, these cafés offer something for everyone.
