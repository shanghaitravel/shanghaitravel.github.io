Shanghai Day Trips: Buddhist Temples & Monasteries

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shanghai, a bustling metropolis known for its futuristic skyline and vibrant nightlife, also harbors serene spiritual retreats just a short trip away. For travelers seeking a break from the urban chaos, a day trip to Shanghai’s Buddhist temples and monasteries offers a perfect blend of culture, history, and tranquility. Whether you’re a devout pilgrim or a curious explorer, these sacred sites provide a glimpse into China’s rich religious heritage.

The Spiritual Side of Shanghai

While Shanghai is often associated with modernity, its surrounding areas are home to some of the most revered Buddhist sites in China. These temples and monasteries are not just places of worship but also architectural marvels, often nestled in picturesque landscapes. A day trip to these sanctuaries can be a transformative experience, offering peace and reflection amid ancient traditions.

Why Visit Buddhist Temples Near Shanghai?

  1. Cultural Immersion: These sites are steeped in history, with some dating back over a thousand years.
  2. Architectural Beauty: Intricate carvings, grand halls, and serene gardens showcase traditional Chinese design.
  3. Mindfulness & Relaxation: The tranquil atmosphere provides a respite from city life.
  4. Photography Opportunities: From golden statues to misty mountain backdrops, these locations are visually stunning.

Top Buddhist Temples & Monasteries for a Day Trip

1. Jade Buddha Temple (Yufo Si)

Located right in Shanghai, the Jade Buddha Temple is a must-visit for those short on time. Built in 1882, it houses two exquisite jade Buddha statues brought from Myanmar. The temple’s vibrant incense-filled halls and peaceful courtyards make it a favorite among locals and tourists alike.

Highlights:
- The 1.9-meter-tall white jade Buddha seated in the main hall.
- The vegetarian restaurant serving delicious temple cuisine.
- The chanting ceremonies, which create a mesmerizing ambiance.

2. Longhua Temple

As one of Shanghai’s oldest temples, Longhua Temple dates back to the Song Dynasty. Its iconic pagoda and sprawling complex are especially enchanting during the spring when peach blossoms bloom.

Highlights:
- The seven-story pagoda, a symbol of longevity.
- The annual Longhua Temple Fair, featuring traditional performances.
- The serene Bell Tower, where visitors can ring the ancient bell for good luck.

3. Jing’an Temple

Nestled in the heart of downtown Shanghai, Jing’an Temple is a striking contrast to the surrounding skyscrapers. Its golden roofs and intricate murals make it a photogenic spot.

Highlights:
- The 8.8-ton pure silver Buddha statue.
- The underground meditation hall, a quiet escape from the city’s hustle.
- The temple’s location near Nanjing Road, perfect for combining spirituality with shopping.

4. Putuo Mountain (Mount Putuo)

A bit further afield but worth the journey, Putuo Mountain is one of China’s Four Sacred Buddhist Mountains. Accessible by ferry from Shanghai, this island is dotted with temples and offers breathtaking coastal views.

Highlights:
- Puji Temple, the largest and most active monastery on the island.
- The 33-meter-tall Guanyin Statue overlooking the sea.
- The scenic hiking trails connecting various temples.

5. Qibao Temple

For a quick half-day trip, Qibao Temple in Shanghai’s Minhang District is ideal. This smaller temple is part of the charming Qibao Ancient Town, where visitors can explore traditional alleyways and snack on local delicacies.

Highlights:
- The Thousand Buddha Hall, adorned with miniature Buddha statues.
- The nearby Qibao Old Street for souvenirs and street food.
- The temple’s intimate atmosphere, perfect for quiet reflection.

Tips for Visiting Buddhist Temples

  • Dress Modestly: Cover shoulders and knees as a sign of respect.
  • Follow Etiquette: Avoid loud conversations and turn off your phone.
  • Photography Rules: Some temples prohibit photos of Buddha statues—always check first.
  • Incense Offering: If you choose to burn incense, follow the locals’ lead on proper rituals.

Combining Temple Visits with Other Activities

To make the most of your day trip, consider pairing temple visits with nearby attractions:

  • After Longhua Temple: Explore the Shanghai Botanical Garden.
  • After Jing’an Temple: Stroll along Nanjing Road or visit the Shanghai Museum.
  • After Qibao Temple: Wander through Qibao’s ancient water town.

Whether you’re seeking spiritual solace or cultural enrichment, Shanghai’s Buddhist temples and monasteries offer a refreshing escape from the city’s fast-paced life. Each site tells a story, inviting visitors to pause, reflect, and connect with China’s timeless traditions.
