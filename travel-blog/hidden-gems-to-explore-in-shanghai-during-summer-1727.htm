Hidden Gems to Explore in Shanghai During Summer

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shanghai is a city that never fails to surprise, especially in the summer when the heat drives most tourists to crowded spots like the Bund or Nanjing Road. But if you’re looking for something off the beaten path, the city has plenty of hidden gems waiting to be discovered. From tranquil gardens to underground art spaces, here’s your guide to the best lesser-known spots in Shanghai this summer.

Escape the Heat: Cool and Quiet Spots

Fuxing Park’s Secret Garden

While Fuxing Park itself is no secret, few visitors venture into its quieter corners. Tucked away behind the main pathways is a secluded garden with traditional Chinese pavilions and lotus ponds. Summer mornings here are magical—locals practice tai chi, and the lotus flowers bloom in full glory. Bring a book, find a shady bench, and enjoy the serenity.

The Cool Underground: M50 Art District

If the sun is too intense, head to M50, an industrial art complex in Putuo District. This former textile mill now houses avant-garde galleries, indie studios, and quirky cafés. The thick brick walls keep the interiors cool, making it a perfect spot to admire contemporary Chinese art without breaking a sweat. Don’t miss the rooftop of some galleries for unexpected skyline views.

Hidden Culinary Delights

Breakfast at A Da Cong You Bing

Forget fancy brunch spots—Shanghai’s best-kept breakfast secret is this tiny street stall near the Old City. Their cong you bing (scallion pancakes) are crispy, flaky, and served fresh off the griddle. Arrive early (before 9 AM) to avoid the line, and pair it with a cold soy milk for the ultimate summer morning treat.

Ice Cream at Gelato Dal Cuore

Tucked away in a quiet corner of Jing’an, this artisanal gelato shop offers flavors you won’t find anywhere else—think drunken rice wine or Sichuan peppercorn chocolate. Their seasonal summer specials, like lychee rose or matcha mint, are worth the detour. Pro tip: Ask for their hidden "off-menu" flavors if you’re feeling adventurous.

Offbeat Summer Activities

Kayaking on Suzhou Creek

Most tourists stick to Huangpu River cruises, but for a more intimate water experience, try kayaking on Suzhou Creek. Several local operators offer guided tours that take you past historic warehouses and under charming bridges. The early evening slots are ideal—cooler temperatures and golden-hour lighting make for stunning photos.

Midnight Bookstore: Zhongshu Ge

Shanghai’s nightlife isn’t just about bars. Zhongshu Ge, a 24-hour bookstore near People’s Square, is a haven for night owls. Browse books under soft lighting, sip on iced oolong tea, and join the late-night reading sessions hosted by local writers. It’s a uniquely Shanghai summer night experience.

Secret Shopping Finds

Spinning Jenny in the French Concession

This tiny boutique is easy to miss, but it’s a treasure trove for vintage lovers. From 1920s-style silk dresses to retro sunglasses, every piece has a story. The owner, a former costume designer, can even tailor outfits on the spot. Perfect for finding a one-of-a-kind summer look.

Yuyuan Bazaar’s Hidden Alley

While Yuyuan Garden is packed with tourists, few explore the narrow alley behind the bazaar. Here, local artisans sell handmade paper fans, bamboo umbrellas, and other traditional summer essentials. Bargain politely, and you might walk away with a unique souvenir for half the price of the main stalls.

Nature Retreats Within the City

Changfeng Park’s Secret Bamboo Forest

Most visitors come for the aquarium, but Changfeng Park hides a lush bamboo grove near its western edge. The dense foliage creates a natural cooling effect, and the rustling leaves drown out the city noise. Pack a picnic and enjoy a shady afternoon here.

Qingpu’s Water Towns (Without the Crowds)

Skip the overcrowded Zhujiajiao and head to quieter water towns like Jinze or Fengjing. These lesser-known gems offer the same charm—ancient bridges, canal-side teahouses—but with fewer tourists. Rent a boat, drift along the waterways, and pretend you’ve stepped back in time.

Unusual Summer Nightspots

The Rooster’s Speakeasy

Hidden behind an unmarked door in Jing’an, this cocktail bar is a local favorite. Their summer specials include drinks infused with seasonal fruits like mulberry or yellow peach. The vibe is intimate, with jazz playing softly in the background—ideal for a low-key night out.

Xintiandi’s Rooftop Cinema

Few know about the open-air cinema atop one of Xintiandi’s buildings. On summer weekends, they screen classic films under the stars. Bring a blanket, order a cocktail, and enjoy a movie with the Shanghai skyline as your backdrop.

Shanghai’s summer doesn’t have to mean battling crowds or sweating through tourist traps. These hidden gems offer a cooler, quieter, and far more memorable way to experience the city. Whether you’re a first-time visitor or a long-time resident, there’s always something new to discover.
