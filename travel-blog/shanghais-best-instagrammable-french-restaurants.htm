Shanghai's Best Instagrammable French Restaurants

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shanghai is a city where East meets West, and nowhere is this fusion more deliciously evident than in its thriving French dining scene. From chic bistros to elegant fine-dining establishments, the city offers a plethora of Instagram-worthy spots that serve up both exquisite cuisine and stunning aesthetics. Whether you're a foodie, a traveler, or just someone who loves a good photo op, these French restaurants in Shanghai are must-visits.

1. Le Bistrot de Joël Robuchon

A Michelin-Starred Masterpiece

Nestled in the historic Bund area, Le Bistrot de Joël Robuchon is a dream for anyone who appreciates both gourmet food and luxury aesthetics. The restaurant’s sleek black-and-red interiors, accented with dramatic floral arrangements, make it a visual feast before you even take your first bite.

What to Order (and Photograph)

  • Caviar with King Crab & Cauliflower Cream – A dish as photogenic as it is decadent.
  • The Famous Robuchon Bread Basket – A golden, buttery tower that’s practically begging for a close-up.

Pro tip: Request a table by the window for a breathtaking view of the Huangpu River—perfect for golden-hour snaps.

2. Mr & Mrs Bund

Modern French with a Side of Glamour

Helmed by celebrity chef Paul Pairet, Mr & Mrs Bund redefines French dining with a playful, contemporary twist. The restaurant’s moody lighting, plush velvet seating, and panoramic Bund views create an effortlessly chic vibe.

Must-Try Dishes for the 'Gram

  • Lemon & Lemon Tart – A deconstructed dessert that’s as artistic as it is zesty.
  • Whole Roasted Chicken for Two – Served under a glass cloche, this dish is pure drama.

Don’t miss their terrace—ideal for sunset cocktails and skyline shots.

3. Maison Lameloise

French Elegance Meets Shanghai Skyline

Located in the soaring heights of the Shanghai Tower, Maison Lameloise brings a slice of Burgundy to the city. The restaurant’s refined décor—think marble tables, crystal glassware, and floor-to-ceiling windows—makes every corner Insta-ready.

Signature Snap-Worthy Plates

  • Foie Gras with Cherry Compote – A vibrant, colorful dish that pops on camera.
  • Pigeon with Black Truffle – Plated like a modern art piece.

For the ultimate flex, capture the view of the city lights from your table—it’s pure magic.

4. Polux by Paul Pairet

Casual French Charm in Xintiandi

If you’re craving a more laid-back yet photogenic French experience, Polux is the spot. Inspired by Parisian cafés, its cozy banquettes, checkered floors, and vintage mirrors create a timeless European vibe.

Photo-Friendly Favorites

  • Croque Monsieur – Oozing cheese and golden crust? Yes, please.
  • Mille-Feuille – Layers of flaky pastry and cream that demand a top-down shot.

The outdoor seating is perfect for people-watching and candid snaps.

5. Jean-Georges at Three on the Bund

Luxury Dining with a View

Jean-Georges needs no introduction—this iconic restaurant offers a sophisticated setting with its crisp white linens, fresh orchids, and stunning Bund vistas. It’s a favorite among influencers and food lovers alike.

Dishes That Steal the Spotlight

  • Egg Caviar – A silky, luxurious starter that’s pure elegance on a plate.
  • Chocolate Soufflé – Served with vanilla ice cream, it’s a dessert worth the wait (and the photos).

Pro tip: Book a lunch reservation for the best natural lighting for your food pics.

6. Blaz

Trendy French Bistro Vibes

Tucked away in the French Concession, Blaz is a hip spot with a moody, intimate atmosphere. Exposed brick, dim lighting, and an open kitchen make it feel like a hidden Parisian gem.

Instagram Highlights

  • Beef Tartare – Served with delicate herbs and crispy toast.
  • Crème Brûlée – That crackable sugar crust is pure ASMR (and great for video content).

The bar area, with its backlit bottles, is a great backdrop for cocktail shots.

7. Le Comptoir de Pierre Gagnaire

Art on a Plate

Located in the Capella Shanghai, this restaurant is a visual masterpiece. The décor blends French sophistication with subtle Chinese influences, while the dishes are edible works of art.

Most Photogenic Picks

  • Scallops with Yuzu Foam – A delicate, ethereal presentation.
  • Chocolate Sphere – A dessert that melts dramatically when hot sauce is poured over it (hello, viral potential).

The courtyard garden is another prime photo spot—lush greenery and soft lighting create a fairytale vibe.

Final Tips for Instagramming Your French Feast

  • Lighting is Key – Natural light works best for food photography.
  • Angles Matter – Top-down for flat lays, 45-degree for depth.
  • Don’t Forget the Details – Capture the textures, the table setting, and even the wine glasses.

Shanghai’s French restaurants aren’t just about the food—they’re about the experience, the ambiance, and yes, the Instagram moments. So grab your phone, book a table, and let the likes roll in.
