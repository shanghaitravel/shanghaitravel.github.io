Shanghai’s Spring Perfume & Scent Workshops

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Spring in Shanghai is a sensory delight—cherry blossoms bloom along the Bund, the air carries a hint of jasmine, and the city’s vibrant energy seems to awaken with the season. For travelers and locals alike, one of the most enchanting ways to experience Shanghai’s spring is through its perfume and scent workshops. These immersive experiences blend art, culture, and personal expression, making them a must-try for anyone visiting the city.

Why Shanghai’s Scent Workshops Are a Spring Must-Do

Shanghai has long been a hub for creativity and luxury, and its perfume workshops are no exception. These sessions offer more than just a chance to mix scents—they’re a deep dive into the world of fragrance, where history, botany, and personal storytelling collide.

The Rise of Bespoke Fragrances in Shanghai

In recent years, bespoke perfumery has gained traction in Shanghai, with workshops popping up in trendy districts like Xintiandi, Tianzifang, and the French Concession. These spaces cater to a growing demand for personalized luxury, allowing participants to craft a scent that’s uniquely theirs.

Spring, with its floral abundance, is the perfect time to explore these workshops. Many incorporate seasonal notes like osmanthus, peony, and fresh green tea, capturing the essence of Shanghai in a bottle.

Top Perfume & Scent Workshops to Try This Spring

1. Le Labo’s Shanghai Experience

Located in the heart of Jing’an District, Le Labo’s workshop is a favorite among fragrance enthusiasts. Here, you can create a custom scent under the guidance of master perfumers. Their spring collection often features bergamot, neroli, and delicate white florals, evoking the city’s blooming gardens.

What Makes It Special:

  • Hand-blended formulas using high-quality essential oils.
  • Eco-friendly packaging—a nod to Shanghai’s growing sustainability movement.
  • A chic, industrial-chic space perfect for Instagram-worthy moments.

2. The Scent Library (气味图书馆)

This homegrown brand has taken Shanghai by storm with its whimsical approach to fragrance. Their workshops are playful yet insightful, often themed around Shanghai’s nostalgic scents—think rain-soaked stone alleys, steamed dumplings, and old books.

Workshop Highlights:

  • "Shanghai Memories" scent-making session, where you can recreate the aroma of the city’s historic lanes.
  • Affordable pricing, making it accessible for younger travelers.
  • A quirky gift shop filled with unique fragrance souvenirs.

3. Floral Alchemy at The PuLi Hotel

For a luxe experience, The PuLi Hotel’s "Spring Blossom Perfume Workshop" is unmatched. Held in their serene spa, the workshop pairs fragrance creation with tea ceremonies and guided meditation.

Why It’s Worth It:

  • Exclusive access to rare floral extracts like chrysanthemum and lotus.
  • A tranquil escape from the city’s hustle.
  • Complimentary afternoon tea featuring local spring delicacies.

How to Choose the Right Workshop for You

With so many options, picking the perfect workshop can be overwhelming. Here’s a quick guide:

For Luxury Seekers

  • Le Labo or The PuLi Hotel for high-end, personalized service.

For Culture Enthusiasts

  • The Scent Library for a playful, locally inspired experience.

For Budget-Conscious Travelers

  • Smaller indie studios in Tianzifang, which offer intimate sessions at lower prices.

The Science & Art Behind Perfume Making

Attending a workshop isn’t just about mixing oils—it’s a lesson in olfactory storytelling. Here’s what you’ll typically learn:

Understanding Fragrance Notes

  • Top notes (citrus, herbs) – The first impression.
  • Heart notes (florals, spices) – The soul of the perfume.
  • Base notes (woods, musk) – The lingering trail.

The Role of Local Ingredients

Many workshops highlight Shanghai’s botanical heritage, using ingredients like:
- Osmanthus – A sweet, apricot-like flower native to East China.
- Longjing tea – A fresh, grassy note perfect for spring.
- Gardenia – A creamy white floral beloved in Chinese perfumery.

Beyond the Workshop: Fragrance-Themed Activities in Shanghai

If you fall in love with scent-making, extend your fragrant journey with these activities:

Visit a Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) Herbal Market

  • Explore Yongkang Road or Qibao Old Street for aromatic herbs used in perfumes and remedies.

Take a Flower Market Tour

  • Jing’an Flower Market bursts with spring blooms, ideal for DIY scent inspiration.

Book a Fragrance High Tea

  • Cafés like The Press by Inno Coffee offer floral-infused desserts paired with scent profiling.

Final Tips for Your Scent Workshop Adventure

  • Wear neutral-smelling clothing to avoid mixing your perfume with strong detergents.
  • Bring a small notebook to jot down your favorite scent combinations.
  • Ask about local collaborations—some workshops partner with Shanghai’s indie designers for limited-edition bottles.

Shanghai’s spring perfume workshops are more than a tourist activity—they’re a way to bottle the city’s essence and take a piece of its magic home. Whether you’re a solo traveler, a couple, or a group of friends, these sessions promise an unforgettable, sensory-rich experience.
