How to Get a Metro Card in Shanghai: A Comprehensive Guide

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shanghai, a sprawling metropolis and one of the most dynamic cities in China, is known for its impressive skyline, historical landmarks, and a seamless transportation system that connects all corners of the city. Among the various modes of transportation, the Shanghai Metro stands out as one of the most efficient, cost-effective, and user-friendly options for both locals and tourists.

The Shanghai Metro is the largest metro system in China and one of the largest in the world. It serves millions of passengers daily, offering a reliable way to navigate through the city. Whether you're visiting Shanghai for a short time or planning to stay longer, having a metro card can make your travels in the city more convenient.

This article will provide a step-by-step guide on how to obtain and use a metro card in Shanghai, the different types of cards available, and tips on how to maximize their benefits during your stay in the city.

1. Types of Metro Cards in Shanghai

Shanghai offers several types of metro cards for different needs, and understanding these options can help you choose the best card for your trip. The primary types of cards available are:

1.1. The Shanghai Public Transportation Card (SPTC)

The Shanghai Public Transportation Card (SPTC), also known as the Jiaotong Card (交通卡), is the most commonly used card by both locals and tourists. This card allows passengers to use not only the metro but also buses, ferries, and even some taxis. It is a convenient option for those who plan to use multiple modes of transportation during their stay in Shanghai.

The SPTC card is rechargeable, and the balance can be topped up at various locations throughout the city, including metro stations, convenience stores, and ticket vending machines. This card also offers a slight discount on fares compared to single journey tickets.

1.2. The Metro Single Journey Ticket

For tourists who do not need to use public transportation extensively, the Metro Single Journey Ticket is a one-time ticket that can be purchased for individual trips. This option does not require a card, and passengers simply buy a ticket for their desired destination. While this option is convenient for one-off journeys, it is not as cost-effective for those who plan to travel multiple times during the day or their stay.

The Single Journey Ticket is a paper-based ticket and is valid only for the journey you purchased it for. If you need to travel to different areas, you'll have to purchase separate tickets for each journey.

1.3. The Shanghai Metro Monthly Card

For long-term residents or frequent travelers, the Shanghai Metro Monthly Card is a great option. This card offers unlimited travel within the metro system for one month. It is especially beneficial for those staying in Shanghai for a longer period, as it provides unlimited access to the entire metro network for a fixed price.

The Monthly Card can be obtained at customer service centers in metro stations. The card is personalized with the holder's name, and a photo ID is typically required for issuance. The card also comes with a deposit, which can be refunded when the card is returned.

2. How to Get a Metro Card in Shanghai

Obtaining a metro card in Shanghai is a simple and straightforward process. There are several ways to get a metro card, depending on the type of card you prefer.

2.1. Buying a Shanghai Public Transportation Card

If you're looking to purchase the Shanghai Public Transportation Card (SPTC), follow these steps:

  1. Find a Metro Station: The easiest way to obtain a Shanghai Public Transportation Card is by visiting any metro station in the city. Metro stations typically have customer service centers where you can purchase the card.

  2. Fill Out a Form: At the customer service counter, you will need to fill out a simple form with your personal information. This form typically requires your name and passport number if you are an international tourist.

  3. Pay the Deposit: The card requires a deposit, usually around 20 RMB (Chinese Yuan), which will be refunded when you return the card. The initial deposit helps cover the cost of the card itself.

  4. Load Funds onto the Card: After paying the deposit, you can load the card with funds. You can choose how much you want to load onto the card, typically starting from a minimum of 50 RMB. This balance will be used for your metro rides, and the fare will be automatically deducted each time you travel.

  5. Activate Your Card: Once the card is issued and the funds are loaded, the card is ready for use. You can use it for metro, bus, and ferry rides, as well as for some taxis. The card is activated once you first use it.

2.2. Buying a Metro Single Journey Ticket

If you only plan on taking a few metro rides, you can purchase a Metro Single Journey Ticket at any metro station. Here's how:

  1. Locate the Ticket Machines: Ticket machines are available at all metro stations. These machines have an easy-to-use interface that allows you to select your destination and pay for your journey.

  2. Select Your Destination: Choose your desired destination on the ticket machine's screen. The machine will display the fare for your trip based on the distance traveled.

  3. Pay for Your Ticket: You can pay for the ticket using cash (Chinese Yuan) or a bank card. Once the payment is made, the machine will issue a paper ticket.

  4. Enter the Metro: Use the paper ticket to enter the metro system by scanning it at the turnstile.

2.3. Buying a Monthly Metro Card

To obtain a Shanghai Metro Monthly Card, you need to visit a customer service center at any major metro station. The process is as follows:

  1. Visit the Customer Service Center: Go to the customer service counter at a metro station, where staff members will assist you with the purchase of the monthly card.

  2. Provide Identification: You may be asked to provide identification, such as your passport (for international tourists), as the card is personalized.

  3. Pay for the Card: The cost of the card depends on the type of card and the number of days you plan to use it. For the monthly pass, you will pay a fixed fee for unlimited metro rides within a month.

  4. Receive Your Card: Once the payment is processed, you will receive your personalized metro card, which can be used immediately.

3. How to Recharge a Metro Card

If you're using the Shanghai Public Transportation Card (SPTC), you will need to recharge it after the balance is used up. There are several ways to top up your card:

3.1. At Metro Stations

You can recharge your card at any metro station by visiting a ticket vending machine or customer service counter. The machine accepts cash and card payments, and you can choose to load a specific amount onto your card.

3.2. At Convenience Stores

Many convenience stores in Shanghai, such as 7-Eleven or FamilyMart, offer the option to recharge your metro card. Simply hand the cashier your card and tell them how much you want to add to it. The process is quick and easy.

3.3. Online Top-Up

For those who prefer the convenience of online services, you can recharge your card through various online platforms, such as mobile apps. The Shanghai metro system also offers online services where you can check your balance and recharge your card via your smartphone.

4. Using Your Metro Card

Once you have your Shanghai Public Transportation Card, using it is simple. Here's how you can use it:

  1. Entering the Metro: To enter the metro system, simply tap your card on the card reader at the entrance. The fare will be automatically deducted from your balance.

  2. Exiting the Metro: When you reach your destination, tap your card again at the exit turnstile. The fare for your journey will be deducted, and the turnstile will open.

  3. Using the Card for Buses: The Shanghai metro card can also be used for bus rides. Just tap the card on the reader when you board the bus and tap again when you get off.

5. Refunding Your Metro Card

If you no longer need your metro card, you can return it and receive a refund. Here's how:

  1. Return the Card: Go to any customer service center at a metro station and hand over your card.

  2. Receive a Refund: You will receive a refund for the remaining balance on your card, minus the deposit. Keep in mind that there may be a small fee for returning the card.

Getting a metro card in Shanghai is a convenient way to navigate the city. Whether you're a tourist visiting for a few days or a resident planning long-term travel, having a metro card will make your transportation experience smoother and more cost-effective. By following the steps outlined in this guide, you’ll be ready to explore the vast and vibrant city of Shanghai with ease!
