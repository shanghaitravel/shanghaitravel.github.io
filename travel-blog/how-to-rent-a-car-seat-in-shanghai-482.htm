How to Rent a Car Seat in Shanghai

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shanghai is a bustling metropolis that blends ancient culture with modern innovation, making it a top destination for families traveling with young children. While navigating the city’s efficient public transport system is easy, many parents prefer the convenience of private cars or taxis—especially when traveling with toddlers or infants. However, car seat regulations and availability can be confusing for visitors. Here’s everything you need to know about renting a car seat in Shanghai, ensuring a safe and stress-free trip for your little ones.

Why Renting a Car Seat in Shanghai Matters

Car seat laws in China are not as strictly enforced as in Western countries, but safety should always come first. Shanghai’s chaotic traffic and unpredictable driving habits make securing your child in a proper car seat essential. While some taxis or ride-hailing services may not provide car seats, renting one ensures compliance with international safety standards and peace of mind.

Types of Car Seats Available for Rent

  1. Infant Car Seats (Rear-Facing)
    Designed for babies up to 2 years old or weighing less than 13 kg (29 lbs), these seats provide optimal support for fragile necks and spines.

  2. Convertible Car Seats
    These can switch from rear-facing to forward-facing, accommodating children from infancy up to around 4 years old (or 18 kg/40 lbs).

  3. Booster Seats
    Ideal for older toddlers (4+ years) who have outgrown convertible seats but still need elevation to safely use adult seat belts.

Where to Rent a Car Seat in Shanghai

1. Airport Rental Services

Major airports like Pudong International (PVG) and Hongqiao (SHA) often have rental kiosks offering car seats. Companies like Avis or Hertz may include them as add-ons when booking a car.

2. Ride-Hailing Apps with Car Seat Options

Apps like Didi Chuxing (China’s Uber equivalent) occasionally offer “Family” ride options with pre-installed car seats. However, availability is limited, so booking in advance is crucial.

3. Baby Gear Rental Companies

Specialized services like Shanghai Baby Rentals or Tiny Wanderers deliver car seats (and other baby equipment) to your hotel or Airbnb. They often provide ISO-certified seats and sanitization guarantees.

4. Hotels and Concierge Services

High-end hotels in Shanghai may arrange car seat rentals for guests. Always confirm in advance, as stock is limited.

How to Book a Car Seat in Advance

  1. Online Reservations
    Visit rental company websites or use apps like Didi to check availability. Some platforms allow you to pre-pay for added convenience.

  2. Verify Safety Standards
    Ensure the car seat meets EU (ECE R44/04) or U.S. (FMVSS 213) safety certifications. Avoid uncertified or second-hand seats.

  3. Delivery and Installation
    Many services include doorstep delivery and professional installation. If DIY is required, request an English manual or tutorial video.

Cost of Renting a Car Seat in Shanghai

Prices vary depending on the rental duration and seat type:
- Daily rates: ¥50–150 (~$7–20 USD)
- Weekly discounts: ~¥300–600 (~$40–85 USD)
- Refundable deposits: Often required (¥500–1,000 or ~$70–140 USD).

Pro tip: Compare prices across providers—some include free delivery or insurance.

Navigating Shanghai with a Car Seat

Taxis vs. Private Cars

Most Shanghai taxis lack built-in car seats, so renting your own is the safest option. For private cars (e.g., Didi Premium), confirm seat availability when booking.

Public Transport Alternatives

The Shanghai Metro is stroller-friendly, but for long distances or late-night travel, a car seat is advisable.

Cultural Tips for Traveling with Kids in Shanghai

  • Restaurants: High chairs are common in international chains but rare in local eateries.
  • Attractions: Disneyland Shanghai and Shanghai Ocean Aquarium are stroller-friendly.
  • Language Barrier: Carry a translated note like “请安装儿童安全座椅” (Please install the car seat) to show drivers.

Final Checklist Before You Rent

  1. Confirm the seat’s weight/height limits match your child’s size.
  2. Inspect for visible damage or missing parts upon delivery.
  3. Test the seat’s fit in the vehicle before hitting the road.

With the right preparation, renting a car seat in Shanghai can be seamless, letting you focus on exploring the city’s vibrant streets, futuristic skyline, and hidden alleyway gems—with your little one safe and snug.
