Unique Restaurants to Try Near Xujiahui

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Xujiahui, a bustling commercial and cultural hub in Shanghai, is not just known for its shopping malls, historical landmarks, and modern skyscrapers, but also for its diverse food scene. Whether you are in the mood for authentic Shanghainese cuisine, international flavors, or trendy fusion dishes, this area offers an abundance of choices. Here are some of the best restaurants near Xujiahui that are worth visiting.

1. Jesse Restaurant (老吉士)

For those looking to experience authentic Shanghainese flavors, Jesse Restaurant is a must-visit. This iconic establishment has been serving classic local dishes for years and remains a favorite among both locals and visitors.

What to Try:

  • Hong Shao Rou (Red-Braised Pork Belly) – A signature dish cooked to perfection with a sweet and savory glaze.
  • Yellow Croaker Soup with Rice Cakes – A rich, umami-packed soup that embodies traditional Shanghai flavors.
  • Drunken Chicken – Marinated in Shaoxing wine, this cold appetizer is both fragrant and flavorful.

Why Visit:

  • Authentic Shanghainese flavors made with high-quality ingredients.
  • Traditional ambiance that enhances the dining experience.
  • Popular among food enthusiasts and even celebrities.

2. Xin Rong Ji (新荣记)

If you are in the mood for high-end Chinese cuisine, Xin Rong Ji is the place to go. Specializing in Taizhou-style seafood, this Michelin-starred restaurant offers premium dishes that highlight the natural flavors of fresh ingredients.

What to Try:

  • Steamed Baby Yellow Croaker – A simple yet flavorful dish that emphasizes freshness.
  • Crispy Eel with Honey Sauce – A perfect balance of crunchiness and sweetness.
  • Braised Fish Maw – A luxurious delicacy that showcases the depth of Chinese culinary techniques.

Why Visit:

  • Michelin-starred restaurant with impeccable service.
  • Fresh seafood flown in daily.
  • Elegant atmosphere, perfect for special occasions.

3. Lost Heaven (迷·上海)

Blending the flavors of Yunnan cuisine with modern dining aesthetics, Lost Heaven is a fantastic choice for those looking for something different. The dim lighting, intricate decor, and exotic dishes transport diners to Southwest China.

What to Try:

  • Dai-Style Grilled Chicken – Marinated in aromatic spices and grilled to perfection.
  • Yunnan Wild Mushroom Soup – A fragrant broth made with handpicked mushrooms from Yunnan.
  • Spicy Mint Beef Salad – A refreshing dish that combines spice, herbs, and tender beef slices.

Why Visit:

  • Unique menu inspired by Yunnan’s ethnic minorities.
  • Stylish and romantic atmosphere.
  • Great for both casual dinners and special celebrations.

4. Maison Lameloise

For those craving fine French cuisine, Maison Lameloise is a standout choice near Xujiahui. This Shanghai outpost of the three-Michelin-starred restaurant from Burgundy offers an exquisite tasting menu with a modern take on French classics.

What to Try:

  • Foie Gras Terrine – A luxurious starter with a silky texture.
  • Lobster Ravioli – Rich and creamy with a perfectly balanced seafood essence.
  • Slow-Cooked Wagyu Beef – Melt-in-your-mouth tenderness with deep flavors.

Why Visit:

  • One of the best French fine dining experiences in Shanghai.
  • Impeccable presentation and flavor combinations.
  • Ideal for a sophisticated night out.

5. Di Shui Dong (滴水洞)

For a fiery, spice-packed meal, Di Shui Dong is the go-to place for Hunan cuisine. This casual eatery is known for its bold flavors and generous portions.

What to Try:

  • Spicy Ribs – Marinated with chilies and garlic, these ribs are the restaurant’s signature dish.
  • Mao Xue Wang (Spicy Blood Curd Hotpot) – A rich, numbing dish packed with ingredients like duck blood, tofu, and tripe.
  • Stir-Fried Green Beans with Minced Pork – Smoky and slightly spicy, this dish is a crowd favorite.

Why Visit:

  • Authentic Hunan flavors that deliver both heat and complexity.
  • Affordable and great for group dining.
  • Lively, casual atmosphere.

6. Bellagio (鹿港小镇)

Bellagio offers a mix of Taiwanese and Cantonese dishes with a modern twist. The restaurant is known for its vibrant ambiance and excellent desserts.

What to Try:

  • Three-Cup Chicken – A rich, aromatic dish cooked with soy sauce, basil, and rice wine.
  • Taiwanese Braised Beef Noodles – A comforting bowl of noodles in a deeply flavorful broth.
  • Mango Shaved Ice – A must-try dessert with fresh mango chunks and creamy condensed milk.

Why Visit:

  • Great spot for casual dining and late-night snacks.
  • Extensive menu catering to a variety of tastes.
  • Excellent desserts that are perfect for sharing.

7. Polux by Paul Pairet

If you’re looking for a relaxed yet stylish café with French-inspired dishes, Polux is a fantastic option. Created by Michelin-starred chef Paul Pairet, this bistro offers a laid-back European dining experience.

What to Try:

  • Croque Monsieur – A classic French sandwich with crispy bread and gooey cheese.
  • Steak Frites – Perfectly cooked steak with a side of golden fries.
  • Chocolate Tart – A rich, indulgent dessert for chocolate lovers.

Why Visit:

  • Chic, Parisian-style atmosphere.
  • High-quality ingredients and carefully crafted dishes.
  • Perfect for brunch or a casual dinner.

8. Xiao Yang Sheng Jian (小杨生煎)

No trip to Shanghai is complete without trying Sheng Jian Bao, and Xiao Yang is one of the most famous places to get them. These crispy-bottomed, juicy dumplings are an essential street food experience.

What to Try:

  • Classic Pork Sheng Jian Bao – The signature dumpling with a juicy pork filling and crispy base.
  • Shrimp and Pork Sheng Jian Bao – A seafood variation that adds a touch of sweetness.
  • Soy Milk and Youtiao – A classic breakfast combination in Shanghai.

Why Visit:

  • Affordable and quick bite option.
  • Iconic Shanghai street food experience.
  • Located conveniently near Xujiahui for an easy stop between sightseeing.

9. The Commune Social

For a unique tapas-style dining experience, The Commune Social offers a fantastic mix of European flavors with an Asian twist. The creative small plates make it ideal for those who enjoy trying a variety of dishes.

What to Try:

  • Iberico Ham Croquettes – Crispy and packed with flavor.
  • Slow-Cooked Lamb Shoulder – Tender and rich in taste.
  • Basque Cheesecake – A creamy, slightly burnt cheesecake that melts in your mouth.

Why Visit:

  • Perfect for sharing multiple small plates.
  • Modern, trendy atmosphere.
  • Excellent cocktails and dessert options.

Final Thoughts

Xujiahui is a paradise for food lovers, offering everything from Michelin-starred fine dining to authentic local street food. Whether you’re in the mood for spicy Hunan dishes, elegant French cuisine, or traditional Shanghainese flavors, you’ll find something that satisfies your cravings. Exploring the diverse food scene in Xujiahui is an adventure in itself, making it a must-visit destination for any culinary enthusiast in Shanghai.
