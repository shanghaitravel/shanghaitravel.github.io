Shanghai’s Best Jazz Bars for US Travelers

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shanghai, a city where East meets West, has long been a hub for jazz music. From the golden era of the 1920s and 1930s to today’s vibrant live music scene, the city offers a unique blend of nostalgia and modernity. For US travelers looking to experience Shanghai’s nightlife, jazz bars are a must-visit. Here’s a curated list of the best spots to enjoy smooth melodies, craft cocktails, and an unforgettable atmosphere.

The Historic Gems: Jazz Bars with a Story

The Peace Hotel Jazz Bar

No visit to Shanghai is complete without stepping into the legendary Peace Hotel Jazz Bar. Located in the iconic Fairmont Peace Hotel on the Bund, this venue is home to the Old Jazz Band, a group of musicians in their 70s and 80s who’ve been playing since the 1930s. The Art Deco interiors, vintage instruments, and classic jazz standards transport visitors back to Shanghai’s golden age.

Why US travelers will love it:
- A living piece of jazz history.
- Perfect for those who appreciate traditional jazz.
- Stunning views of the Bund.

JZ Club

Founded in 2004, JZ Club is one of Shanghai’s most respected jazz venues. With locations in the Former French Concession and the Bund, this club hosts both local talents and international acts. The intimate setting and high-quality acoustics make it a favorite among jazz enthusiasts.

Highlights:
- Weekly jam sessions.
- A mix of contemporary and classic jazz.
- Great cocktails and a cozy vibe.

Modern Vibes: Trendy Jazz Spots

Heyday Jazz Bar

Tucked away in the Former French Concession, Heyday Jazz Bar is a hidden gem with a speakeasy feel. The dim lighting, vintage decor, and live performances create an intimate setting perfect for a night out.

What makes it special:
- A curated selection of rare vinyl records.
- Talented local and expat musicians.
- Craft cocktails with a Shanghai twist.

Wooden Box

For a more experimental jazz experience, Wooden Box in Jing’an District is the place to be. This small but lively bar features avant-garde jazz, fusion, and even electronic-infused performances.

Why it stands out:
- Eclectic lineup of artists.
- Laid-back, artsy crowd.
- Affordable drinks and a welcoming vibe.

Jazz with a View: Rooftop and Riverside Venues

Bar Rouge

While not exclusively a jazz bar, Bar Rouge on the Bund often features jazz nights with breathtaking skyline views. The glamorous setting and live music make it a great spot for a sophisticated evening.

Perks for visitors:
- Stunning panoramic views.
- A mix of jazz, house, and lounge music.
- High-energy atmosphere.

The Nest

Another Bund favorite, The Nest combines Nordic-inspired design with live jazz performances. The sleek interiors and creative cocktails add to the allure.

Don’t miss:
- Weekend jazz brunches.
- Innovative drink pairings.
- A chic, cosmopolitan crowd.

Off the Beaten Path: Unique Jazz Experiences

Shake

Located in the trendy Ferguson Lane, Shake is a cocktail bar with a strong jazz influence. The live performances range from soulful vocals to instrumental trios, making it a versatile choice.

What to expect:
- Creative mixology.
- A mix of jazz, blues, and R&B.
- A stylish, relaxed setting.

Cotton Club

Inspired by the famous Harlem venue, Shanghai’s Cotton Club offers a lively mix of jazz, blues, and funk. The energetic performances and dance-friendly vibe set it apart.

Why go?
- High-energy live acts.
- A fun, unpretentious crowd.
- Great for dancing.

Tips for US Travelers Visiting Shanghai’s Jazz Bars

  • Timing is key: Most jazz bars start performances around 9 PM, with weekends being the busiest.
  • Dress code: While some spots are casual, upscale venues like Bar Rouge may require smart attire.
  • Cover charges: Some clubs have entry fees, especially for international acts. Check in advance.
  • Transportation: Use Didi (China’s Uber) or the metro to navigate the city safely at night.

Shanghai’s jazz scene is as diverse as the city itself. Whether you’re a die-hard jazz fan or just looking for a memorable night out, these bars promise an unforgettable experience. So grab a drink, settle in, and let the music take you on a journey through Shanghai’s soul.
