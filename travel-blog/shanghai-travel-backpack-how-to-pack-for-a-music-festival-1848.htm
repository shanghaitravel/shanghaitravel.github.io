Shanghai Travel Backpack: How to Pack for a Music Festival

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shanghai’s music festival scene is electric—think neon lights, pulsating beats, and crowds dancing under the skyline. Whether you’re heading to Strawberry Music Festival, Storm, or a hidden underground rave, packing the right gear can make or break your experience. Here’s your ultimate guide to stuffing that backpack like a pro.

The Essentials: What You Can’t Forget

1. Festival Tickets & ID

No ticket, no entry. Triple-check that you’ve got your e-ticket saved offline (cell service at festivals is notoriously spotty). Bring a physical ID, especially if you plan to enjoy Shanghai’s legendary cocktail tents.

2. Weather-Appropriate Clothing

Shanghai’s weather is unpredictable. For summer festivals:
- Lightweight, breathable fabrics (think cotton or moisture-wicking tech gear).
- A compact rain poncho—monsoon season can ambush you mid-mosh pit.
- Layers for evening chills when the breeze rolls in off the Huangpu River.

For cooler months:
- A stylish yet warm jacket (patches or band logos earn bonus points).
- Gloves and a beanie if it’s an open-air winter event like JZ Festival.

3. Comfortable Footwear

You’ll be on your feet for hours. Opt for:
- Sturdy sneakers (white ones will not stay white).
- Blister-resistant socks—trust us, your feet will thank you.

Tech & Gadgets: Stay Charged, Stay Connected

1. Portable Charger

A festival isn’t the place to hunt for outlets. Pack a 10,000mAh+ power bank to keep your phone alive for pics, maps, and emergency Uber rides.

2. Earplugs

Protect your hearing without missing the music. High-fidelity earplugs (like Loop Experience) cut decibels without muffling sound.

3. Mini LED Flashlight or Glow Sticks

Navigating a packed festival at night? A tiny flashlight or glow accessories double as safety tools and vibe enhancers.

Hydration & Snacks: Fuel for the Dance Floor

1. Collapsible Water Bottle

Many festivals (like Concrete & Grass) have refill stations. Hydration is key—Shanghai’s humidity is no joke.

2. Energy Bars or Local Snacks

Save cash and avoid long food lines. Throw in Shanghai-style spicy dried tofu (là dòufu) or xianbing (savory pancakes) for a local twist.

3. Electrolyte Packets

Dancing under the sun? Replenish with oral rehydration salts—sold at any Shanghai convenience store.

Style & Extras: Look Good, Feel Good

1. Festival Fashion with a Shanghainese Edge

  • Cheongsam-inspired crop tops for a retro-meets-rave look.
  • UV-protective sunglasses (the Bund’s glare is real).

2. Mini First-Aid Kit

Bandaids, painkillers, and tiger balm (for sore muscles or mosquito bites).

3. A Foldable Tote or Fanny Pack

For merch hauls or ditching layers when the crowd gets hot.

Pro Tips for Shanghai Festivals

  • Cash in Small Bills: Some vendors don’t take WeChat Pay.
  • Metro Card: Shanghai’s subway runs late during big events—avoid surge pricing.
  • Learn Basic Mandarin Phrases: “Nǎlǐ yǒu shǒuxījiān?” (“Where’s the restroom?”) is a lifesaver.

Now, zip up that backpack and dive into Shanghai’s music madness. The skyline’s glowing, the bass is dropping—see you in the crowd!
