Shanghai’s Best Spots for Dramatic Cloudy Skies

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shanghai, a city where futuristic skyscrapers meet historic alleyways, offers some of the most breathtaking urban landscapes—especially when the skies are moody and dramatic. Cloudy days in Shanghai aren’t just about gray monotony; they transform the city into a cinematic masterpiece, with towering structures piercing through rolling clouds and golden-hour light breaking through the gloom. Whether you’re a photographer, a traveler, or just someone who appreciates atmospheric beauty, these spots will give you the best views of Shanghai’s dramatic cloudy skies.

The Bund: Where History Meets the Heavens

Classic Skyline Views

No list of Shanghai’s best skyline spots would be complete without The Bund. On overcast days, the contrast between the historic European-style buildings and the modern Pudong skyline becomes even more striking. The clouds add depth to the scene, making the Oriental Pearl Tower and Shanghai Tower appear like they’re emerging from another world.

Best Time to Visit

Early morning or late afternoon, when the light filters through the clouds, creates a surreal glow over the Huangpu River. If you’re lucky, a foggy day will wrap the skyscrapers in mist, giving the illusion of a floating city.

Lujiazui: Skyscrapers in the Clouds

Shanghai Tower’s Observation Deck

At 632 meters, the Shanghai Tower’s observation deck offers an unparalleled vantage point. On cloudy days, you might find yourself literally above the clouds, with only the tips of other skyscrapers visible. It’s like standing in the middle of an urban mountain range.

Jin Mao Tower’s Hidden Perk

While many head to the Shanghai Tower or the Oriental Pearl, the Jin Mao Tower’s 88th-floor observation deck provides a slightly different perspective. The Art Deco-inspired design frames the skyline beautifully, and the clouds often create a moody, noir-esque atmosphere.

Longhua Temple: Ancient Beauty Under Stormy Skies

A Spiritual Backdrop

One of Shanghai’s oldest temples, Longhua Temple, takes on an almost mystical aura when dark clouds loom overhead. The golden pagoda against a brooding sky makes for an incredible contrast—perfect for photographers looking for a blend of culture and nature.

Cherry Blossom Season Bonus

If you visit in early spring, the temple’s cherry blossoms paired with dramatic clouds create an unforgettable scene. The soft pink petals against the dark sky is a sight straight out of a painting.

Zhujiajiao Water Town: A Dreamy Escape

Venice of Shanghai Under Moody Skies

Just an hour from downtown Shanghai, Zhujiajiao’s ancient canals and stone bridges look even more enchanting when reflected under a stormy sky. The water takes on a deeper hue, and the traditional boats (wupeng) gliding through the mist feel like something from an old Chinese poem.

Hidden Tea Houses

Duck into one of the riverside tea houses when the clouds threaten rain. Sipping tea while watching the sky shift over the water is one of the most serene experiences you can have near Shanghai.

Century Park: Urban Oasis with Big Skies

Expansive Views

Shanghai’s largest park, Century Park, offers wide-open spaces where you can fully appreciate the scale of the sky. On cloudy days, the park’s lakes mirror the dramatic heavens, doubling the visual impact.

The Metal Sun Sculpture

One of the park’s most iconic features, the giant metal sun sculpture, looks particularly striking when set against fast-moving clouds. It’s a favorite spot for photographers playing with light and shadow.

Sheshan: Shanghai’s Only Mountain

Basilica of Our Lady of Sheshan

Perched atop Sheshan Hill, this Catholic basilica offers panoramic views of Shanghai’s outskirts. On overcast days, the Gothic spires rise dramatically into the clouds, creating a hauntingly beautiful scene.

Hiking with a View

The hike up Sheshan is relatively easy, and the reward is a sweeping vista of forests and distant cityscapes—all under a canopy of ever-changing clouds.

Fuxing Park: Parisian Vibes in the Heart of Shanghai

European Elegance Meets Stormy Skies

Modeled after French gardens, Fuxing Park’s symmetrical pathways and fountains look even more romantic under moody lighting. The willow trees swaying against a dark sky make for a perfect, melancholic stroll.

Morning Tai Chi Sessions

If you arrive early, you’ll see locals practicing Tai Chi under the clouds, adding a layer of cultural charm to the already picturesque setting.

Tips for Capturing the Perfect Shot

Golden Hour Magic

Cloudy skies amplify the colors of sunrise and sunset. Arrive early or stay late to catch the best light breaking through the gloom.

Use Reflections

Shanghai’s many rivers, lakes, and glass skyscrapers provide endless reflection opportunities. Use them to double the drama in your photos.

Embrace the Fog

Don’t shy away from foggy days—they can turn ordinary scenes into moody masterpieces.

Shanghai’s ever-changing skies offer endless possibilities for those who know where to look. From futuristic skyscrapers to ancient temples, each location transforms under the drama of the clouds, revealing a different side of the city’s personality.
