Can You Take a Cruise on the Huangpu River in Shanghai?

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

A visit to Shanghai would not be complete without experiencing the iconic Huangpu River. Flowing through the heart of the city, this historic waterway divides Shanghai into two main districts: Puxi (west of the river) and Pudong (east of the river). Taking a cruise along the Huangpu River is a quintessential activity that offers breathtaking views of the city’s modern skyline, historic landmarks, and vibrant life along its banks. Here's everything you need to know about experiencing a Huangpu River cruise.

What Is a Huangpu River Cruise?

A Huangpu River cruise is a guided boat journey along the river that allows travelers to experience Shanghai’s most famous attractions from the water. These cruises are popular among both tourists and locals for their relaxing atmosphere and the unique perspective they offer of the city’s architectural diversity, from historic buildings in the Bund to the futuristic skyscrapers of Lujiazui.

Where to Board a Huangpu River Cruise?

The Main Cruise Terminals

There are several terminals along the Huangpu River, but the most popular ones are located near the Bund and Lujiazui.

  1. Shiliupu Wharf (十六铺码头)
    This is one of the oldest and busiest docks for Huangpu River cruises. Located near the southern end of the Bund, Shiliupu Wharf is the most convenient terminal for tourists staying in the central part of Shanghai.

  2. Qinghuangdao Road Wharf (秦皇岛路码头)
    Situated in the northern part of the Bund, this terminal is often less crowded and is an excellent choice for those seeking a quieter boarding experience.

  3. Binjiang Avenue Wharf (滨江大道码头)
    Located on the Pudong side, near the Lujiazui financial district, this terminal is ideal for visitors staying near iconic landmarks like the Oriental Pearl Tower or the Shanghai Tower.

Booking and Ticketing

Tickets for Huangpu River cruises can be purchased at the terminals or through online platforms like Trip.com or Meituan. Many hotels and travel agencies in Shanghai also offer ticketing services. Booking in advance is recommended, especially during weekends and public holidays.

Types of Huangpu River Cruises

Daytime Cruises

Daytime cruises are perfect for travelers who want to admire Shanghai’s landmarks under the bright sunlight. These cruises highlight the architectural contrasts between the colonial-style buildings of the Bund and the modern skyscrapers of Pudong.

  • Duration: Typically 30–60 minutes
  • Highlights: The Bund, Lujiazui skyline, and bustling river activities
  • Who It’s For: Families, history enthusiasts, and those with limited time

Evening Cruises

Evening cruises are the most popular option, offering a magical experience as the city’s skyline lights up with colorful illuminations. The vibrant reflections on the water create an unforgettable atmosphere.

  • Duration: Typically 1–2 hours
  • Highlights: The dazzling lights of the Bund and Pudong, illuminated landmarks like the Oriental Pearl Tower and the Shanghai Tower
  • Who It’s For: Couples, photographers, and those looking for a romantic experience

Dinner Cruises

For a more luxurious experience, consider a dinner cruise. These cruises include a gourmet meal along with the scenic views of the river.

  • Duration: 2–3 hours
  • Menu Options: Chinese cuisine, international dishes, and buffet-style meals
  • Who It’s For: Food lovers, business travelers, and special occasions

What to Expect on a Huangpu River Cruise

Iconic Landmarks Along the Route

  1. The Bund (Waitan)
    Known for its historic buildings and colonial-era architecture, the Bund showcases Shanghai’s rich history as a trading port. From the cruise, you can see famous landmarks such as the Customs House and the Peace Hotel.

  2. Lujiazui Skyline
    On the Pudong side, you’ll witness the futuristic skyline featuring the Oriental Pearl Tower, Jin Mao Tower, and the Shanghai Tower, which is the tallest building in China.

  3. Yangpu and Nanpu Bridges
    These two massive bridges are engineering marvels and serve as key transportation links across the Huangpu River.

  4. Shanghai Tower Light Show
    During evening cruises, the Shanghai Tower’s colorful light show is a highlight, with dynamic displays that captivate visitors.

Practical Tips for Taking a Huangpu River Cruise

Best Time to Go

  • Season: Spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) are the best seasons to enjoy a cruise, thanks to the pleasant weather.
  • Time of Day: Evening cruises offer the best views of Shanghai’s illuminated skyline, while daytime cruises are ideal for capturing the city’s architectural details.

Ticket Prices

  • Standard Cruises: Tickets typically range from ¥60 to ¥150, depending on the duration and type of seating.
  • Dinner Cruises: Prices can go up to ¥300 or more per person, depending on the menu and level of luxury.

What to Bring

  • Comfortable clothing and shoes
  • A camera for capturing the stunning views
  • A light jacket, especially for evening cruises in cooler months

Accessibility and Language Options

Most Huangpu River cruises are tourist-friendly and offer English signage, brochures, and audio guides. However, it’s a good idea to check the language options when booking, especially for guided cruises or dinner services.

Why Take a Huangpu River Cruise?

  1. Unique Perspective: A cruise provides a unique vantage point of Shanghai’s iconic landmarks, blending history and modernity in one journey.
  2. Relaxing Atmosphere: The serene experience of gliding along the river offers a peaceful escape from the city’s hustle and bustle.
  3. Memorable Photos: Whether it’s the golden hour during sunset or the vibrant evening lights, a cruise is a photographer’s dream.
  4. Cultural Insight: Many cruises provide narrated tours, giving passengers insights into Shanghai’s history, architecture, and culture.

Taking a Huangpu River cruise is an unforgettable way to experience the beauty and vibrancy of Shanghai. Whether you’re a first-time visitor or a frequent traveler to the city, this activity offers a perfect blend of relaxation, sightseeing, and cultural immersion. From budget-friendly options to luxurious dinner cruises, there’s something for everyone to enjoy on the Huangpu River.
