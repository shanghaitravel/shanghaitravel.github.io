Shanghai Itinerary: 3 Days of History & High-Tech

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

The very name Shanghai conjures images of a dizzying future—skyscrapers piercing the clouds, neon rivers flowing through canyon-like streets, and a pace of life that feels perpetually fast-forward. Yet, beneath that hyper-modern veneer pulses the heart of old China, of treaty ports and Art Deco grandeur, of hidden alleyways and steaming baskets of xiaolongbao. This three-day itinerary is designed to tear down that false dichotomy. Here, history and high-tech aren’t separate destinations; they are intertwined realities, each giving context and wonder to the other. Prepare to walk where dynasties fell and republics rose, and then ascend to where the 22nd century is being drafted.

Day 1: The Bund, Colonial Echoes & River of Light

Begin your journey at the geographical and symbolic core of modern Shanghai: The Bund. This famous waterfront promenade is your first lesson in the city’s layered identity. Stand facing west, and you are presented with a textbook of early 20th-century architecture—Neoclassical, Beaux-Arts, and Art Deco edifices that once housed global banks and trading houses. This is the "museum of international buildings," a stone-and-mortar record of Shanghai’s colonial past.

Morning: A Stroll Through Financial History

Start at the Rockbund Art Museum area, a beautifully restored cluster that sets the tone. Walk south, letting the grand facades of the Peace Hotel, the Customs House, and the HSBC Building (now the Shanghai Pudong Development Bank headquarters) tell their stories. Peek into the lobbies; many are open to the public and reveal opulent mosaics, vaulted ceilings, and a palpable sense of history. This stretch was once nicknamed "the Wall Street of the East," and its power still resonates.

Afternoon: Crossing Centuries in a Tunnel

For lunch, escape the main drag into the side streets. Yuanmingyuan Road offers charming cafes in renovated shikumen (stone-gate) houses. Afterward, make a quintessentially Shanghai crossing: descend into the Bund Sightseeing Tunnel. This brief, psychedelic subway ride under the Huangpu River is a gloriously kitschy piece of 1990s "futurism," a perfect palate cleanser before the shock of the new. You emerge not just in Pudong, but in a different epoch.

Evening: Pudong's Dazzling Symphony

Your afternoon and evening belong to Pudong. Ascend one of the iconic tri-towers. While the Oriental Pearl TV Tower is the most recognizable, the Shanghai World Financial Center (the "bottle opener") or the Shanghai Tower (China’s tallest building) offer more staggering views. From the Shanghai Tower’s observation deck, you’ll see the Bund’s historical curve dwarfed by the forest of skyscrapers you’re standing among—a breathtaking perspective on the city’s transformation.

As dusk falls, witness the technological marvel of the Pudong skyline light show. It’s not a single show, but a coordinated digital symphony across dozens of intelligent facades. For dinner, explore the IFC Mall or the Super Brand Mall, not just for food, but to experience the consumer-tech integration, from robotic waiters to facial recognition payments. Take the Huangpu River cruise at night. Seeing the Bund’s golden-hour glow from the water, with Pudong’s digital canvas as a backdrop, is the definitive Shanghai experience.

Day 2: Old City Cycles & The Museum of Tomorrow

Today is a day of deliberate contrasts, moving from the winding lanes of ancient Shanghai to the sleek lines of its most ambitious cultural institutions.

Morning: Lost in the Lanes of Old Shanghai

Dive into the Old City, centered around the Yu Garden (Yuyuan). Arrive early to beat the crowds to this 16th-century classical Chinese garden, an oasis of serene pavilions, koi ponds, and dragon-walled corridors. Then, get gloriously lost in the surrounding Yuyuan Bazaar. Though now tourist-friendly, its zigzag bridges and traditional architecture evoke the past. This is the place for souvenirs, but also for street-food staples: try the famed Nanxiang Steamed Bun Restaurant for xiaolongbao. Just a few blocks away, the quiet, residential lanes offer a glimpse of unvarnished local life, a stark contrast to the glass towers visible just over the rooftops.

Afternoon: A Portal to the Future

After lunch, take the metro (an attraction in itself for its scale and efficiency) to Pudong’s cultural district. Your destination is the Shanghai Astronomy Museum, located in the distant Lingang area—a journey worth every minute. Designed by Ennead Architects, the building itself is a mind-bending, high-tech artifact with no straight lines or right angles, embodying the principles of orbital mechanics. Its exhibits on cosmology and space exploration are world-class, featuring real moon rocks and immersive simulations. It’s a profound reminder that Shanghai’s gaze isn’t just on the next financial quarter, but on the cosmos.

Evening: The Smart Streets of Xintiandi

Return to Puxi for an evening at Xintiandi. This is history meticulously repackaged with high-tech flair. The shikumen exteriors have been preserved, but the interiors now house chic boutiques, award-winning restaurants, and cocktail bars. It’s a perfect case study in urban regeneration. Here, you can enjoy a modern twist on Shanghainese cuisine, then wander the illuminated brick lanes, where QR codes on plaques tell the story of the Communist Party’s founding (the site is next to the Site of the 1st CPC National Congress) while you sip a craft gin and tonic.

Day 3: Art, AI, and the Avant-Garde

Shanghai’s creative soul is where its historical resilience and technological ambition fuse most explosively. Day three explores this vibrant intersection.

Morning: The Power Station of Art

Start at the Power Station of Art on the West Bund. Housed in a former power plant, this is a monument to industrial history reborn as Shanghai’s premier public contemporary art museum. The vast turbine hall and massive crane structures are as much a part of the exhibition as the art itself. The shows here are consistently cutting-edge, often featuring Chinese and international artists grappling with themes of urbanization, digital life, and globalism—the very forces that shaped Shanghai.

Afternoon: West Bund & Digital Dialogues

Stroll along the West Bund, a regenerated waterfront area akin to London’s South Bank. You’ll pass the Long Museum, the Yuz Museum, and striking modern architecture. This is the city’s burgeoning gallery district. For a direct hit of tech-meets-art, seek out exhibitions or installations that utilize AI, virtual reality, or generative algorithms. Shanghai’s art scene is particularly engaged with these tools, creating dialogues that are uniquely of this city and this moment.

Evening: Jing'an's Neon Pulse & Silent Disco

Your final evening takes you to Jing'an. Pay a visit to the Jing'an Temple, a stunning, golden-hued Buddhist temple dating back over 780 years, now sitting in surreal silence amidst a roaring vortex of traffic and luxury malls. It’s the ultimate photo of old and new in a single frame. For your farewell dinner, explore the culinary hotbeds of Fumin Road or Julu Road, where innovative fusion restaurants thrive.

End your Shanghai journey with a cocktail at one of the city’s famed rooftop bars, perhaps Flair at the Ritz-Carlton or Bar Rouge. Look out over the endless city lights—the historical Bund on one side, the futuristic Pudong on the other, and the buzzing, ever-evolving metropolis stretching to the horizon in every direction. You’ve not just visited a city; you’ve time-traveled along its ongoing narrative, a story where every chapter, from the dynastic to the digital, is still being written, and every street corner holds a fragment of both the past and the future.
