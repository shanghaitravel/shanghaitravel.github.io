The Best Spots to See Shanghai’s Skyline in Spring

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shanghai’s skyline is a mesmerizing blend of historic charm and futuristic ambition. In spring, when the city bursts into life with cherry blossoms, mild temperatures, and clear skies, the views become even more spectacular. Whether you’re a photographer, a traveler, or a local looking to rediscover your city, these spots offer the best vantage points to soak in Shanghai’s skyline during this vibrant season.

The Bund: Classic Elegance Meets Modern Grandeur

No visit to Shanghai is complete without a stroll along The Bund. This iconic waterfront promenade offers a front-row seat to the dazzling skyline of Pudong, where skyscrapers like the Shanghai Tower and the Oriental Pearl Tower dominate the horizon.

Best Time to Visit

Spring evenings are magical here. Arrive just before sunset to watch the sky turn golden, then stay as the city lights up. The reflection of the neon-lit buildings on the Huangpu River creates a scene straight out of a sci-fi movie.

Pro Tip

For a less crowded experience, head to the Rockbund Art Museum area or the Waibaidu Bridge for unique angles.

Lujiazui Skyline: Heart of Pudong

If you want to be inside the skyline rather than just admiring it from afar, Lujiazui is the place to be. This financial district is home to some of the tallest buildings in the world, and spring adds a refreshing contrast of greenery against the steel and glass.

Must-Visit Observation Decks

  1. Shanghai Tower (118th Floor Observation Deck)
    At 632 meters, this is the highest observation deck in the city. On clear spring days, you can see for miles, with the Yellow River winding through the landscape.

  2. Jin Mao Tower’s Cloud 9 Bar
    Skip the observation deck fee and enjoy a cocktail at this stylish bar on the 87th floor. The floor-to-ceiling windows offer an unbeatable view, especially at twilight.

Hidden Gem

The Binjiang Green Park in Pudong offers a grassy, relaxed setting to admire the skyline without the hustle of the financial district.

Long Museum (West Bund): Art and Skyline Combined

For a cultural twist, visit the Long Museum on the West Bund. This area has become a hotspot for contemporary art, and the museum’s rooftop provides a stunning, lesser-known view of the skyline across the river.

Why Spring?

The West Bund is lined with cherry trees that bloom in March and April, framing the skyline with delicate pink flowers. It’s a favorite spot for Instagrammers and artists alike.

Century Park: A Green Escape with Skyline Views

Shanghai’s largest park, Century Park, is a springtime paradise. While it’s not as high as the skyscrapers, the park’s Lakeside Plaza offers a serene view of Pudong’s skyline in the distance, especially at sunrise.

Activities to Pair with Your Visit

  • Rent a paddleboat on the lake.
  • Have a picnic under the cherry blossoms.
  • Visit the Shanghai Science and Technology Museum nearby.

Hyatt on the Bund’s Vue Bar: Rooftop Luxury

For a sophisticated skyline experience, head to Vue Bar at the Hyatt on the Bund. This rooftop spot offers 360-degree views, including the Huangpu River and both Pudong and Puxi skylines.

Spring Specials

The bar often features seasonal cocktails in spring, like the Sakura Spritz, made with cherry blossom-infused gin.

Nanpu Bridge: A Unique Perspective

Most visitors stick to ground-level or high-rise views, but Nanpu Bridge offers something different. The spiral ramp leading up to the bridge creates a photogenic loop, and the elevated position provides a sweeping view of the city.

Best for Photography

Visit at blue hour (just after sunset) when the city lights are on but the sky still has a deep blue hue.

Fuxing Park: A Slice of Old Shanghai

For a skyline view with a historic backdrop, Fuxing Park in the French Concession is a charming choice. From certain angles, you can catch glimpses of modern skyscrapers peeking over the treetops and traditional shikumen buildings.

Spring Highlights

The park’s rose garden blooms in late April, adding a romantic touch to your skyline photos.

Tips for Capturing the Perfect Skyline Shot in Spring

  1. Timing is Everything

    • Sunrise (5:30-6:30 AM) offers soft light and fewer crowds.
    • Sunset (6:00-7:00 PM) brings golden hour magic.

  2. Use a Polarizing Filter
    This helps reduce glare from the glass skyscrapers and enhances the colors of the sky.

  3. Drones? Think Twice
    Drone regulations in Shanghai are strict. Always check local laws before flying.

  4. Pack Light but Smart
    A lightweight tripod and a wide-angle lens are ideal for skyline photography.

Shanghai’s skyline is ever-evolving, and spring breathes new life into its already breathtaking scenery. Whether you prefer the classic allure of The Bund or the modern heights of Lujiazui, each spot offers a unique way to experience the city’s grandeur. So grab your camera, enjoy the mild spring air, and let Shanghai’s skyline leave you in awe.
