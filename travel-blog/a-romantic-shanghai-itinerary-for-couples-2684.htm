A Romantic Shanghai Itinerary for Couples

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shanghai, the "Paris of the East," is a city where modernity meets tradition, creating the perfect backdrop for a romantic getaway. Whether you're strolling hand-in-hand along the Bund, savoring dim sum in a hidden alley, or watching the sunset from a rooftop bar, Shanghai offers endless opportunities for couples to create unforgettable memories. Here’s a curated itinerary to help you and your loved one experience the magic of this vibrant city.

Day 1: Classic Romance in the Heart of Shanghai

Morning: A Stroll Along the Bund

Start your day with a leisurely walk along The Bund, Shanghai’s iconic waterfront promenade. The mix of colonial-era architecture and futuristic skyscrapers across the Huangpu River makes for a breathtaking view. Stop by %Arabica for artisanal coffee and snap some couple photos against the skyline.

Afternoon: Lunch and Art in Tianzifang

Head to Tianzifang, a labyrinth of narrow lanes filled with boutique shops, galleries, and cozy cafés. Enjoy a romantic lunch at Kommune, a bohemian-style eatery with a charming courtyard. Afterward, explore the area’s quirky art installations and pick up unique souvenirs.

Evening: Rooftop Drinks and Fine Dining

As the sun sets, make your way to Bar Rouge or Flair Rooftop for cocktails with a view. For dinner, book a table at Mr & Mrs Bund, a Michelin-starred restaurant offering modern French cuisine. End the night with a moonlit walk along the Lujiazui Ring Bridge, where the city’s neon lights create a dreamy atmosphere.

Day 2: Cultural Charm and Hidden Gems

Morning: Breakfast in the French Concession

The French Concession is one of Shanghai’s most romantic neighborhoods. Start your day with brunch at Baker & Spice or Egg, known for their cozy vibes and delicious avocado toast. Afterward, wander through the tree-lined streets and admire the historic villas.

Afternoon: A Quiet Escape to Zhujiajiao

Take a short trip to Zhujiajiao Water Town, often called "Shanghai’s Venice." Glide through ancient canals on a gondola, visit quaint tea houses, and explore centuries-old bridges. It’s a peaceful retreat from the city’s hustle and bustle.

Evening: Jazz and Intimate Dining

Return to Shanghai and unwind at the Peace Hotel Jazz Bar, where legendary musicians play nostalgic tunes. For dinner, try Fu 1039, a hidden gem serving Shanghainese cuisine in a restored mansion. The intimate setting is perfect for a romantic evening.

Day 3: Modern Adventures and Relaxation

Morning: Sunrise at Century Park

For early risers, Century Park offers serene lakes and lush gardens—ideal for a quiet morning picnic. Rent a tandem bike and explore the park’s scenic trails together.

Afternoon: Shopping and Sweet Treats

Indulge in some retail therapy at Xintiandi, a stylish district blending old and new architecture. Stop by Lady M for their famous mille crêpes cake or Chikalicious for an elegant dessert experience.

Evening: A Magical Night at Disneyland

If you’re up for some fun, Shanghai Disneyland is a whimsical way to end your trip. Watch the fireworks over the Enchanted Storybook Castle, and let your inner child shine.

Romantic Extras: Unique Experiences for Couples

Couple’s Spa Day

Pamper yourselves at Auriga Spa in the Capella Shanghai, where traditional Chinese therapies meet luxury.

Huangpu River Cruise

Book a private dinner cruise on the Huangpu River for a mesmerizing view of Shanghai’s skyline.

Lock Your Love at Love Lane

Visit Tianzifang’s Love Lane, where couples attach padlocks to the walls as a symbol of their commitment.

Shanghai’s blend of romance, culture, and excitement makes it an ideal destination for couples. Whether you’re savoring gourmet meals, exploring hidden alleys, or simply enjoying each other’s company against a glittering skyline, this city will leave you both enchanted.
