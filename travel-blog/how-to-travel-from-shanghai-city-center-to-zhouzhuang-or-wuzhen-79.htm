How to Travel from Shanghai City Center to Zhouzhuang or Wuzhen

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Zhouzhuang and Wuzhen, two of China's most famous ancient water towns, are popular destinations for those seeking a serene escape from the bustling metropolis of Shanghai. Known for their picturesque canals, stone bridges, and traditional architecture, both towns provide a glimpse into China's historical and cultural heritage. If you're planning a visit, here’s a comprehensive guide to traveling from Shanghai city center to Zhouzhuang or Wuzhen.

Getting to Zhouzhuang

By Bus

One of the most convenient and cost-effective ways to reach Zhouzhuang is by bus.

  1. From Shanghai Long-Distance Bus Station:

    • Take a direct bus to Zhouzhuang from the Shanghai General Bus Station near Shanghai Railway Station.
    • Buses typically operate from early morning to late afternoon, with departures every 30 to 60 minutes.
    • The journey takes about 1.5 hours, depending on traffic.

  2. From Shanghai Hongqiao Transportation Hub:

    • Some buses also depart from Hongqiao, which may be a more convenient option if you’re staying near Hongqiao International Airport or the train station.

  3. Tips for Bus Travel:

    • Purchase tickets in advance, especially during weekends or holidays, as Zhouzhuang is a popular destination.
    • Bring some snacks and water for the ride.

By Private Car or Taxi

Hiring a private car or taking a taxi is a faster and more flexible option, ideal for families or small groups.

  1. Taxi Ride:

    • A taxi ride from Shanghai city center to Zhouzhuang usually takes around 1.5 hours.
    • Fares range from 400 to 600 RMB, depending on traffic and exact pickup and drop-off points.

  2. Private Car Services:

    • Platforms like Didi Chuxing offer private car services that can be booked in advance.
    • This option provides the added benefit of door-to-door service and flexibility with departure times.

  3. Driving Yourself:

    • If you’re comfortable driving in China, renting a car gives you the freedom to explore Zhouzhuang and its surroundings at your own pace.
    • Zhouzhuang has well-maintained parking areas near the entrance to the scenic area.

By Train

Although Zhouzhuang doesn’t have its own train station, you can take a train to Kunshan South Railway Station and then transfer to a local bus or taxi.

  1. High-Speed Train:

    • Take a high-speed train from Shanghai Hongqiao Railway Station to Kunshan South.
    • The train ride takes about 20 minutes.

  2. Connecting to Zhouzhuang:

    • From Kunshan South, local buses or taxis can take you to Zhouzhuang in approximately 30 to 40 minutes.

  3. Advantages of This Route:

    • High-speed trains are fast, comfortable, and punctual, making this a reliable option.

Getting to Wuzhen

By Bus

Direct buses to Wuzhen are available from several locations in Shanghai, making this an easy option for travelers.

  1. From Shanghai South Long-Distance Bus Station:

    • Buses to Wuzhen operate regularly, with a travel time of about 2 to 2.5 hours.
    • Tickets can be purchased at the station or online through travel apps like Ctrip.

  2. From Shanghai Hongqiao Transportation Hub:

    • Similar to Zhouzhuang, some buses to Wuzhen also depart from Hongqiao.

  3. Tips for Bus Travel:

    • Check the bus schedules in advance, as the last return bus to Shanghai may leave earlier than expected.
    • Consider traveling on a weekday to avoid large crowds.

By Private Car or Taxi

For a more comfortable and direct trip, consider hiring a car or taking a taxi.

  1. Taxi Ride:

    • A taxi ride from Shanghai city center to Wuzhen typically takes around 2 hours.
    • Expect to pay between 600 and 800 RMB for a one-way trip.

  2. Private Car Services:

    • Private car services can be booked for a day trip or overnight stay.
    • Many drivers offer packages that include waiting times and return trips.

  3. Driving Yourself:

    • Driving to Wuzhen is straightforward, with clear highway signs leading to the town.
    • Ample parking is available near the entrance to the scenic areas.

By Train

Similar to Zhouzhuang, Wuzhen doesn’t have a train station, but you can take a train to Tongxiang Railway Station and then transfer to local transportation.

  1. High-Speed Train:

    • High-speed trains from Shanghai Hongqiao Railway Station to Tongxiang take about 30 minutes.

  2. Connecting to Wuzhen:

    • From Tongxiang, take a local bus or taxi to Wuzhen, which is about 20 kilometers away.
    • A taxi ride from Tongxiang to Wuzhen takes about 30 minutes and costs around 50 RMB.

  3. Advantages of This Route:

    • Combining the speed of trains with the short taxi ride makes this a time-efficient option.

Choosing Between Zhouzhuang and Wuzhen

While both Zhouzhuang and Wuzhen offer stunning water town experiences, each has its unique charm:

  • Zhouzhuang:

    • Often referred to as the "Venice of the East," Zhouzhuang is known for its well-preserved ancient bridges and canals.
    • It has a quieter, more traditional atmosphere, making it ideal for those seeking a peaceful getaway.

  • Wuzhen:

    • Wuzhen is divided into two main sections, the East Scenic Zone and the West Scenic Zone.
    • It offers a mix of traditional charm and modern amenities, including boutique hotels and cultural performances.
    • The town is especially magical at night, with beautifully lit canals and lively night markets.

Tips for Visiting Both Towns

  1. Timing Your Visit:

    • Weekdays are less crowded than weekends.
    • Spring and autumn offer the most pleasant weather, while summer can be hot and winter can be chilly.

  2. Exploring on Foot:

    • Both towns are best explored on foot, so wear comfortable shoes.

  3. Dining Options:

    • Try local specialties like braised pork in soy sauce (dongpo rou) in Wuzhen and freshwater fish dishes in Zhouzhuang.

  4. Overnight Stays:

    • Consider staying overnight in Wuzhen to experience its enchanting evening atmosphere.
    • Zhouzhuang also offers charming guesthouses for an immersive cultural experience.

  5. Cultural Etiquette:

    • Respect the local customs and traditions while visiting temples and historical sites.

  6. Photography Tips:

    • Early mornings and late afternoons offer the best lighting for photos.
    • Don’t forget to capture the iconic stone bridges and reflections in the water.

Both Zhouzhuang and Wuzhen are delightful destinations that offer a perfect blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you choose one or visit both, the journey from Shanghai to these picturesque water towns is well worth the effort.
