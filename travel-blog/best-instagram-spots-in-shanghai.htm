Best Instagram Spots in Shanghai

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shanghai is a dazzling metropolis where futuristic skyscrapers meet historic alleyways, creating a visual feast for photographers and Instagram enthusiasts. Whether you're a traveler, expat, or local influencer, the city offers endless opportunities to snap jaw-dropping shots. From neon-lit streets to tranquil gardens, here’s your ultimate guide to the best Instagram spots in Shanghai.

1. The Bund: Iconic Skyline Views

Classic Skyline Shot

No visit to Shanghai is complete without a photo at The Bund. This waterfront promenade offers the most iconic view of Pudong’s futuristic skyline, especially at night when the Oriental Pearl Tower and Shanghai Tower glow against the sky.

Golden Hour Magic

For a dreamy effect, visit during golden hour (just before sunset). The soft light reflects off the Huangpu River, creating a mesmerizing backdrop. Pro tip: Head to the rooftop bars like Bar Rouge or Flair for an elevated perspective.

2. Tianzifang: Artsy Alleyways & Quirky Cafés

Hidden Street Art

Tianzifang is a labyrinth of narrow lanes filled with indie boutiques, street art, and cozy cafés. Every corner is a potential Instagram post—think colorful murals, vintage doors, and hanging lanterns.

Café Culture

Stop by Kommune or Lost Bakery for a coffee break. Their rustic interiors and outdoor seating make for great lifestyle shots.

3. Lujiazui: Futuristic Urban Jungle

Pudong’s Skyline

Lujiazui is the financial heart of Shanghai, home to the Shanghai Tower, Jin Mao Tower, and Oriental Pearl Tower. For the best angles:
- Binjiang Avenue: A pedestrian walkway with unobstructed skyline views.
- Shanghai World Financial Center Observatory: Shoot from above for a bird’s-eye view.

Reflection Shots

After rain, puddles near Super Brand Mall create stunning reflections of the skyscrapers—perfect for a mirror-effect shot.

4. Yu Garden: Traditional Chinese Elegance

Classic Pavilions & Bridges

Yu Garden is a 16th-century classical Chinese garden with zigzag bridges, koi ponds, and intricate rooftops. The Exquisite Jade Rock and Huxinting Teahouse are must-capture spots.

Night Illumination

Visit during the Lantern Festival for an otherworldly experience—the garden lights up with colorful lanterns, making it a fantasy-like setting.

5. M50 Creative Park: Industrial Chic & Street Art

Graffiti & Murals

This former textile factory is now Shanghai’s top art district. The walls are covered in bold graffiti and contemporary murals—ideal for edgy, urban shots.

Art Galleries & Cafés

Check out Creater Space or The Press for minimalist backdrops that scream "aesthetic."

6. Shanghai Disneyland: Whimsical & Colorful

Enchanted Storybook Castle

The largest Disney castle in the world is a fairy-tale dream. Best shots:
- Front Plaza: Classic castle shot.
- At night: When the castle lights up for fireworks.

Instagrammable Rides

Don’t miss Tron Lightcycle Power Run—its neon-blue tracks make for a futuristic photo.

7. Fuxing Park: Parisian Vibes in Shanghai

European-Style Gardens

Fuxing Park blends French and Chinese landscaping. The rose gardens, fountains, and tree-lined paths are perfect for a romantic, Parisian-inspired feed.

Morning Tai Chi

Capture locals practicing tai chi in the early hours for an authentic Shanghai moment.

8. Jing’an Temple: Golden Grandeur

Ancient Meets Modern

This 1,700-year-old Buddhist temple sits amidst skyscrapers, creating a striking contrast. The golden roof and giant incense burners are incredibly photogenic.

Nighttime Glow

The temple is beautifully illuminated after dark, making it a mystical backdrop.

9. Wukang Road: Historic Mansions & Autumn Leaves

Colonial Architecture

This tree-lined street is famous for its European-style villas. The Wukang Mansion and Normandie Apartments are top photo ops.

Fall Foliage

Visit in November when the ginkgo trees turn golden—pure Instagram gold.

10. Propaganda Poster Art Centre: Retro & Nostalgic

Vintage Communist Posters

This underground museum houses Mao-era propaganda art. The bold colors and dramatic compositions make for unique, retro-style posts.

Moody Lighting

The dim lighting adds a cinematic vibe—great for moody portraits.

Bonus: Hidden Gems & Local Favorites

1933 Old Millfun

An Art Deco slaughterhouse turned creative hub—its spiral staircases and concrete walkways are a dark academia dream.

Zhujiajiao Water Town

A short trip from Shanghai, this ancient canal town offers gondola rides and stone bridges straight out of a painting.

Smart Coffee (Yongkang Road)

A tiny café with a retro neon sign—ideal for a hipster coffee shot.

Shanghai is a city of contrasts, where every corner tells a story. Whether you’re chasing neon lights, ancient temples, or artsy hideouts, these Instagram spots will make your feed unforgettable. Grab your camera and start exploring!
