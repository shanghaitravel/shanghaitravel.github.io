The Best Shanghai Dishes for a Perfect Brunch

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shanghai, a city where East meets West, offers a brunch scene unlike any other. Whether you're a traveler exploring the Bund or a local looking for a leisurely weekend meal, Shanghai’s culinary delights will leave you craving more. From steaming xiaolongbao to crispy scallion pancakes, here’s your ultimate guide to the best Shanghai dishes for a perfect brunch.

The Irresistible Classics

Xiaolongbao (Soup Dumplings)

No brunch in Shanghai is complete without xiaolongbao. These delicate steamed dumplings are filled with savory pork and a burst of hot, flavorful broth. The best spots, like Jia Jia Tang Bao or Din Tai Fung, serve them fresh out of bamboo steamers. Pro tip: Dip them in Zhenjiang vinegar with ginger slivers for an extra kick.

Shengjianbao (Pan-Fried Pork Buns)

For those who prefer a crispy texture, shengjianbao is a must-try. These pan-fried buns have a juicy pork filling and a golden, crunchy bottom. Yang’s Fry Dumplings is a legendary spot where locals queue up for these addictive bites. Pair them with a light soy-vinegar dip for balance.

Cong You Bing (Scallion Pancakes)

A simple yet satisfying dish, cong you bing is a flaky, layered pancake infused with scallions. Street vendors and upscale cafes alike serve this crispy delight. For an elevated version, try the ones at Fu Chun Xiaolong, where they’re cooked to perfection—crispy outside, chewy inside.

Sweet Treats to Balance the Meal

Ci Fan Tuan (Glutinous Rice Balls)

This handheld snack is a brunch favorite. Sticky glutinous rice wraps around a filling of crispy youtiao (fried dough) and savory or sweet ingredients. The classic version includes pork floss and pickled vegetables, but sweet variations with black sesame or red bean paste are equally delicious.

Doujiang (Soy Milk) & Youtiao (Fried Dough Sticks)

A quintessential Shanghai breakfast combo, doujiang and youtiao is comfort food at its finest. Freshly made soy milk—served hot or cold—pairs perfectly with crispy, golden youtiao. For an authentic experience, visit local breakfast stalls where this duo is a morning ritual.

Modern Twists on Traditional Flavors

Avocado & Century Egg Toast

Shanghai’s café culture has given brunch a creative spin. Avocado toast gets a local upgrade with slices of century egg (皮蛋, pídàn), a preserved egg with a unique umami flavor. Spots like Baker & Spice or Egg Café serve this fusion dish with a drizzle of soy sauce and chili oil.

Matcha Red Bean Pancakes

For those with a sweet tooth, matcha red bean pancakes are a trendy brunch pick. Fluffy matcha-infused pancakes are layered with sweet red bean paste, creating a harmonious blend of flavors. Café like Fine Café & Canteen in the French Concession excel at this modern dessert-brunch hybrid.

Where to Enjoy the Best Brunch in Shanghai

The Bund: Luxury with a View

For a brunch with skyline views, head to Mr & Mrs Bund or Mercato. These high-end spots offer both Western and Shanghainese dishes, making them perfect for travelers who want the best of both worlds.

Tianzifang: Quaint Cafés & Hidden Gems

This artsy lane is packed with charming cafés and small eateries. Try Kommune for a relaxed vibe or Lost Bakery for freshly baked pastries with a Shanghainese twist.

Jing’an: Trendy & International

Jing’an District is a hub for expats and foodies. Places like Al’s Diner serve hearty brunch plates, while local joints like Xiao Yang Sheng Jian keep the traditional flavors alive.

Tips for the Ultimate Shanghai Brunch Experience

  • Go early – Popular spots like Jia Jia Tang Bao sell out by midday.
  • Share dishes – Brunch is best enjoyed family-style, so order multiple items to taste everything.
  • Try street food – Some of the best flavors come from unassuming stalls. Don’t skip them!
  • Pair with tea – A pot of jasmine or chrysanthemum tea complements rich flavors beautifully.

From time-honored classics to modern fusions, Shanghai’s brunch scene is a feast for the senses. Whether you’re strolling along the Huangpu River or exploring hidden alleys, these dishes will make your morning unforgettable.
