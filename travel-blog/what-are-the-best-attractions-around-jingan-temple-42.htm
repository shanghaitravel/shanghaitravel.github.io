What Are the Best Attractions Around Jing'an Temple?

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|

Jing'an Temple, located in the bustling Jing'an District of Shanghai, is a fascinating mix of history, culture, and modernity. This iconic Buddhist temple is surrounded by an array of attractions that cater to diverse interests, from shopping and dining to art and leisure. Whether you're a first-time visitor or a seasoned traveler, there’s always something new to explore near this historic landmark. Here’s a detailed guide to the best things to do around Jing'an Temple.

Exploring Jing'an Temple Itself

Before venturing out, take some time to explore Jing'an Temple. This Buddhist temple, originally built in 247 AD, is an architectural marvel. The intricate wood carvings, majestic golden statues, and tranquil courtyards offer a peaceful respite from the city's fast pace. Don’t miss the massive jade Buddha and the grand hall, where devotees light incense and pray.

Opening Hours and Entrance Fee

The temple is open daily, typically from morning until late afternoon. Visitors should check the specific timings and entrance fees before planning their visit.

Jing'an Park: A Green Escape

Directly across from Jing'an Temple lies Jing'an Park, a serene oasis amidst the urban chaos. The park features winding pathways, traditional pavilions, and lush greenery, making it an ideal spot for a leisurely stroll or a moment of relaxation. Early mornings are particularly vibrant as locals practice tai chi or engage in group dancing. The koi pond and small bamboo forest add to the park's charm.

Activities to Enjoy

  • Watch elderly residents play chess or mahjong.
  • Join locals in tai chi or dancing sessions.
  • Enjoy a picnic or read a book by the pond.

Shopping at Jing'an Kerry Centre

If you're in the mood for some retail therapy, Jing'an Kerry Centre is a must-visit. This luxury shopping mall boasts a wide range of high-end brands, trendy boutiques, and international designer stores. In addition to shopping, the mall offers a plethora of dining options, from fine dining restaurants to casual cafes.

Highlights

  • Explore flagship stores of luxury brands like Gucci and Louis Vuitton.
  • Enjoy gourmet meals at restaurants like Din Tai Fung or Wagas.
  • Relax at one of the chic rooftop bars with views of the city skyline.

Art and Culture at MOCA Shanghai

Located a short walk from Jing'an Temple, the Museum of Contemporary Art (MOCA) Shanghai is a haven for art lovers. This museum showcases cutting-edge exhibitions from both local and international artists, making it a dynamic space for exploring modern art trends.

Current Exhibitions

Check the museum’s website for current and upcoming exhibitions. MOCA regularly hosts interactive installations, photography displays, and multimedia art shows.

Tips for Visiting

  • Allocate at least two hours to fully experience the exhibits.
  • The museum often has English descriptions for its displays, making it accessible for international visitors.

The Former Residence of Mao Zedong

History buffs will appreciate a visit to the Former Residence of Mao Zedong, located nearby on Maoming Road. This small but significant museum offers insight into the life of Mao during his time in Shanghai. The residence is preserved with period furnishings, documents, and photographs.

What to Expect

  • A glimpse into the history of China's revolutionary period.
  • Exhibits showcasing Mao’s correspondence and writings.
  • A quiet, reflective atmosphere away from the busy streets.

Food and Dining Around Jing'an

Jing'an District is a food lover’s paradise, with countless dining options ranging from street food stalls to Michelin-starred restaurants. Here are some top recommendations:

Local Delights

  • Yang's Dumplings: Renowned for its pan-fried soup dumplings (shengjian bao), Yang’s Dumplings is a must-visit for an authentic taste of Shanghai.
  • Old Jesse: This traditional restaurant serves some of the best Shanghainese cuisine, including braised pork belly and drunken chicken.

International Cuisine

  • Mr & Mrs Bund: For a fine dining experience with a French twist, this restaurant is an excellent choice.
  • Element Fresh: Known for its healthy salads, wraps, and juices, it’s a great spot for casual dining.

Nightlife in Jing'an

As the sun sets, Jing'an comes alive with vibrant nightlife options. From cozy bars to energetic nightclubs, the district offers something for everyone.

Recommendations

  • The Apartment: A stylish bar with a rooftop terrace, perfect for cocktails and conversations.
  • Revolucion Cocktail: A lively spot known for its Latin-inspired drinks and music.
  • Arkham: A popular nightclub hosting international DJs and live music events.

Unique Experiences: Jing'an Sculpture Park

Art and nature enthusiasts should not miss Jing'an Sculpture Park. This expansive green space features a variety of modern sculptures and installations. The park frequently hosts outdoor exhibitions and art festivals, making it a dynamic cultural hub.

Things to Do

  • Admire sculptures created by renowned artists from around the world.
  • Attend one of the seasonal art fairs or workshops.
  • Enjoy a family-friendly environment with playgrounds and open spaces.

Day Trips from Jing'an

If you have extra time, consider exploring nearby attractions that are easily accessible from Jing'an District.

Jade Buddha Temple

Just a short drive away, Jade Buddha Temple is another significant Buddhist site in Shanghai. It houses two stunning jade Buddha statues brought from Myanmar, alongside beautiful traditional architecture.

The Bund

A quick metro ride from Jing'an Temple takes you to The Bund, Shanghai's iconic waterfront area. The historic architecture, coupled with stunning views of the modern skyline, makes it a perfect spot for photos and leisurely walks.

Nanjing Road

One of the world’s busiest shopping streets, Nanjing Road offers an endless array of stores, restaurants, and entertainment options. Whether you're window shopping or indulging in some retail therapy, the vibrant atmosphere is sure to leave an impression.

Tips for Visiting Jing'an Area

  • Best Time to Visit: Early mornings and evenings are ideal for exploring Jing'an Temple and nearby attractions.
  • Transportation: The Jing'an Temple metro station (Line 2 and Line 7) makes it convenient to access the area.
  • Walking-Friendly: Many attractions are within walking distance, so wear comfortable shoes.
  • Cultural Etiquette: When visiting temples, dress modestly and respect local customs.

Jing'an District is a vibrant blend of tradition and modernity, offering endless opportunities for exploration and discovery. Whether you’re drawn to its spiritual landmarks, contemporary art, or bustling shopping streets, there’s no shortage of experiences to make your visit unforgettable.
