Shanghai’s Best Budget-Friendly Korean BBQ

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

If you’re craving sizzling meats, bold flavors, and an unforgettable dining experience without breaking the bank, Shanghai’s Korean BBQ scene has got you covered. From smoky galbi (marinated short ribs) to crispy samgyeopsal (pork belly), the city offers plenty of wallet-friendly spots that deliver authentic Korean barbecue. Whether you’re a local foodie or a traveler exploring Shanghai’s culinary gems, here’s your ultimate guide to the best budget-friendly Korean BBQ joints in the city.

Why Korean BBQ in Shanghai?

Korean BBQ has taken Shanghai by storm, blending the city’s love for communal dining with Korea’s rich grilling traditions. What makes it even better? Many restaurants offer all-you-can-eat deals or affordable à la carte options, making it perfect for groups, solo adventurers, or anyone looking for a flavorful feast. Plus, the interactive cooking experience—grilling your own meats right at the table—adds a fun twist to mealtime.

Top Budget-Friendly Korean BBQ Spots

1. Gyu-Kaku (牛角) – Affordable Luxury

While Gyu-Kaku is a Japanese chain, its Korean-inspired BBQ offerings in Shanghai are a hit. The lunch sets are especially budget-friendly, featuring premium cuts like harami (skirt steak) and kalbi at reasonable prices. Don’t miss their signature miso-marinated meats, which pack a punch of umami.

Must-Try:
- Kalbi (Beef Short Ribs) – Tender, sweet, and perfectly charred.
- Pork Belly with Garlic Soy Sauce – Crispy edges with a savory glaze.

2. Gogiyo (高基屋) – All-You-Can-Eat Heaven

For those with a big appetite, Gogiyo offers an unbeatable all-you-can-eat deal under 150 RMB. The menu includes marinated meats, fresh veggies, and classic Korean sides like kimchi and pickled radish. The casual, no-frills vibe makes it a favorite among students and young professionals.

Pro Tip: Go during off-peak hours to avoid the crowd and grill at your own pace.

3. Jang Gun (장군) – Authentic Flavors, Wallet-Friendly Prices

Tucked away in Hongkou’s Korean district, Jang Gun is a hidden gem serving up traditional Korean BBQ without the tourist markup. Their set menus are a steal, often including banchan (side dishes), soup, and rice. The staff is friendly and will gladly help first-timers navigate the grilling process.

Must-Try:
- Samgyeopsal (Pork Belly) – Unmarinated, letting the natural flavors shine.
- Dakgalbi (Spicy Marinated Chicken) – A fiery alternative to beef and pork.

4. K-Town BBQ (K-TOWN BBQ) – Trendy Vibes, Great Deals

Located near People’s Square, K-Town BBQ combines Instagram-worthy aesthetics with affordable pricing. Their lunch specials include a protein, rice, and soup for under 60 RMB. The modern, industrial-chic decor makes it a great spot for snapping food pics while enjoying quality BBQ.

Must-Try:
- Bulgogi (Sweet Soy-Marinated Beef) – A crowd-pleaser with melt-in-your-mouth texture.
- Cheese Corn Side Dish – A decadent, cheesy accompaniment to grilled meats.

Tips for Enjoying Korean BBQ on a Budget

Go for Lunch Specials

Many Korean BBQ spots offer discounted lunch sets, making it the perfect time to indulge without overspending. Look for combos that include meat, rice, and soup—they’re often half the price of dinner menus.

Share with Friends

Korean BBQ is meant to be communal. Splitting an all-you-can-eat package or ordering a few meats to share keeps costs low while letting you sample more dishes.

Skip the Fancy Cuts

While premium Wagyu or aged beef is tempting, sticking to classic cuts like pork belly or chicken keeps the bill down without sacrificing flavor.

BYOB (Bring Your Own Booze)

Some budget-friendly spots allow you to bring your own beer or soju, saving you money on drinks. Just check the restaurant’s policy beforehand.

Beyond the Grill: Must-Try Korean Sides

No Korean BBQ experience is complete without banchan (side dishes). Here are a few budget-friendly favorites to round out your meal:

  • Kimchi – Spicy fermented cabbage, a staple at every table.
  • Japchae – Sweet potato glass noodles stir-fried with veggies.
  • Gyeran-jjim – Steamed egg custard, fluffy and comforting.

Final Thoughts

Shanghai’s Korean BBQ scene proves you don’t need to splurge for a memorable meal. Whether you’re digging into all-you-can-eat spreads or savoring à la carte specialties, these budget-friendly spots deliver big flavors without the hefty price tag. So fire up the grill, gather your friends, and get ready for a meaty adventure in the heart of Shanghai.
