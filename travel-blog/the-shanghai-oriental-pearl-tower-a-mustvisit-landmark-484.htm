The Shanghai Oriental Pearl Tower: A Must-Visit Landmark

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Standing tall along the Huangpu River, the Shanghai Oriental Pearl Tower is more than just a broadcasting tower—it’s a symbol of China’s rapid modernization and a must-see attraction for travelers. With its futuristic design, breathtaking views, and immersive experiences, this iconic structure offers something for everyone. Whether you're a first-time visitor or a seasoned traveler, here’s why the Oriental Pearl Tower deserves a spot on your Shanghai itinerary.

The Architectural Marvel

A Fusion of Tradition and Innovation

Designed by architects Jia Huan Cheng and Zhang Bo, the Oriental Pearl Tower was completed in 1994 and quickly became one of Shanghai’s most recognizable landmarks. Its unique structure, featuring 11 spheres of varying sizes, was inspired by a line from a Tang Dynasty poem: "大珠小珠落玉盘" (Dà zhū xiǎo zhū luò yù pán), which translates to "large and small pearls falling onto a jade plate." The design blends traditional Chinese aesthetics with cutting-edge engineering.

Record-Breaking Heights

At 468 meters (1,535 feet), the tower held the title of the tallest structure in China until 2007. While it’s no longer the tallest, its observation decks—especially the Sightseeing Floor at 263 meters and the Transparent Glass Skywalk at 259 meters—remain unmatched for panoramic views of Shanghai’s skyline.

Unmissable Experiences

The Glass Skywalk: A Thrill Seeker’s Dream

One of the tower’s most exhilarating attractions is its transparent glass-floored observation deck. Walking on this platform feels like floating above the city, offering a dizzying yet unforgettable perspective of the bustling streets below. If you’re afraid of heights, this might test your limits—but the bragging rights are worth it!

The Revolving Restaurant: Dining in the Clouds

Located at 267 meters, the Oriental Pearl Revolving Restaurant is one of the highest rotating restaurants in Asia. Serving a mix of Chinese and Western cuisine, it completes a full rotation every two hours, allowing diners to enjoy 360-degree views of Shanghai while savoring gourmet dishes. Pro tip: Book a table at sunset for the most magical experience.

The Shanghai History Museum

At the tower’s base, the Shanghai Urban History Development Museum offers a fascinating journey through the city’s past. Lifelike dioramas, antique artifacts, and multimedia exhibits showcase Shanghai’s transformation from a small fishing village to a global metropolis. It’s a great way to ground your visit in historical context before heading up to the futuristic heights.

Best Times to Visit

Avoiding the Crowds

The Oriental Pearl Tower is one of Shanghai’s most popular attractions, so timing is key. Weekday mornings (right at opening time, 8:30 AM) are ideal for avoiding long lines. Evenings are also spectacular, as the tower lights up with a dazzling LED display, but expect larger crowds.

Seasonal Considerations

Spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) offer the most comfortable weather for visiting. Summers can be hot and humid, while winter brings chilly winds—though the views are just as stunning year-round.

Nearby Attractions

The Bund: A Perfect Pairing

No visit to the Oriental Pearl Tower is complete without exploring The Bund, just across the Huangpu River. This historic waterfront promenade contrasts the tower’s modernity with its colonial-era architecture. Stroll along the riverbank for postcard-perfect photos of both old and new Shanghai.

Lujiazui Financial District: Skyscraper Central

Adjacent to the tower, Lujiazui is home to some of the world’s tallest skyscrapers, including the Shanghai Tower and Jin Mao Tower. Architecture enthusiasts will love comparing these giants to the Oriental Pearl’s playful design.

Practical Tips for Visitors

Ticket Options

The tower offers several ticket tiers:
- Basic Admission: Access to the main observation decks.
- Premium Tickets: Includes the glass skywalk and the Shanghai History Museum.
- VIP Packages: Skip-the-line access and dining credits.

Getting There

The tower is easily accessible via Metro Line 2 (Lujiazui Station, Exit 1). Taxis and rideshares are also convenient, but traffic can be heavy during peak hours.

Photography Tips

For the best shots:
- Use a wide-angle lens to capture the tower’s full height.
- Visit at dusk for a mix of daylight and neon-lit nightscapes.
- The Binjiang Avenue pedestrian area offers unobstructed riverfront views.

Fun Facts You Didn’t Know

  • The tower’s pink lighting during October is for Breast Cancer Awareness Month.
  • It was featured in Mission: Impossible III, where Tom Cruise’s character scales its exterior.
  • The largest sphere houses a hotel, though it’s not open to the public for overnight stays.

The Shanghai Oriental Pearl Tower isn’t just a sight to see—it’s an experience to remember. From its awe-inspiring architecture to its heart-pounding skywalk, this landmark captures the spirit of a city that’s always reaching for the future.
