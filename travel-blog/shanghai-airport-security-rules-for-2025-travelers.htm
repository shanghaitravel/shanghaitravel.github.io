Shanghai Airport Security Rules for 2025 Travelers

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

The year is 2025, and the world of travel has evolved. For those dreaming of wandering the futuristic Bund, exploring the serene Yu Garden, or diving into the city's pulsating culinary scene, Shanghai awaits. But before you can sip a cocktail in a cloud-top bar, you must first master the art of the airport security check. Shanghai's airports—Pudong (PVG) and Hongqiao (SHA)—are not just gateways; they are a first glimpse into China's high-tech, efficient future. The security protocols have been refined for a new era, blending cutting-edge technology with streamlined processes. This isn't just about rules; it's about a seamless start to your adventure. Forget the horror stories of long, chaotic lines. The 2025 experience is designed to be swift, smart, and surprisingly smooth, provided you come prepared.

The Digital Traveler's Foundation: Documentation & Biometrics

Gone are the days of frantically searching for your paper boarding pass and passport. The foundation of the 2025 Shanghai airport experience is digital and biometric.

Your Digital Identity: More Than a Passport

Your passport remains essential, but it's no longer the sole star of the show. Ensure it has at least six months of validity remaining from your date of entry. Alongside it, you must have a valid Chinese visa. The key 2025 update is the integration of your visa and passport data into the airport's biometric ecosystem. Upon your first checkpoint, you will be directed to a kiosk where your passport will be scanned, and a live photo will be taken. This photo creates your unique biometric token for the journey.

The "Face as Boarding Pass" System

At major checkpoints—baggage drop, security screening, and boarding—you will encounter facial recognition cameras. Simply look into the camera. The system will verify your identity against your flight information in milliseconds. You rarely need to present a physical or digital boarding pass at these points. However, always have a digital copy on your phone as a backup. This system drastically reduces queue times and creates a touchless, fluid journey through the terminal.

Health Declarations: The New Normal

While global health crises have subsided, a digital health declaration remains a standard pre-travel requirement. Approximately 72 hours before your flight, you must complete the China Customs Health Declaration form via the official WeChat mini-app or website. A QR code will be generated, which you must show to officials upon arrival. While not strictly a "security" rule in the traditional sense, it is a mandatory step for entry and is checked immediately after disembarking.

Smart Packing for a Swift Security Screening

What you pack, and how you pack it, is more critical than ever. The rules are strictly enforced, but technology has made the inspection process more transparent and faster.

The Liquids & Gels Revolution: The 100ml Rule Gets an Upgrade

The classic 100ml container rule still applies. All liquids, aerosols, and gels must be in containers of 100 milliliters or less and placed inside a single, transparent, resealable plastic bag of approximately 1-liter capacity. The 2025 twist? The screening technology can now analyze the chemical composition of liquids inside bags and even sealed bottles without opening them. This means fewer secondary bag searches. However, do not test the system; non-compliant items will be confiscated. Pro Tip: Pack expensive skincare or larger liquid items in your checked luggage. Duty-free purchases made after security in your departure airport are generally fine, but ensure they are in the security tamper-evident bag provided.

Electronics: The "Everything-Out" Rule is Over

Rejoice, digital nomads! Thanks to advanced CT (Computed Tomography) scanners for carry-on luggage, you no longer need to remove your laptop, tablet, or camera from your bag. These 3D scanners allow security officers to inspect your bag's contents from every angle. However, this is not universal. Be prepared to follow the instructions at your specific lane. Some lanes might still require you to place large power banks and dense electronics in a separate bin. A good rule of thumb: keep your electronics organized and not buried under a mess of cables and clothes to facilitate a quick scan.

The Forbidden List: Power Banks and Smart Luggage

Power banks are a major focus. They must be carried in your hand luggage only. Power banks with a capacity exceeding 32,000mAh (or approximately 160Wh) are strictly prohibited. The watt-hour rating must be clearly labeled on the power bank itself. If it's not, it may be denied. Smart luggage with non-removable lithium batteries is banned from both checked and carry-on luggage. If your suitcase has a removable battery, you must take it out and carry it with you in the cabin.

Navigating the Checkpoint: A Step-by-Step Walkthrough

Knowing what to expect can eliminate anxiety and help you move like a seasoned pro.

Step 1: The Pre-Check Queuing Area

Look for the digital signage above the lanes. Some lanes are designated for "Expert Travelers" (those familiar with the process) and families. Choose the appropriate lane. Have your passport ready for the initial officer, who will direct you to a biometric kiosk if it's your first entry into the system for the day.

Step 2: The Bin Process

Even with advanced scanners, you will still use bins for certain items. You must place the following in separate bins: * Your transparent bag of liquids. * Your jacket, coat, or belt with a large metal buckle. * Any metal items from your pockets (keys, coins, wallet). * If instructed, place your entire carry-on bag in a bin.

Step 3: The Body Scanner

Millimeter-wave body scanners are standard. You will be asked to step inside, stand in a designated position with your feet apart and hands slightly raised. The scan takes a few seconds. If an anomaly is detected, you will undergo a polite and professional pat-down by a security officer of your gender.

Special Considerations for 2025 Travelers

Traveling with Tech: Drones, Gimbals, and VR Headsets

Shanghai is a content creator's paradise, but your gear needs to pass security. Drones are subject to strict regulations. You must carry them in your hand luggage, and the batteries must be removed and protected. Be prepared to explain your equipment. It is highly advisable to check China's specific drone laws regarding registration and no-fly zones (which include most of urban Shanghai) before you travel. Gimbals and VR headsets are generally permitted but may be subject to additional screening.

Family Travel and Medical Needs

Traveling with infants and young children? Formula, breast milk, and baby food are exempt from the 100ml liquid rule in reasonable quantities. Inform the security officer before screening begins. The same applies for essential liquid medicines, for which you should carry a doctor's note or prescription. Strollers will need to go through the X-ray machine.

The Cultural Artifact & Souvenir Caveat

Be cautious when purchasing souvenirs that could be considered cultural artifacts. There are strict laws against exporting antiques and certain works of art without proper permits. Replicas are generally fine, but if you have any doubt, declare the item and ask. A seemingly innocent purchase could lead to significant delays and confiscations at security.

The journey through Shanghai's airport security in 2025 is a preview of the city you are about to explore: orderly, technologically advanced, and focused on efficiency. By understanding and respecting these rules, you transform a potentially stressful chore into a simple, quick step. You'll be through the checkpoint and on your way to experiencing the magic of Shanghai faster than you can say "Xiexie." Your adventure begins not at the gate, but the moment you step into the airport. Embrace the future, pack smart, and travel well.
