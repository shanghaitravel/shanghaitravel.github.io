Must-Visit Shopping Malls in Shanghai

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|

Shanghai is a paradise for shoppers, boasting an array of world-class shopping malls that cater to diverse tastes and preferences. From luxury fashion brands to local artisan crafts, the city offers a shopping experience that combines modern sophistication with cultural heritage. Whether you're a fashion enthusiast, tech lover, or someone looking for unique souvenirs, Shanghai’s shopping malls have something special for everyone.

Plaza 66 (恒隆广场)

Plaza 66 is one of the most prestigious shopping malls in Shanghai, located in the heart of Jing’an District. Known for its collection of luxury brands, this mall is a favorite destination for high-end shoppers.

  • Brands to Explore: Gucci, Prada, Louis Vuitton, and Hermès.
  • Dining Options: Fine dining restaurants like Jade Garden and upmarket cafes are scattered throughout the mall.
  • Unique Features: The mall often hosts exclusive brand events and pop-up exhibitions, offering a peek into the world of haute couture.

IAPM Mall (环贸广场)

Located on Huaihai Road, IAPM Mall is a trendy shopping center that attracts a young and stylish crowd. It stands out for its late-night shopping hours, making it ideal for evening explorers.

  • Fashion Highlights: Chanel, Balenciaga, and Saint Laurent are among the premium stores.
  • Entertainment: A state-of-the-art cinema and several chic bars add to its appeal.
  • Dining: From Michelin-starred restaurants to casual eateries like Din Tai Fung, the food options are diverse.

Super Brand Mall (正大广场)

Super Brand Mall in Pudong is a family-friendly shopping destination with something for everyone. Situated near the iconic Oriental Pearl Tower, it combines shopping, dining, and entertainment under one roof.

  • Shops to Visit: Mid-range brands like Zara, H&M, and Uniqlo, alongside local boutiques.
  • Family Fun: An indoor amusement park and kids' play area make it perfect for families.
  • Dining: With over 70 restaurants, you can sample everything from Chinese dim sum to international cuisines.

Shanghai IFC Mall (上海国际金融中心)

The IFC Mall in Lujiazui epitomizes elegance and luxury. Overlooking the Huangpu River, it is part of the iconic financial district and attracts both tourists and professionals.

  • Luxury Brands: Dior, Tiffany & Co., and Cartier are among the top-tier stores.
  • Special Attractions: A rooftop garden with stunning views of the Bund.
  • Dining: Michelin-starred establishments like Imperial Treasure Fine Chinese Cuisine.

K11 Art Mall

Blending shopping with art, K11 Art Mall in Huangpu District offers a unique cultural experience. The mall’s focus on art and sustainability sets it apart from traditional shopping centers.

  • Artistic Experience: Regular art exhibitions and installations are featured throughout the mall.
  • Fashion Meets Art: High-end brands like Alexander McQueen are paired with local designers.
  • Dining: A variety of organic and health-conscious restaurants.

Global Harbor (环球港)

Global Harbor is one of the largest shopping malls in Shanghai, featuring a European-style design that makes it an architectural marvel as well as a retail destination.

  • Shopping Variety: From fast fashion like Forever 21 to premium Chinese brands.
  • Cultural Appeal: The design includes stunning frescoes, sculptures, and even a mini museum.
  • Entertainment: A cinema, ice-skating rink, and arcade provide activities beyond shopping.

Xintiandi Style

Xintiandi Style is a boutique shopping mall that combines modern trends with historical charm. Located in the fashionable Xintiandi district, it’s perfect for those looking for unique, high-quality products.

  • Niche Stores: Independent designers and local brands offer exclusive collections.
  • Dining Scene: Upscale restaurants and cozy cafes are perfect for a leisurely break.
  • Surroundings: Its proximity to the pedestrian streets of Xintiandi adds to its allure.

Raffles City

Raffles City is a versatile mall located at People’s Square, making it a convenient stop for tourists exploring the city center. Its modern design and diverse offerings make it a crowd favorite.

  • Fashion and Accessories: Brands like Coach, Levi’s, and Sephora cater to different tastes.
  • Technology: Stores like Apple and Xiaomi provide the latest gadgets.
  • Dining: A large food court and rooftop restaurants.

Grand Gateway 66 (港汇恒隆广场)

Situated in Xujiahui, Grand Gateway 66 is a comprehensive shopping destination with a blend of luxury and high-street brands.

  • Retail Highlights: Max Mara, Sandro, and international sportswear brands.
  • Entertainment: A luxury cinema and frequent promotional events.
  • Convenience: Directly connected to the metro for easy access.

Joy City (大悦城)

Joy City, located in Jing’an, is a youthful and vibrant shopping center that appeals to a trendy crowd.

  • Unique Features: Interactive art installations and a Ferris wheel on the rooftop.
  • Retail Mix: Popular Japanese and Korean brands like Muji and Innisfree.
  • Dining: Creative eateries offering everything from bubble tea to fusion cuisine.

Hongqiao Paradise Walk

Located near Hongqiao Railway Station, this mall is perfect for travelers looking to shop before catching a train.

  • Shopping Highlights: A mix of international and Chinese brands.
  • Family-Friendly: Play areas for kids and family-oriented stores.
  • Dining: Casual dining options for a quick bite.

Metro City (美罗城)

Metro City in Xujiahui is a tech lover's haven, known for its electronics and gaming stores.

  • Tech Heaven: Shops specializing in gadgets, gaming, and accessories.
  • Other Shopping: Fashion outlets and bookstores round out the experience.
  • Dining: Affordable eateries serving local snacks and quick meals.

Huaihai Road Malls

Huaihai Road, often compared to Paris’s Champs-Élysées, is lined with various malls and boutiques.

  • Lane Crawford: Offers luxury goods and designer brands.
  • Parkson Mall: Known for mid-range fashion and accessories.
  • Shops to Explore: Both international chains and local designers.

Westgate Mall (梅龙镇广场)

Westgate Mall in Jing’an District is a high-end shopping destination with a focus on luxury and premium brands.

  • Luxury Retailers: Fendi, Burberry, and more.
  • Dining: Gourmet restaurants and chic cafes.
  • Ambiance: A quieter, more sophisticated shopping environment.

Shanghai’s shopping malls are more than just retail centers; they are lifestyle hubs that offer entertainment, dining, and cultural experiences. Whether you’re hunting for the latest fashion, indulging in world-class cuisine, or simply soaking in the vibrant atmosphere, these malls provide an unforgettable shopping adventure.
