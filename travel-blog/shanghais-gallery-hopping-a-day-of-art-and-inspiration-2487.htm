Shanghai’s Gallery Hopping: A Day of Art and Inspiration

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shanghai, a city where futuristic skyscrapers meet colonial-era architecture, is also a thriving hub for contemporary art. For travelers and locals alike, spending a day gallery hopping through Shanghai’s art districts is a journey through creativity, culture, and inspiration. From the iconic M50 Creative Park to the sleek Power Station of Art, here’s how to make the most of a day immersed in Shanghai’s vibrant art scene.

The Best Neighborhoods for Art Lovers

M50 Creative Park: The Heart of Shanghai’s Art Scene

Tucked away in a former textile factory complex along Suzhou Creek, M50 is Shanghai’s most famous art district. With over 100 galleries, studios, and creative spaces, this industrial-chic neighborhood is a must-visit for anyone exploring the city’s contemporary art scene.

Highlights:
- ShanghART Gallery – One of China’s most influential contemporary art galleries, showcasing works by leading artists like Zeng Fanzhi and Ding Yi.
- Island6 Arts Center – A collective known for its tech-driven installations and interactive exhibits.
- Random street art – The entire complex is covered in murals and graffiti, making it a photographer’s paradise.

Pro tip: Visit on a weekday to avoid crowds and catch artists at work in their studios.

West Bund: Where Art Meets the Huangpu River

The West Bund has rapidly transformed into Shanghai’s answer to New York’s Chelsea district. With world-class museums, sleek galleries, and riverside views, this area is perfect for a leisurely art-filled afternoon.

Must-See Spots:
- Long Museum (West Bund) – Founded by billionaire collectors Liu Yiqian and Wang Wei, this private museum houses everything from classical Chinese art to cutting-edge installations.
- Tank Shanghai – A former fuel depot turned avant-garde art space, featuring massive industrial tanks repurposed as exhibition halls.
- Yuz Museum – Known for its collaborations with international artists like KAWS and its striking architecture.

Don’t miss the riverside promenade—perfect for a coffee break with skyline views.

Tianzifang & Former French Concession: Quirky Galleries & Boutiques

For a more bohemian vibe, head to Tianzifang or the Former French Concession. These labyrinthine lanes are packed with small galleries, indie boutiques, and cozy cafés.

Hidden Gems:
- Art Labor – A boutique gallery focusing on emerging Chinese artists.
- Studio Rouge – A mix of contemporary art and vintage Shanghai charm.
- Street murals & pop-up exhibits – The area is constantly evolving, with new installations appearing unexpectedly.

Planning Your Gallery-Hopping Day

Morning: M50 & Coffee Breaks

Start your day at M50, where you can spend a few hours exploring galleries and snapping photos of the vibrant street art. Stop by Café du Monde for a croissant and espresso—it’s a favorite among local artists.

Afternoon: West Bund & Museum Hopping

Take a taxi or the metro to the West Bund for a more polished art experience. The Long Museum and Yuz Museum are both expansive, so allocate at least two hours for each. If you’re short on time, prioritize Tank Shanghai for its unique industrial-art fusion.

Evening: Tianzifang & Drinks

Wrap up your day in Tianzifang, where the narrow alleys come alive at night. Grab a cocktail at Bar Rouge or a craft beer at The Cannery while discussing the day’s artistic discoveries.

Insider Tips for Art Travelers

  • Free Admission Days – Many museums, like the Power Station of Art, offer free entry on certain days (usually the first Tuesday of the month).
  • Gallery Openings – Check local listings for exhibition openings—these often include free drinks and a chance to meet artists.
  • Art Fairs – If visiting in November, don’t miss West Bund Art & Design Fair, one of Asia’s most prestigious art events.

Beyond Galleries: Art-Inspired Experiences

Stay at an Art Hotel

For the full immersion, book a room at The Middle House or PUYI, both of which feature rotating art exhibitions in their lobbies.

Take a Workshop

Several galleries, like Island6, offer hands-on workshops where visitors can try their hand at digital art or silk-screen printing.

Art-Themed Dining

End your day at Ultraviolet by Paul Pairet, a multi-sensory dining experience that feels like edible art.

Shanghai’s art scene is as dynamic as the city itself—constantly evolving, bold, and full of surprises. Whether you’re a seasoned collector or just looking for inspiration, a day of gallery hopping in Shanghai promises unforgettable discoveries.
