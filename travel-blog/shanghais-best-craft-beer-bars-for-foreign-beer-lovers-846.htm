Shanghai’s Best Craft Beer Bars for Foreign Beer Lovers

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shanghai is a city that never sleeps, and its craft beer scene is just as vibrant. For foreign beer enthusiasts, the city offers an impressive array of bars serving everything from locally brewed IPAs to rare international imports. Whether you're a hophead, a stout lover, or just someone who enjoys a cold pint in a cozy setting, Shanghai has something for you. Here’s a curated list of the best craft beer bars in the city that cater to foreign tastes and offer unforgettable experiences.

The Rise of Craft Beer in Shanghai

Over the past decade, Shanghai has seen a craft beer revolution. What started as a niche hobby for expats and locals alike has grown into a thriving industry. Microbreweries and taprooms have popped up across the city, offering unique flavors that challenge the dominance of mass-produced lagers. The demand for quality beer has led to collaborations between local brewers and international experts, resulting in brews that blend global techniques with Chinese ingredients.

Why Foreign Beer Lovers Should Explore Shanghai’s Scene

For travelers and expats, Shanghai’s craft beer bars are more than just places to drink—they’re social hubs where cultures collide. Many bars host events like tap takeovers, live music, and beer-pairing dinners, making them perfect for meeting fellow beer lovers. Plus, with English-speaking staff and menus, navigating the scene is a breeze.

Top Craft Beer Bars in Shanghai

1. The Brew

Located in the heart of the Former French Concession, The Brew is a pioneer in Shanghai’s craft beer movement. This brewpub not only serves its own line of house-made beers but also features a rotating selection of guest taps from around the world.

Must-Try Beers:

  • Jing’an IPA – A bold, citrusy IPA with a local twist.
  • Honey Ale – Brewed with Chinese honey for a smooth, slightly sweet finish.

The industrial-chic decor and spacious outdoor seating make it a great spot for groups. Don’t miss their beer-infused dishes, like the IPA-battered fish and chips.

2. Boxing Cat Brewery

A household name among Shanghai’s beer aficionados, Boxing Cat Brewery combines American-style brewing with a laid-back, sports-bar vibe. With multiple locations across the city, their flagship spot in Xuhui is a must-visit.

Highlights:

  • Right Hook Helles – A crisp, refreshing lager perfect for Shanghai’s humid summers.
  • TKO IPA – A West Coast-style IPA with a punch of tropical fruit notes.

Their rooftop terrace is ideal for sunny afternoons, and their weekly trivia nights draw a lively crowd.

3. Liquid Laundry

Part beer bar, part gourmet restaurant, Liquid Laundry is where craft beer meets elevated dining. Their extensive tap list features both local and international brews, with a focus on small-batch producers.

Beer Pairing Recommendations:

  • Pair their Smoked Porter with the house-made charcuterie board.
  • The Sour Ale complements their tangy barbecue ribs perfectly.

The sleek, modern interior and knowledgeable staff make it a top choice for beer geeks and foodies alike.

4. Shanghai Beer Factory

For those who want to see the brewing process up close, Shanghai Beer Factory in Pudong offers a behind-the-scenes experience. This microbrewery serves fresh, unfiltered beers straight from the tanks.

Unique Offerings:

  • Dragon’s Breath Stout – A rich, chocolatey stout with a hint of Sichuan peppercorn.
  • Jasmine Lager – A light, floral brew inspired by Chinese tea culture.

Their weekend brewery tours are a hit with visitors, and the industrial-chic taproom is a great place to unwind.

5. Cheers Beer & Bites

Tucked away in Jing’an, Cheers is a cozy neighborhood bar with a rotating selection of 20+ craft beers on tap. The owner, a former expat, ensures the menu caters to international tastes.

Standout Picks:

  • Mango Wheat Ale – A fruity, refreshing summer sipper.
  • Double Chocolate Brown Ale – Dessert in a glass.

The bar’s friendly vibe and regular live music make it a favorite among locals and expats.

Hidden Gems for Beer Explorers

The Beer Lady

This unassuming hole-in-the-wall in Changning is a treasure trove for rare imports. The owner, affectionately known as “The Beer Lady,” has an encyclopedic knowledge of global brews and always has something new to try.

Tap That

A minimalist bar in Huangpu, Tap That focuses on quality over quantity. With just 10 taps, each beer is carefully curated, often featuring limited-edition releases from top breweries.

Craft Beer Events to Watch For

Shanghai’s beer scene isn’t just about the bars—it’s also about the community. Keep an eye out for these annual events:

  • Shanghai International Beer Festival – A weekend-long celebration featuring hundreds of beers from around the world.
  • Brew & Bites Fest – A food-and-beer pairing event held in the spring and fall.
  • Tap Takeover Nights – Many bars host special nights where a single brewery takes over all the taps.

Tips for Foreign Beer Lovers in Shanghai

  • Ask for samples – Most bars will let you try a small pour before committing to a full pint.
  • Check for happy hours – Many spots offer discounts in the early evening.
  • Follow local breweries on WeChat – This is the best way to stay updated on new releases and events.

Shanghai’s craft beer bars offer a taste of home for foreign visitors while introducing them to exciting new flavors. Whether you’re a seasoned beer nerd or just looking for a great place to unwind, the city’s vibrant scene won’t disappoint.
