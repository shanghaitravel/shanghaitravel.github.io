Shanghai Travel Apps for Fitness and Outdoor Activities

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shanghai, a bustling metropolis known for its futuristic skyline and rich cultural heritage, is also a fantastic destination for fitness enthusiasts and outdoor lovers. Whether you're a local or a traveler, staying active while exploring the city can be incredibly rewarding. Fortunately, several travel apps cater specifically to fitness and outdoor activities in Shanghai, helping you discover the best running routes, cycling paths, hiking trails, and more. Here’s a curated list of must-have apps to enhance your active adventures in Shanghai.

Top Fitness Apps for Exploring Shanghai

1. Strava – Track Your Runs and Rides

Strava is a favorite among runners and cyclists worldwide, and it’s just as useful in Shanghai. The app allows you to record your workouts, compete with others on leaderboards, and discover popular routes.

  • Why Use It in Shanghai?
    • Find scenic running routes along the Huangpu River or through lush parks like Century Park.
    • Join local cycling clubs and explore bike-friendly paths in less congested areas.
    • Track your progress with detailed metrics like pace, distance, and elevation.

2. Nike Run Club – Guided Runs with a Local Twist

Nike Run Club offers personalized coaching and guided runs, making it perfect for travelers who want to stay fit while sightseeing.

  • Shanghai-Specific Features:
    • Pre-mapped routes that pass by landmarks like The Bund and Xintiandi.
    • Audio-guided runs with motivational tips, ideal for solo explorers.
    • Challenges that sync with local running events.

3. Keep (KEEP Fitness App) – Workouts on the Go

While not exclusively a travel app, Keep is widely used in China and offers workout plans that require minimal equipment—perfect for hotel room sessions.

  • Why It’s Great for Travelers:
    • Short, high-intensity workouts that fit into a busy sightseeing schedule.
    • Yoga and stretching routines to recover after long walks.
    • Available in both English and Chinese.

Best Outdoor Adventure Apps

1. AllTrails – Discover Hiking Trails Near Shanghai

If you’re willing to venture beyond the city, AllTrails helps you find hiking spots in nearby areas like Sheshan or Moganshan.

  • Key Benefits:
    • Detailed trail maps with difficulty ratings.
    • User reviews and photos to help you choose the best routes.
    • Offline maps for areas with poor signal.

2. Komoot – Plan Cycling and Walking Tours

Komoot is ideal for those who love exploring on two wheels or on foot. It provides turn-by-turn navigation for custom routes.

  • Shanghai Highlights:
    • Scenic cycling routes along the Suzhou Creek.
    • Themed walking tours, such as historical Shanghai or modern art districts.
    • Voice navigation to keep your eyes on the sights, not your phone.

3. WeChat Mini Programs (Local Hidden Gems)

WeChat isn’t just for messaging—it hosts mini-programs that can enhance your outdoor adventures.

  • Examples:
    • "Shanghai Hiking Trails" – Find group hikes and local guides.
    • "Bike Sharing Apps" – Access Mobike or HelloBike for quick rides.

Apps for Wellness and Recovery

1. Headspace – Mindfulness in a Busy City

After a day of exploring, unwind with guided meditation sessions tailored for travelers.

  • Why It’s Useful:
    • Short meditations to combat jet lag or city fatigue.
    • Sleep sounds to help you rest in noisy urban environments.

2. ClassPass – Book Fitness Classes on the Go

ClassPass partners with gyms and studios across Shanghai, offering everything from boxing to Pilates.

  • Perks for Visitors:
    • Try boutique fitness studios without long-term commitments.
    • Discover unique workouts like aerial yoga or indoor rowing.

Bonus: Navigation and Safety Apps

1. Google Maps (with VPN)

While Google services are restricted in China, using a VPN can help you access maps for route planning.

  • Alternatives:
    • Baidu Maps (本地人首选) – The go-to app for locals, with real-time transit updates.
    • Amap (高德地图) – Another reliable option for walking and public transport.

2. SOS功能 on Smartwatches

Devices like Apple Watch or Garmin have emergency features—useful if you’re exploring remote areas.

With these apps, you can seamlessly blend fitness and adventure into your Shanghai travels. Whether you're running along the Bund, cycling through French Concession, or hiking in nearby mountains, technology can make your journey smoother and more enjoyable. So lace up your shoes, download these tools, and experience Shanghai in a whole new way!
