The Best Documentaries About Shanghai’s Architectural Wonders

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shanghai is a city where the past and future collide in the most spectacular ways. Its skyline is a testament to human ingenuity, blending historic landmarks with futuristic skyscrapers. For travelers and architecture enthusiasts, documentaries offer a captivating way to explore Shanghai’s structural marvels without leaving home. Here’s a curated list of the best documentaries that showcase Shanghai’s architectural wonders, from the colonial-era Bund to the soaring heights of Pudong.

1. "Shanghai: The Fastest Growing City in the World"

A Glimpse into Shanghai’s Rapid Urbanization

This documentary by National Geographic dives into how Shanghai transformed from a modest fishing village into a global megacity. The film highlights key architectural projects, including the Shanghai Tower and the Oriental Pearl TV Tower, while exploring the challenges of rapid urban development.

Why It’s a Must-Watch

  • Stunning cinematography of Pudong’s skyline at night.
  • Interviews with architects behind Shanghai’s most iconic buildings.
  • Insights into sustainable urban planning in a city of 26 million people.

2. "The Bund: Shanghai’s Colonial Legacy"

Exploring the Historic Waterfront

The Bund is Shanghai’s most famous architectural landmark, a stretch of colonial-era buildings that once housed banks and trading companies. This documentary traces the history of the Bund, from its origins as a British concession to its current status as a tourist hotspot.

Key Highlights

  • Before-and-after comparisons of the Bund’s transformation.
  • Stories of the architects who designed these neoclassical and art deco masterpieces.
  • A look at the Bund at night, when its buildings are illuminated in a dazzling light show.

3. "Shanghai Tower: Reaching for the Sky"

The Engineering Marvel of China’s Tallest Building

Standing at 632 meters, the Shanghai Tower is not just a skyscraper—it’s a symbol of China’s ambition. This documentary takes viewers behind the scenes of its construction, revealing the cutting-edge technology that made it possible.

What Makes It Fascinating

  • The twisted design that reduces wind resistance.
  • The world’s fastest elevators, traveling at 20.5 meters per second.
  • The eco-friendly features, including wind turbines and rainwater recycling.

4. "Tianzifang: The Soul of Old Shanghai"

A Walk Through Shanghai’s Artsy Labyrinth

Unlike the city’s towering skyscrapers, Tianzifang is a maze of narrow alleyways filled with boutique shops, art galleries, and cafés. This documentary captures the charm of this preserved Shikumen (stone-gate) neighborhood, where old meets new in the most creative ways.

Why Architecture Lovers Will Enjoy It

  • The adaptive reuse of traditional Shikumen houses.
  • Interviews with local artists who have revitalized the area.
  • The contrast between Tianzifang and modern Shanghai.

5. "The Future of Shanghai: Smart City Innovations"

How Technology is Shaping Shanghai’s Architecture

Shanghai isn’t just building taller—it’s building smarter. This documentary explores how AI, big data, and green technology are being integrated into the city’s infrastructure, from self-sustaining buildings to smart traffic systems.

Notable Features

  • The role of AI in urban planning.
  • Green roofs and vertical gardens in skyscrapers.
  • The vision for Shanghai in 2050.

6. "Longhua Temple: A Sanctuary in the Metropolis"

The Ancient Architecture Hidden in Modern Shanghai

Amidst the glass and steel, Longhua Temple stands as a serene reminder of Shanghai’s spiritual past. This documentary delves into the temple’s Tang Dynasty-inspired architecture and its significance in a rapidly modernizing city.

What Viewers Will Appreciate

  • The intricate wooden carvings and traditional Chinese roofing.
  • The contrast between the temple and nearby skyscrapers.
  • The annual Longhua Temple Fair, a vibrant cultural event.

7. "Disneyland Shanghai: Where Fantasy Meets Architecture"

The Theatrical Design of China’s Largest Disney Park

Disneyland Shanghai isn’t just an amusement park—it’s an architectural spectacle. This documentary breaks down the park’s design, from the Enchanted Storybook Castle (the largest Disney castle in the world) to the futuristic Tomorrowland.

Why It’s Worth Watching

  • The fusion of Chinese and Western architectural styles.
  • The engineering behind the TRON Lightcycle Power Run ride.
  • How Disney adapted its magic for a Chinese audience.

Final Thoughts

Whether you’re planning a trip to Shanghai or simply fascinated by urban design, these documentaries offer an immersive way to experience the city’s architectural brilliance. From historic lanes to space-age towers, Shanghai’s buildings tell a story of innovation, resilience, and cultural fusion.
