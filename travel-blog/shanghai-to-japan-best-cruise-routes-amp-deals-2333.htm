Shanghai to Japan: Best Cruise Routes & Deals

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Cruising from Shanghai to Japan is an unforgettable way to explore the rich culture, stunning landscapes, and vibrant cities of East Asia. Whether you're looking for luxury, adventure, or budget-friendly options, there are plenty of cruise routes and deals to suit every traveler. Below, we’ll dive into the top cruise itineraries, onboard experiences, and insider tips to help you plan the perfect voyage.

Why Choose a Cruise from Shanghai to Japan?

Convenience & Comfort

Forget about airport hassles—cruising offers a seamless travel experience. Once you board, your transportation, accommodation, and entertainment are all taken care of. Plus, you’ll wake up to breathtaking ocean views every morning.

Multiple Destinations in One Trip

A cruise allows you to visit several Japanese ports without the stress of booking separate flights or trains. Popular stops include Fukuoka, Osaka, Tokyo, and Okinawa, each offering unique cultural experiences.

Onboard Luxury & Activities

Modern cruise ships are floating resorts, featuring gourmet dining, live entertainment, spas, and even water parks. Whether you want to relax or stay active, there’s something for everyone.

Top Cruise Routes from Shanghai to Japan

1. Shanghai to Fukuoka & Nagasaki

This route is perfect for first-time cruisers or those short on time. Fukuoka is known for its delicious ramen and historic temples, while Nagasaki offers a fascinating blend of Japanese and Western influences.

  • Duration: 5-7 days
  • Highlights:
    • Exploring Fukuoka’s Canal City
    • Visiting the Peace Park in Nagasaki
    • Sampling Hakata ramen

2. Shanghai to Osaka & Kobe

Ideal for foodies and culture lovers, this route takes you to Osaka, Japan’s culinary capital, and Kobe, famous for its premium beef and scenic harbor.

  • Duration: 7-9 days
  • Highlights:
    • Tasting street food at Dotonbori (Osaka)
    • Visiting Kobe’s sake breweries
    • Taking a day trip to Kyoto

3. Shanghai to Tokyo (Yokohama) & Mt. Fuji

For those who want to experience Japan’s bustling metropolis and natural wonders, this route includes stops near Tokyo and scenic views of Mt. Fuji.

  • Duration: 10-12 days
  • Highlights:
    • Exploring Shibuya and Shinjuku in Tokyo
    • Visiting Hakone’s hot springs
    • Cruising past Mt. Fuji

4. Shanghai to Okinawa & Ishigaki

If you prefer tropical vibes, this route takes you to Okinawa’s white-sand beaches and Ishigaki’s crystal-clear waters—perfect for snorkeling and diving.

  • Duration: 8-10 days
  • Highlights:
    • Relaxing on Miyako Island’s beaches
    • Trying Okinawan soba
    • Exploring the Yaeyama Islands

Best Cruise Lines for Shanghai-Japan Routes

1. Royal Caribbean International

Known for its family-friendly amenities, Royal Caribbean offers exciting onboard activities like rock climbing and Broadway-style shows.

  • Popular Ships: Spectrum of the Seas, Quantum of the Seas
  • Deals to Watch For: Early-bird discounts, free onboard credit

2. Princess Cruises

Princess Cruises focuses on luxury and immersive cultural experiences, including themed dining and local performances.

  • Popular Ships: Diamond Princess, Majestic Princess
  • Deals to Watch For: All-inclusive packages, reduced deposits

3. MSC Cruises

A great option for budget-conscious travelers, MSC Cruises offers affordable fares without sacrificing quality.

  • Popular Ships: MSC Bellissima
  • Deals to Watch For: Kids sail free promotions

4. Norwegian Cruise Line (NCL)

NCL is perfect for those who prefer a freestyle cruising experience with no fixed dining times.

  • Popular Ships: Norwegian Joy
  • Deals to Watch For: Free open bar packages

How to Find the Best Cruise Deals

Book Early or Last-Minute

Early bookings often come with perks like cabin upgrades, while last-minute deals can offer steep discounts.

Travel During Off-Peak Seasons

Avoid Golden Week (late April to early May) and cherry blossom season (March-April) for lower prices.

Use a Travel Agent or Cruise Specialist

They often have access to exclusive promotions and can help customize your itinerary.

Look for All-Inclusive Packages

Some cruises bundle drinks, Wi-Fi, and shore excursions, saving you money in the long run.

Essential Tips for Your Cruise to Japan

Pack Smart

  • Bring comfortable walking shoes for shore excursions.
  • Pack layers—Japan’s weather can vary.
  • Don’t forget a universal adapter for electronics.

Learn Basic Japanese Phrases

While many Japanese people speak English, knowing simple phrases like "arigato" (thank you) can enhance your experience.

Respect Local Customs

  • Remove shoes when entering temples or traditional restaurants.
  • Avoid loud conversations in public transport.

Try Local Cuisine Onboard & Onshore

Many cruises feature Japanese-themed dining nights, but don’t miss out on authentic street food during port stops.

Final Thoughts on Planning Your Cruise

A cruise from Shanghai to Japan is more than just a vacation—it’s a journey through history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re drawn to neon-lit cities, serene temples, or tropical beaches, there’s a perfect cruise waiting for you. Keep an eye on deals, pack wisely, and get ready for an adventure of a lifetime!
