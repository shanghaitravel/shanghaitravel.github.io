Best Luxury Hotels in Shanghai for a Splurge

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shanghai, the glittering gem of China’s east coast, is a city where tradition meets modernity in the most extravagant ways. From the neon-lit skyline of Pudong to the colonial charm of the Bund, this metropolis offers an unparalleled luxury experience. If you’re looking to indulge in the finest accommodations, here’s a curated list of the best luxury hotels in Shanghai that promise opulence, world-class service, and unforgettable memories.

The Pinnacle of Opulence: Top 5 Luxury Hotels

1. The Peninsula Shanghai

Nestled along the historic Bund, The Peninsula Shanghai is the epitome of timeless elegance. With its Art Deco-inspired interiors and panoramic views of the Huangpu River, this hotel seamlessly blends old-world charm with cutting-edge amenities.

Why Stay Here?

  • Exquisite Suites: The Peninsula’s rooms feature bespoke furnishings, marble bathrooms, and high-tech controls (yes, even a touchscreen tablet for room service).
  • Rooftop Bliss: The rooftop bar, Sir Elly’s, offers craft cocktails with a side of jaw-dropping skyline views.
  • Luxury Transport: Arrive in style with the hotel’s fleet of Rolls-Royce Phantoms or a vintage-style Peninsula yacht cruise.

2. Mandarin Oriental Pudong, Shanghai

For those who prefer Pudong’s futuristic vibe, the Mandarin Oriental delivers a sanctuary of sophistication. Its sleek design, infused with subtle Chinese motifs, creates a serene escape from the city’s hustle.

Standout Features

  • Spa Heaven: The award-winning spa offers holistic treatments, including traditional tui na massages.
  • Michelin-Starred Dining: Yong Yi Ting serves refined Cantonese cuisine, while 58° Grill is a carnivore’s paradise.
  • Riverfront Location: Floor-to-ceiling windows ensure every room has a postcard-worthy view.

3. Bulgari Hotel Shanghai

A newcomer with a legacy, Bulgari Hotel Shanghai is where Italian luxury meets Shanghai’s cosmopolitan flair. Located in the vibrant Jing’an District, it’s a haven for fashionistas and aesthetes.

Luxury Perks

  • Designer Everything: Think custom-made Bulgari toiletries, silk-lined walls, and private gardens.
  • Exclusive Club: The Bulgari Lounge offers VIP access to curated events and private shopping experiences.
  • Rooftop Gem: Il Ristorante – Niko Romito serves haute Italian cuisine with a skyline backdrop.

4. The Ritz-Carlton Shanghai, Pudong

Perched on the upper floors of the Shanghai IFC, The Ritz-Carlton Pudong redefines high-rise luxury. Its sky lobby on the 52nd floor sets the tone for an elevated stay—literally and figuratively.

Must-Experience Highlights

  • Cloud 9 Vibes: The Flair Rooftop Bar is one of the highest in Shanghai, perfect for sunset cocktails.
  • Club Level Access: Enjoy personalized butler service and gourmet bites in the exclusive lounge.
  • Art & Culture: The hotel collaborates with local artists, blending contemporary art with luxury.

5. Capella Shanghai

For travelers craving privacy and heritage, Capella Shanghai is a restored shikumen complex in the former French Concession. It’s a boutique gem with a residential feel.

Unique Touches

  • Villa Living: Book a heritage villa with a private plunge pool and courtyard.
  • Cultural Immersion: The hotel offers guided tours of the neighborhood’s hidden lanes (longtangs).
  • Auriga Spa: A wellness retreat with lunar cycle-based treatments.

Beyond the Room: Luxury Experiences in Shanghai

Fine Dining at Its Finest

Shanghai’s luxury hotels are culinary destinations in their own right. From Jean-Georges at Three on the Bund to Ultraviolet (a multi-sensory dining adventure), gourmands will be spoiled for choice.

High-End Shopping

Staying at these hotels often means VIP access to Nanjing Road or the IFC Mall. Some, like Bulgari, even arrange private shopping sprees with personal stylists.

Exclusive Nightlife

Skip the crowded clubs—hotels like The Edition and The PuLi offer chic lounges with live jazz and bespoke mixology.

Final Tips for a Lavish Stay

  • Book a Club Room: The added perks (private check-in, complimentary cocktails) are worth the splurge.
  • Time Your Visit: Spring and autumn offer ideal weather for rooftop indulgence.
  • Go Beyond the Obvious: Ask the concierge for unique experiences, like a private qipao fitting or a hutong food tour.

Shanghai’s luxury hotels aren’t just places to sleep—they’re gateways to the city’s most exclusive experiences. Whether you’re here for business, romance, or pure indulgence, these properties promise a stay that’s as unforgettable as the city itself.
