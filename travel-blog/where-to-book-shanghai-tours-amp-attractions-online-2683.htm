Where to Book Shanghai Tours & Attractions Online

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shanghai, China’s dazzling metropolis, is a city where futuristic skyscrapers stand alongside historic alleyways, and traditional tea houses coexist with avant-garde art galleries. Whether you're a first-time visitor or a seasoned traveler, booking tours and attractions online can save you time, money, and hassle. But with so many platforms available, where should you turn for the best deals and experiences?

Top Platforms for Booking Shanghai Tours

1. Trip.com (Formerly Ctrip)

As one of Asia’s largest online travel agencies, Trip.com offers a vast selection of Shanghai tours, from Huangpu River cruises to day trips to the nearby water towns like Zhujiajiao. Their user-friendly interface and English-language support make it a go-to for international travelers.

Why Choose Trip.com?
- Exclusive discounts for bundled attractions (e.g., Shanghai Tower + Oriental Pearl Tower combo tickets).
- Instant e-ticket delivery.
- 24/7 customer service.

2. Klook

Klook specializes in curated experiences, making it perfect for travelers seeking unique activities like a nighttime bike tour through the French Concession or a dumpling-making class.

Popular Shanghai Experiences on Klook:
- Disneyland Shanghai Fast Pass bundles.
- Private photography tours at Yu Garden.
- Food crawls in Nanjing Road.

3. Viator (by Tripadvisor)

Viator is ideal for travelers who prioritize reviews and ratings. Their platform aggregates feedback from past participants, helping you avoid underwhelming experiences.

Top-Rated Shanghai Tours on Viator:
- Half-day historic walking tour of the Bund.
- Customized private tours with local guides.
- Evening acrobatics show tickets.

4. GetYourGuide

GetYourGuide focuses on flexibility, offering free cancellation up to 24 hours before most tours. This is great for travelers with unpredictable schedules.

Flexible Options Include:
- Skip-the-line tickets for Shanghai Museum.
- Day trips to Hangzhou or Suzhou.
- Huangpu River dinner cruises.

Must-Book Shanghai Attractions in 2024

The Bund: Day vs. Night Tours

The Bund is stunning at any hour, but booking a guided tour can enhance your experience. Night tours often include LED light shows, while day tours dive deeper into the area’s colonial history.

Shanghai Disneyland: Skip the Lines

Disneyland Shanghai is a must-visit, but queues can be brutal. Booking through Klook or Trip.com often includes expedited entry or meal vouchers.

Lujiazui Skyline: Observation Decks Compared

  • Shanghai Tower (632m): The highest, with a glass-floor observation deck.
  • Oriental Pearl Tower (468m): Iconic, with a thrilling glass-bottom walkway.
  • Jin Mao Tower (420m): Less crowded, great for photography.

Pro tip: Bundled tickets for multiple towers are often cheaper.

Niche Experiences Worth Booking Online

Food & Market Tours

Shanghai’s culinary scene is legendary. Platforms like Klook and Viator offer food tours that take you beyond xiaolongbao (soup dumplings) to hidden street food gems.

Art & Culture Walks

From the Power Station of Art to M50’s graffiti alleys, specialized tours help you navigate Shanghai’s thriving contemporary art scene.

Day Trips to Water Towns

Zhujiajiao, Tongli, and Wuzhen are picturesque ancient towns just a short drive from Shanghai. Booking a guided tour ensures seamless transport and local insights.

Money-Saving Tips for Online Bookings

  1. Compare Multiple Platforms – Prices for the same tour can vary significantly.
  2. Look for Combo Deals – Bundling attractions saves money (e.g., Yu Garden + Shanghai Old Street tours).
  3. Check for Last-Minute Discounts – Some apps offer flash sales on same-day tickets.
  4. Use Cashback Sites – Platforms like Rakuten sometimes offer rebates for travel bookings.

Avoiding Scams: Trusted Platforms Only

Stick to well-known sites like those listed above. Avoid third-party sellers on social media or unverified marketplaces. Always read cancellation policies before booking.

Final Pro Tip: Download Offline Maps & e-Tickets

Shanghai’s internet restrictions can make accessing booking confirmations tricky. Save screenshots or offline copies of your tickets to avoid connectivity issues.

Now that you know where to book, it’s time to explore Shanghai’s magic—from its neon-lit streets to its tranquil temples. Happy travels!
