3-Day Shanghai Itinerary: Must-See Attractions & Hidden Gems

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shanghai, China’s glittering metropolis, is a city where futuristic skyscrapers meet historic alleyways, and where Michelin-starred dining coexists with bustling street food stalls. Whether you’re a first-time visitor or a seasoned traveler, this 3-day itinerary will guide you through Shanghai’s iconic landmarks and lesser-known treasures.

Day 1: The Classic Shanghai Experience

Morning: The Bund & Huangpu River

Start your day at The Bund, Shanghai’s iconic waterfront promenade. Stroll along the Huangpu River and marvel at the contrast between the colonial-era buildings on one side and the futuristic skyline of Pudong on the other. For the best views, head to the Bund Sightseeing Tunnel or take a short Huangpu River cruise to see the city from the water.

Pro Tip: Visit early in the morning to avoid crowds and catch the sunrise over Pudong.

Afternoon: Yu Garden & Old Shanghai

Next, dive into Shanghai’s history at Yu Garden, a 16th-century classical Chinese garden with pavilions, koi ponds, and intricate rockeries. After exploring, wander through the adjacent Yuyuan Bazaar, where you can shop for souvenirs or try local snacks like xiaolongbao (soup dumplings) at Nanxiang Steamed Bun Restaurant.

For a deeper dive into Old Shanghai, visit Tianzifang, a labyrinth of artsy alleyways filled with boutique shops, cafes, and galleries. It’s a great spot to pick up unique handicrafts or sip coffee in a hidden courtyard.

Evening: Nanjing Road & Rooftop Bars

As night falls, head to Nanjing Road, one of the world’s busiest shopping streets. Even if shopping isn’t your thing, the neon lights and lively atmosphere are worth experiencing.

Cap off the night with cocktails at a rooftop bar. Bar Rouge or Flair Rooftop (at The Ritz-Carlton) offer stunning views of the Pudong skyline.

Day 2: Modern Shanghai & Hidden Gems

Morning: Pudong’s Skyline

Cross the river to Pudong, Shanghai’s financial hub. Ride the elevator to the top of the Shanghai Tower (the world’s second-tallest building) for panoramic views from the Shanghai Observatory Deck. Alternatively, visit the Oriental Pearl Tower for its glass-bottomed observation floor.

Hidden Gem: Skip the crowds and head to the Cloud 9 bar in the Jin Mao Tower for a drink with a view.

Afternoon: French Concession & Coffee Culture

Spend the afternoon in the French Concession, a leafy district filled with European-style villas and trendy cafes. Walk along Wukang Road to admire the historic architecture, then stop by % Arabica or Lokal for artisanal coffee.

For lunch, try Jianbing (savory Chinese crepes) from a street vendor or dine at Commune Social, a chic tapas-style restaurant.

Evening: Jing’an Temple & Speakeasies

Visit Jing’an Temple, a stunning Buddhist temple surrounded by skyscrapers. At night, the golden pagoda glows against the modern backdrop.

For a unique nightlife experience, seek out one of Shanghai’s speakeasies. Speak Low (hidden behind a bookshelf) or The Odd Couple (a retro-themed bar) are local favorites.

Day 3: Culture & Local Life

Morning: Shanghai Museum & People’s Park

Begin at the Shanghai Museum, home to an impressive collection of ancient Chinese art, including bronzes, ceramics, and calligraphy. Afterward, relax in People’s Park, where locals practice tai chi or dance in the mornings.

Hidden Gem: On weekends, the park hosts the Shanghai Marriage Market, where parents gather to find matches for their single children.

Afternoon: M50 Art District & Local Eats

Head to M50, Shanghai’s premier contemporary art district. This former factory complex is now filled with galleries showcasing cutting-edge Chinese art.

For lunch, venture to A Da Congyoubing for crispy scallion pancakes or Yang’s Fry Dumplings for shengjianbao (pan-fried dumplings).

Evening: Xintiandi & Nightlife

End your trip in Xintiandi, a stylish pedestrian area blending traditional shikumen houses with modern boutiques and restaurants. Dine at Hai by Goga for fusion cuisine or Din Tai Fung for more dumplings.

If you’re up for more nightlife, check out Found 158, a subterranean hub of bars and clubs, or catch a live jazz performance at JZ Club.

This 3-day itinerary balances Shanghai’s must-see attractions with off-the-beaten-path gems, ensuring you experience the city’s dynamic energy and rich culture. Pack comfortable shoes—you’ll need them!
