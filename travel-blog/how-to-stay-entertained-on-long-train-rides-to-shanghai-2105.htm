How to Stay Entertained on Long Train Rides to Shanghai

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Long train rides can be both exciting and tedious, especially when traveling to a bustling metropolis like Shanghai. Whether you’re taking the high-speed rail from Beijing or a scenic route from Hangzhou, keeping yourself entertained is key to making the journey enjoyable. Here’s a comprehensive guide to turning those hours on the train into a fun and productive experience.

Pre-Trip Preparation: Pack Smart

Before you even board the train, your entertainment options start with what you pack. A well-prepared traveler is never bored.

1. Load Up on Digital Content

  • Movies & TV Shows: Download your favorite series or films on Netflix, Disney+, or other streaming platforms. Pro tip: Choose lighthearted comedies or travel documentaries to match the journey’s vibe.
  • Podcasts & Audiobooks: Whether you’re into true crime, self-improvement, or fiction, podcasts and audiobooks are perfect for passive listening.
  • Music Playlists: Curate a mix of relaxing tunes and upbeat tracks to match your mood.

2. Bring a Good Book (or Two)

A physical book or e-reader is a classic way to pass the time. Consider bringing:
- A gripping novel
- A travel guide about Shanghai (bonus points for highlighting places to visit)
- A language-learning book if you want to brush up on Mandarin

3. Portable Gaming

If you’re a gamer, pack a Nintendo Switch, a tablet with mobile games, or even a deck of cards for solo or group play.

Onboard Entertainment: Make the Most of Your Time

Once you’re settled into your seat, it’s time to dive into activities that keep boredom at bay.

1. Engage with Fellow Travelers

Train rides are a great opportunity to meet people. Strike up a conversation with your seatmate—you might learn about hidden gems in Shanghai or even make a new friend.

2. Journaling or Sketching

  • Travel Journal: Document your thoughts, itinerary, or even doodle scenes from the passing landscape.
  • Sketching: If you’re artistically inclined, sketch the countryside views or urban skylines.

3. Learn Something New

Use the time productively by:
- Taking an online course (language, photography, etc.)
- Practicing Mandarin with apps like Duolingo
- Reading up on Shanghai’s history or culture

Tech-Free Alternatives

If you’re trying to reduce screen time, here are some old-school ways to stay entertained.

1. Puzzle Books & Crosswords

Sudoku, crosswords, or word searches are great for mental stimulation.

2. People-Watching

Observe fellow passengers and imagine their stories—train rides are full of interesting characters.

3. Mindfulness & Relaxation

  • Meditate to the rhythm of the train.
  • Practice deep breathing exercises.
  • Simply gaze out the window and enjoy the scenery.

Arrival in Shanghai: Transition Smoothly

As the train pulls into Shanghai, shift your focus to the adventures ahead. Use the last stretch to:
- Review your itinerary.
- Charge your devices.
- Freshen up before stepping into the city’s vibrant energy.

With these tips, your long train ride to Shanghai will fly by—leaving you refreshed and ready to explore.
