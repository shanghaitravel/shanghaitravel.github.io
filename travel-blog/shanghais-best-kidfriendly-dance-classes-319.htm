Shanghai’s Best Kid-Friendly Dance Classes

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shanghai is a city that never stops moving—literally. From its towering skyscrapers to its bustling streets, energy pulses through every corner. For families visiting or living in this dynamic metropolis, finding engaging activities for kids is a top priority. Dance classes offer a perfect blend of fun, fitness, and cultural immersion, making them an ideal choice for young travelers and locals alike.

In this guide, we’ll explore the best kid-friendly dance classes in Shanghai, highlighting studios that combine creativity, skill-building, and a touch of local flair. Whether your child is a budding ballerina, a hip-hop enthusiast, or just loves to move, Shanghai has something to offer.

Why Dance Classes Are a Must for Kids in Shanghai

Dance isn’t just about learning steps—it’s a gateway to creativity, discipline, and cultural appreciation. In a city as diverse as Shanghai, dance classes can also introduce kids to both traditional Chinese arts and global dance styles. Here’s why signing up for a class is a great idea:

  • Physical Activity: In an era of screens, dance keeps kids active and healthy.
  • Social Skills: Group classes encourage teamwork and friendship.
  • Cultural Exposure: Many studios incorporate Chinese dance traditions.
  • Confidence Boost: Performing helps kids build self-esteem.

Top Kid-Friendly Dance Studios in Shanghai

1. Shanghai Dance School (上海舞蹈学校)

Location: Hongqiao District

As one of the most prestigious dance institutions in China, Shanghai Dance School offers world-class training for kids. While it’s known for its rigorous ballet program, they also have introductory classes for younger children (ages 4+).

Highlights:
- Professional ballet, contemporary, and Chinese classical dance.
- Bilingual instructors for international students.
- Annual performances at top venues like the Shanghai Grand Theatre.

Best For: Serious young dancers or those interested in traditional Chinese dance.

2. Dance & Me

Locations: Jing’an, Xuhui, and Pudong

This studio is a favorite among expat families for its playful, inclusive approach. Classes range from toddler movement sessions to hip-hop for teens.

Highlights:
- Mommy & Me classes for ages 1.5–3.
- Hip-hop, jazz, and creative movement options.
- Casual vibe with no pressure to perform.

Best For: Families looking for a relaxed, fun introduction to dance.

3. RMB Dance

Location: Former French Concession

Specializing in street dance and urban styles, RMB Dance is where kids can learn popping, locking, and breaking from some of Shanghai’s best instructors.

Highlights:
- Cool, high-energy classes for ages 6+.
- Frequent battles and showcases.
- Great for kids who love K-pop or TikTok trends.

Best For: Aspiring hip-hop stars and kids who want to dance like their favorite idols.

4. Little Princess Ballet

Locations: Multiple across Shanghai

True to its name, this studio offers enchanting ballet classes designed for young children. The whimsical decor and patient teachers make it a hit with little ones.

Highlights:
- Fairy-tale-themed classes (think tutus and tiaras).
- Small class sizes for personalized attention.
- Recitals with adorable costumes.

Best For: Tiny dancers (ages 3–8) who dream of pirouettes.

5. Shanghai Acrobatic Troupe Kids’ Classes

Location: Near People’s Square

For something uniquely Chinese, these classes blend dance with acrobatics—a traditional art form with roots in Chinese opera.

Highlights:
- Kids learn flexibility, tumbling, and rhythmic dance.
- Exposure to China’s rich performance heritage.
- Spectacular annual shows.

Best For: Adventurous kids who love gymnastics and cultural arts.

Choosing the Right Class for Your Child

With so many options, how do you pick the best fit? Consider these factors:

Age & Skill Level

  • Toddlers (1–4): Look for Mommy & Me or creative movement classes.
  • Beginners (5+): Try ballet, jazz, or introductory hip-hop.
  • Advanced Dancers: Seek out competitive studios or specialized training.

Class Atmosphere

  • Some kids thrive in structured environments (like Shanghai Dance School).
  • Others prefer playful, non-competitive settings (like Dance & Me).

Location & Schedule

  • Studios in expat-heavy areas (Jing’an, Pudong) often cater to international families.
  • Weekends are popular, but weekday after-school slots are also available.

Dance Classes as a Cultural Experience

Shanghai’s dance scene isn’t just about technique—it’s a way to connect with the city’s culture. Many studios incorporate elements like:

  • Chinese Fan Dance: Graceful movements with traditional props.
  • Lion Dance: Energetic routines often seen during Lunar New Year.
  • Minority Folk Dances: Introduces kids to China’s diverse ethnic traditions.

For travelers, these classes can be a memorable part of a Shanghai trip, offering more depth than typical tourist activities.

Tips for Enrolling

  1. Try a Drop-In Class: Many studios offer trial sessions.
  2. Ask About Performances: Some include recitals, which can be a fun goal.
  3. Check for Discounts: Sibling rates or package deals may be available.

From classical ballet to high-energy hip-hop, Shanghai’s dance studios provide endless opportunities for kids to learn, grow, and have a blast. Whether you’re a local or just passing through, enrolling your child in a dance class might be the highlight of your Shanghai adventure.
