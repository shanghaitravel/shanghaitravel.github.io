Shanghai Travel Essentials: Portable Chargers for On-the-Go

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shanghai is a city that never sleeps, and neither should your devices. From snapping photos of the Bund’s skyline to navigating the labyrinth of Nanjing Road, your smartphone is your ultimate travel companion. But with so much to see and do, keeping your devices charged is non-negotiable. That’s where portable chargers come in—your lifeline in this electrifying metropolis.

Why You Need a Portable Charger in Shanghai

The Digital Demands of Modern Travel

Shanghai is a tech-savvy city where QR codes rule. Whether you’re scanning for metro tickets, ordering food, or hailing a Didi, your phone is constantly in use. A dead battery isn’t just an inconvenience—it can derail your entire itinerary.

Limited Charging Opportunities

While cafes and hotels offer charging ports, they’re not always accessible when you’re exploring. Public charging stations exist, but they’re often crowded or require local apps. A portable charger ensures you’re never left scrambling for power.

Top Portable Chargers for Shanghai Travel

1. Anker PowerCore 10000

Compact yet powerful, this charger fits in your pocket and delivers 2-3 full charges for most smartphones. Its fast-charging capability is perfect for topping up between stops at Yu Garden or Tianzifang.

2. Xiaomi Mi Power Bank 3 Pro

A favorite among locals, this 20000mAh beast can charge multiple devices simultaneously. Ideal for long days out, like a trip to Disneyland Shanghai or a cruise on the Huangpu River.

3. RAVPower 60W PD Charger

For travelers with laptops or tablets, this charger’s high wattage is a game-changer. It’s a must for digital nomads working from co-working spaces like WeWork in Jing’an.

Where to Buy Portable Chargers in Shanghai

Electronics Markets

  • Xujiahui Electronics Market: A hub for tech gadgets, where you can haggle for the best deals.
  • Shenzhen Lu Tech Street: Less touristy but packed with authentic bargains.

Convenience Stores & Supermarkets

  • FamilyMart or Lawson: These chains often carry affordable power banks in a pinch.
  • Suning or Gome: Reliable retailers with English-speaking staff.

Pro Tips for Using Portable Chargers in Shanghai

Avoid Counterfeits

Stick to reputable brands like Anker or Xiaomi—cheap knockoffs sold on the street may overheat or damage your devices.

Check Airline Regulations

If you’re flying into Pudong or Hongqiao, ensure your power bank is under 100Wh to comply with aviation safety rules.

Optimize Battery Life

Turn on battery-saving mode, close unused apps, and carry a multi-port charger to juice up multiple devices at once.

Beyond Chargers: Other Tech Essentials for Shanghai

Universal Adapters

Shanghai uses Type A and C plugs, so pack a universal adapter if you’re coming from abroad.

VPN Services

Access to Google, Instagram, and WhatsApp requires a VPN. Services like ExpressVPN or NordVPN are traveler favorites.

Offline Maps

Download Baidu Maps or Google Maps offline to navigate without draining data.

Final Thoughts

Shanghai’s energy is contagious, and with the right portable charger, your devices will keep up with the pace. Whether you’re capturing the neon glow of Lujiazui or live-streaming from the French Concession, staying powered ensures you won’t miss a moment of this dynamic city.
