Best Backpacks for Shanghai’s Hidden Speakeasies

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shanghai never truly sleeps, but after midnight, it learns to whisper. Behind unmarked doors, at the end of dimly lit alleys, and disguised as everything from a vintage tea shop to a working laundry press, lies the city's most thrilling secret: its speakeasy bar scene. This is not a night for the loud, neon-drenched clubs of The Bund. This is a quest, an urban treasure hunt that demands a specific kind of traveler and, most importantly, the perfect backpack. Your bag isn't just an accessory here; it's your trusted companion for an evening of clandestine discovery. It needs to be stylish enough to pass the discerning gaze of a doorman, functional enough to carry your essentials through a night of hopping from one hidden gem to the next, and secure enough to protect your valuables as you lose yourself in the intoxicating atmosphere of old Shanghai glamour.

Why Your Backpack is Your Speakeasy Co-Conspirator

In a city where first impressions are everything, your backpack is the first thing a host or doorman will notice. Arriving at a place like Speak Low, where you must find a secret door in a bartending supply shop, or The Odd Couple, with its sleek, hidden entrance, a bulky hiking pack or a touristy daypack immediately marks you as an outsider. The speakeasy culture is built on an illusion of exclusivity and effortless cool. Your bag must contribute to that narrative.

The Threefold Challenge: Style, Security, and Space

Navigating Shanghai's hidden bars presents a unique set of challenges that your backpack must solve.

First, Style and Stealth. You're not climbing a mountain; you're navigating the slick, polished floors of a bar that might be tucked behind a fake refrigerator door. Your bag should be minimalist, crafted from high-quality materials like full-grain leather, waxed canvas, or technical nylon. Earth tones, blacks, and deep navys are your friends. The goal is to look like a stylish local or a seasoned traveler, not a tourist on a day trip.

Second, Urban Security. Shanghai is incredibly safe, but a packed bar is a packed bar anywhere in the world. A backpack with hidden zippers, lockable compartments, or most importantly, a sleek anti-theft design that sits close to your body is crucial. You want to be able to sling it over a barstool without a second thought, knowing your passport, wallet, and phone are safe from pickpockets.

Third, Practical Capacity. You need to carry your essentials for a night out, but you don't want to be lugging around a suitcase. The perfect speakeasy backpack has just enough room for your "urban survival kit" without looking overstuffed.

Anatomy of the Perfect Speakeasy Backpack

So, what exactly should you be looking for? Let's break down the non-negotiable features.

1. The Material: From Leather to Tech Fabric

  • Waxed Canvas or Leather: This is the classic choice. A well-worn leather backpack or a sturdy waxed canvas model evokes a sense of timeless adventure. It pairs perfectly with the Prohibition-era aesthetic of many bars like Union Trading Company. It’s durable, develops a beautiful patina over time, and looks sophisticated. The downside? It can be heavier and less water-resistant than technical fabrics.
  • High-Density Nylon or Polyester: Modern, lightweight, and often treated for water resistance, these technical fabrics are excellent for a city that can surprise you with a sudden downpour. Brands like Tumi or Peak Design use these materials to create sleek, urban packs that are incredibly functional and easy to clean. They offer a more contemporary, minimalist look.

2. The Compartments: Organized for the Quest

An organized interior is key to a stress-free night. Look for a bag with:

  • A padded laptop/tablet sleeve (13-inch is ideal). You might need to do some work before the fun begins.
  • Multiple internal pockets for organizing cables, a power bank, and your wallet.
  • A dedicated, easy-access pocket for your phone. This is a lifesaver when you're trying to show a reservation confirmation or a map to the next location.
  • A main compartment that is spacious enough for a light jacket or a sweater (Shanghai's evenings can be cool), a compact umbrella, and a small camera.

3. The Carrying System: Comfort for the Long Haul

Your "speakeasy crawl" might take you from the former French Concession to the Jing'an district. Padded, adjustable shoulder straps are a must. A sternum strap can also be incredibly helpful for stability during long walks or crowded subway rides. The back panel should be breathable; you'll be doing a fair amount of walking, and Shanghai's humidity is no joke for most of the year.

Top Backpack Contenders for Your Shanghai Night

Here are a few archetypes of backpacks that would excel on your speakeasy adventure.

The Urban Explorer: Peak Design Everyday Backpack (20L)

This is the ultimate choice for the traveler who is also a creator. Its sleek, low-profile design doesn't scream "camera bag," which is perfect for maintaining a low-key vibe. The signature MagLatch closure provides quick, one-handed access to your gear. The internal organization is superb, with flexible dividers that can accommodate a camera, a lens, your tech essentials, and a jacket. It’s made from durable, weatherproof canvas, and its modern aesthetic fits right in with Shanghai's futuristic-meets-vintage energy.

The Timeless Classic: Filson Journeyman Backpack

Crafted from rugged tin cloth leather and bridle leather straps, the Filson Journeyman is built to last a lifetime and look better with age. It embodies the spirit of classic adventure, making it a perfect match for speakeasies that channel 1920s glamour. It’s not overloaded with techy features, offering a simple, robust main compartment and a few external pockets. This bag says you have refined, understated taste without trying too hard. It’s a statement piece that will earn you nods of approval.

The Sleek Minimalist: Bellroy Classic Backpack Plus

Bellroy is renowned for its intelligent design and premium materials. The Classic Backpack Plus is the epitome of minimalist, urban style. Made from recycled, water-resistant nylon, it’s incredibly lightweight and sleek. Its magic lies in its clever organization—it has a dedicated laptop compartment, a quick-access top pocket, and a main cavity that expands to hold more than you’d expect. It’s the perfect bag for someone who values a clean, uncluttered look and wants a pack that feels almost invisible on their back.

The Secure Fortress: Pacsafe Metrosafe LS450 Backpack

For the security-conscious traveler, the Pacsafe line is unbeatable. This backpack is built with anti-theft features like slash-proof material, lockable zippers, and RFID-blocking pockets. While it looks a bit more technical than the other options, its design is still sleek enough for a night out. If your priority is absolute peace of mind, allowing you to fully immerse yourself in the experience of a place like the opulent Sober Company, this is the backpack for you.

Packing Your Speakeasy Survival Kit

Now that you have the perfect vessel, what goes inside? Here’s your essential packing list for a night of hunting Shanghai's best-kept secrets.

  • The Essentials: Wallet (with some local RMB, as some smaller places may not take foreign cards), phone, a portable power bank (you'll be using maps and taking photos all night).
  • Navigation & Access: Have the addresses and any necessary passcodes or WeChat contacts saved offline. A screenshot of the unmarked door can be a lifesaver!
  • Atmosphere Enhancers: A small, high-quality camera. A pocket-sized notebook and pen to jot down the names of incredible cocktails you'll want to remember.
  • Practical Items: A compact umbrella or a light, packable rain jacket. The weather in Shanghai can change in an instant. A packable tote bag is also a great idea—if you pick up a bottle of baijiu or some souvenirs, you have an extra bag.
  • The Finishing Touch: A breath mint or two. You'll be talking to bartenders and fellow explorers up close, and after a few complex cocktails, it’s a simple courtesy.

The hunt for Shanghai's speakeasies is one of the city's greatest modern pleasures. It’s a journey into its layered soul, past and present. And with the right backpack on your shoulder—a blend of style, function, and security—you’re not just a tourist. You’re an insider, ready to discover the magic that lies just behind the hidden door.
