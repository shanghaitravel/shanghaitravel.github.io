Shanghai’s Coolest Themed Cafés

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shanghai is a city that never stops surprising its visitors. Beyond its towering skyscrapers and historic alleyways, the city boasts an incredible café culture with some of the most imaginative themed spots in the world. Whether you're a coffee enthusiast, a pop-culture fanatic, or just looking for a unique place to unwind, these cafés offer unforgettable experiences. Here’s a curated list of Shanghai’s coolest themed cafés that should be on every traveler’s itinerary.

1. Cat Café: Where Coffee Meets Cuddles

A Purr-fect Escape

If you love cats, Shanghai’s cat cafés are a dream come true. These cozy spots let you sip your latte while surrounded by adorable, playful felines. One of the most popular is “Meow Meow Paradise” in the French Concession, where rescued cats roam freely in a whimsical, cat-friendly environment.

What to Expect

  • A menu of cat-themed drinks (try the “Whisker Latte”).
  • Playtime with friendly, well-cared-for cats.
  • Photo ops with quirky cat décor.

Pro tip: Visit during weekdays to avoid crowds and enjoy more one-on-one time with the kitties.

2. Gaming Cafés: Level Up Your Coffee Break

For the Ultimate Gamer

Shanghai’s gaming cafés blend caffeine with consoles, creating a paradise for gamers. “Player’s Haven” in Jing’an District is a standout, offering high-end PCs, VR setups, and retro gaming corners—all while serving specialty coffee and snacks.

Must-Try Features

  • Competitive gaming tournaments (check their schedule).
  • Themed drinks like the “Power-Up Espresso.”
  • A retro arcade section with classic games.

Whether you’re a casual player or a hardcore gamer, this café is a thrilling pit stop.

3. Bookworm’s Paradise: Literary Cafés

Coffee and Classics

For those who love getting lost in a good book, Shanghai’s literary cafés are a haven. “The Bookworm” (a beloved chain with a Shanghai branch) combines a café, bookstore, and event space, hosting author readings and poetry slams.

Why Book Lovers Adore It

  • Walls lined with books in multiple languages.
  • Quiet corners perfect for reading or writing.
  • A menu of “novelty” drinks like the “Shakespearean Spice Latte.”

It’s the ideal spot to relax with a book and a cup of expertly brewed coffee.

4. Retro Futurism: Sci-Fi Themed Cafés

Step into Another Dimension

Shanghai’s sci-fi cafés transport you to a futuristic world. “Starbase 55” in Xuhui District is decked out like a spaceship, with neon lights, holographic menus, and baristas dressed as astronauts.

Out-of-This-World Highlights

  • Zero-gravity-themed desserts (floating mousse cakes!).
  • Interactive alien “crew members” (staff in costume).
  • A “mission control” coffee bar with high-tech brewing gadgets.

This café is a must for sci-fi fans and Instagrammers alike.

5. Kawaii Culture: Hello Kitty & Anime Cafés

Adorably Over-the-Top

Shanghai’s kawaii (cute) cafés are a pastel-colored dream. The “Hello Kitty Secret Garden” in Huangpu District is a pink wonderland, serving everything from Kitty-shaped waffles to rose petal lattes.

Cuteness Overload

  • Themed afternoon tea sets with character-shaped treats.
  • Limited-edition merchandise for sale.
  • Photo booths with props for the ultimate kawaii snap.

Even if you’re not an anime fan, the sheer creativity is worth the visit.

6. Mystery & Intrigue: Detective-Themed Cafés

Solve a Crime with Your Coffee

For a dash of mystery, Shanghai’s detective cafés like “Sherlock’s Study” offer immersive whodunit experiences. Sip on a “Clue Cappuccino” while solving puzzles or participating in live mystery games.

Sleuth-Worthy Perks

  • Themed décor (think magnifying glasses and vintage typewriters).
  • Mystery boxes with surprise drinks and desserts.
  • Weekly murder mystery nights (reservations recommended).

It’s a fun, interactive way to enjoy your coffee break.

7. Nature Lover’s Retreat: Greenhouse Cafés

Urban Jungle Vibes

Escape the city hustle in Shanghai’s greenhouse cafés, where lush plants and fresh brews coexist. “Botanic Brew” in Pudong is a serene oasis with hanging gardens, terrarium workshops, and organic, plant-based menus.

Green Delights

  • Drinks served in edible flower cups.
  • DIY terrarium sessions (great for souvenirs).
  • A tranquil atmosphere perfect for digital nomads.

If you crave nature without leaving the city, this is your spot.

8. Artistic Flair: Gallery Cafés

Sip Among Masterpieces

Shanghai’s art cafés double as mini-galleries, showcasing local talent. “Canvas & Coffee” in M50 Art District rotates exhibitions monthly, so every visit offers something new.

Creative Perks

  • Artisanal drinks named after famous paintings.
  • Live sketching sessions with local artists.
  • The chance to purchase unique artworks.

A treat for both your taste buds and your creative soul.

9. Time Travel: Vintage & Nostalgia Cafés

A Blast from the Past

Step back in time at cafés like “Old Shanghai Memories”, where 1920s jazz, antique décor, and traditional treats like egg tarts and chrysanthemum tea reign supreme.

Retro Charm

  • Live jazz or vinyl record nights.
  • Vintage photo ops with period costumes.
  • A menu of forgotten Shanghainese delicacies.

Perfect for history buffs and nostalgia seekers.

10. Minimalist Zen: Japanese-Inspired Cafés

Tranquility in a Cup

For a calming experience, Shanghai’s Japanese-style cafés like “Wabi-Sabi Brew” focus on simplicity, matcha rituals, and serene interiors.

Zen Highlights

  • Traditional tea ceremonies (book in advance).
  • Minimalist, Instagram-worthy aesthetics.
  • Seasonal flavors like sakura latte in spring.

Ideal for those seeking a mindful coffee moment.

Shanghai’s themed cafés are more than just places to grab a drink—they’re immersive experiences that reflect the city’s creativity and diversity. Whether you’re a local or a traveler, these spots promise unforgettable memories (and fantastic photos!).
