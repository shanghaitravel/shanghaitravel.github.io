Shanghai Hospitals for Foreigners in 2025 – A Guide

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shanghai, a bustling metropolis blending tradition and modernity, is a top destination for travelers, expats, and digital nomads. As the city continues to grow, so does its healthcare infrastructure. By 2025, Shanghai’s hospitals catering to foreigners will be more advanced, accessible, and tailored to international standards. Whether you’re a tourist needing urgent care or an expat settling in long-term, this guide covers everything you need to know about medical services in Shanghai.

Top Hospitals for Foreigners in Shanghai

1. Shanghai United Family Hospital (SHUFH)

A pioneer in international healthcare, SHUFH remains a top choice for expats and travelers. By 2025, the hospital plans to expand its specialties, including advanced telemedicine services for remote consultations.

Highlights:
- English-speaking staff and Western-trained doctors.
- 24/7 emergency care with international insurance acceptance.
- Pediatric and maternity services for families.

2. ParkwayHealth (Raffles Medical)

With multiple clinics across Shanghai, ParkwayHealth offers convenience and quality care. Their 2025 upgrades include AI-assisted diagnostics and faster appointment systems.

Why Choose ParkwayHealth?
- Same-day appointments for non-emergency cases.
- Dental, dermatology, and wellness check-ups under one roof.
- Partnerships with global insurance providers.

3. Huashan Hospital International Medical Center

Affiliated with Fudan University, this center combines local expertise with international protocols. By 2025, it aims to enhance its multilingual services and medical tourism packages.

Notable Features:
- Specialized departments for chronic disease management.
- VIP wards with luxury amenities for medical tourists.
- Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) integration.

Navigating Healthcare as a Tourist

Emergency Services

In case of emergencies, dial 120 for an ambulance. Major hospitals like SHUFH and Huashan have dedicated ER units for foreigners.

Tips:
- Carry a translated medical history or use apps like Pleco for quick translations.
- Keep your insurance card and passport handy.

Travel Insurance Recommendations

Don’t rely on domestic coverage—opt for international travel insurance. Popular providers in 2025 include:
- Allianz Travel Insurance (covers COVID-19 and adventure sports).
- Cigna Global (ideal for long-term stays).

Wellness Tourism in Shanghai

Integrative Medicine Retreats

Shanghai’s 2025 wellness scene blends high-tech care with holistic healing. Try:
- The Dragon Retreat: Combines TCM, acupuncture, and detox programs.
- Bund Wellness Clinic: Offers cryotherapy and IV drips for jet-lagged travelers.

Luxury Check-Up Packages

For health-conscious tourists, hospitals like SHUFH offer executive physicals, including:
- Full-body MRI scans.
- Genetic testing for personalized nutrition plans.

Tech-Driven Healthcare Innovations

AI and Robotics

By 2025, Shanghai hospitals will adopt:
- AI triage systems to reduce wait times.
- Robot-assisted surgeries for precision treatments.

Digital Health Platforms

Apps like WeDoctor (Weiyi) allow:
- Online consultations with English-speaking doctors.
- Medicine delivery to your hotel.

Final Tips for a Stress-Free Experience

  • Book appointments via hospital apps or international hotlines.
  • Learn basic Mandarin medical terms (e.g., "téngtòng" for pain).
  • Explore pharmacies like Watson’s for over-the-counter remedies.

Shanghai’s healthcare system in 2025 promises efficiency, innovation, and comfort—ensuring you stay healthy while enjoying the city’s vibrant culture and attractions.
