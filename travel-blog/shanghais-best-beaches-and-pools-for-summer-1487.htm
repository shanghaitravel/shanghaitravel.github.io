Shanghai’s Best Beaches and Pools for Summer

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

When summer arrives in Shanghai, the city transforms into a bustling hub of sun-seekers looking to escape the heat. While Shanghai isn’t known for its natural beaches, there are plenty of man-made shores, waterfront spots, and luxurious pools where locals and tourists can cool off. Whether you’re craving a tropical vibe, a family-friendly splash zone, or a chic rooftop pool with skyline views, this guide covers the best beaches and pools in Shanghai for summer 2024.

Top Man-Made Beaches Near Shanghai

1. Jinshan City Beach (金山城市沙滩)

Located about an hour’s drive from downtown Shanghai, Jinshan City Beach is one of the most popular artificial beaches in the region. Stretching over 23,000 square meters, this golden-sand shoreline offers a surprisingly authentic beach experience.

  • Highlights:
    • Soft imported sand perfect for sunbathing
    • Water sports like jet skiing and banana boat rides
    • Annual music festivals and beach volleyball tournaments
  • Best for: Families, groups, and water sports enthusiasts
  • Tip: Visit on weekdays to avoid crowds.

2. Bihaijinsha (碧海金沙) in Fengxian

Another top contender, Bihaijinsha, is a sprawling artificial beach with turquoise waters and palm trees, giving off major tropical resort vibes.

  • Highlights:
    • Crystal-clear lagoon-style swimming area
    • Water slides and inflatable obstacle courses
    • Evening bonfire parties in summer
  • Best for: Young travelers and thrill-seekers
  • Tip: Bring your own snacks—food options are limited.

Best Outdoor Pools in Shanghai

1. The Waterhouse at Cool Docks

This industrial-chic hotel features an open-air rooftop pool with stunning views of the Huangpu River.

  • Highlights:
    • Infinity-edge design overlooking the skyline
    • Poolside cocktails and light bites
    • Nighttime swims under city lights
  • Best for: Couples and Instagrammers
  • Tip: Book a day pass in advance—spots fill up fast.

2. Shanghai Circus World Pool

A hidden gem near the Shanghai Circus World, this Olympic-sized pool is perfect for serious swimmers.

  • Highlights:
    • Less crowded than downtown pools
    • Affordable entry fee
    • Nearby acrobatic shows for post-swim entertainment
  • Best for: Fitness-focused visitors
  • Tip: Check the schedule—it occasionally closes for events.

Luxury Hotel Pools for a VIP Experience

1. The Ritz-Carlton Shanghai, Pudong

The Ritz-Carlton’s 58th-floor pool is a glamorous retreat with panoramic city views.

  • Highlights:
    • Heated indoor/outdoor pool
    • Complimentary fruit-infused water and towels
    • Spa access with pool entry
  • Best for: Luxury travelers
  • Tip: Non-guests can purchase a day pass, but it’s pricey.

2. W Shanghai – The Bund

The W Hotel’s Wet Deck is the ultimate party pool, complete with DJ beats and signature cocktails.

  • Highlights:
    • Poolside cabanas with bottle service
    • Themed pool parties on weekends
    • Stunning sunset views over the Bund
  • Best for: Socialites and nightlife lovers
  • Tip: Dress to impress—this is a stylish crowd.

Hidden Gems & Local Favorites

1. Dianshan Lake (淀山湖)

A natural freshwater lake on the outskirts of Shanghai, Dianshan offers a quieter alternative to crowded beaches.

  • Highlights:
    • Kayaking and paddleboarding rentals
    • Lakeside picnics with local snacks
    • Less commercialized than Jinshan or Bihaijinsha
  • Best for: Nature lovers and solo travelers
  • Tip: Rent a bike to explore the surrounding trails.

2. Shanghai Star Pool (大宁灵石公园泳池)

Tucked inside Daning Park, this public pool is a local secret with reasonable prices.

  • Highlights:
    • Lush park surroundings
    • Separate lanes for lap swimmers
    • Budget-friendly entry
  • Best for: Budget-conscious travelers
  • Tip: Arrive early—it gets busy after noon.

Family-Friendly Water Fun

1. Happy Valley Shanghai Water Park

Adjacent to the famous amusement park, this water park is a hit with kids and teens.

  • Highlights:
    • Wave pools and lazy rivers
    • Giant water slides for adrenaline junkies
    • Shaded areas for parents to relax
  • Best for: Families with older children
  • Tip: Weekdays = shorter lines.

2. Shanghai Ocean Aquarium Splash Zone

A small but fun water play area near the aquarium, perfect for cooling off after a day of marine exploration.

  • Highlights:
    • Interactive fountains and mini slides
    • Shallow water for toddlers
    • Discounted combo tickets with aquarium entry
  • Best for: Young kids
  • Tip: Bring waterproof phone cases—kids will get you wet!

Insider Tips for Beating the Heat

  • Avoid weekends: Popular spots like Jinshan Beach and Happy Valley get packed.
  • Sun protection: Shanghai’s UV index can be brutal—reef-safe sunscreen is a must.
  • Transport hacks: Use Didi for beach trips; metro for downtown pools.
  • Cashless payments: Most places accept WeChat Pay or Alipay, but carry some cash for rural areas.

From glamorous rooftop dips to sandy shores just outside the city, Shanghai’s summer scene has something for every type of traveler. Whether you’re splashing with kids, sipping cocktails by an infinity pool, or riding waves at an artificial beach, these spots promise a refreshing escape from the urban heat.
