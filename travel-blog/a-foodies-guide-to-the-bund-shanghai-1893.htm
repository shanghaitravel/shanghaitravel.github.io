A Foodie’s Guide to The Bund, Shanghai

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

The Bund, Shanghai’s iconic waterfront, is a dazzling fusion of history, architecture, and—most importantly—culinary delights. Whether you’re a first-time visitor or a seasoned traveler, this stretch along the Huangpu River offers an unforgettable gastronomic journey. From Michelin-starred restaurants to hidden street food gems, here’s your ultimate guide to eating your way through The Bund.

The Bund’s Culinary Landscape

A Mix of Old and New

The Bund’s food scene reflects Shanghai’s unique blend of tradition and modernity. On one side, you’ll find century-old establishments serving classic Shanghainese dishes like xiaolongbao (soup dumplings) and hongshao rou (braised pork). On the other, sleek rooftop bars and avant-garde restaurants push the boundaries of global cuisine.

Must-Try Dining Experiences

  1. Riverside Fine Dining – Savor gourmet meals with unbeatable views of Pudong’s skyline.
  2. Local Eateries – Dive into hole-in-the-wall spots for authentic flavors.
  3. Afternoon Tea – Indulge in high tea at historic hotels like the Peace Hotel.

Top Restaurants on The Bund

1. Mr & Mrs Bund

A modern French brasserie by celebrity chef Paul Pairet, this spot redefines luxury dining. Must-tries include the Lemon Tart and the “Bagged” Salmon.

2. Hakkasan

This Michelin-starred Cantonese restaurant offers dim sum with a twist. The crispy duck salad is a crowd favorite.

3. M on the Bund

One of the first high-end Western restaurants in Shanghai, M serves Mediterranean-inspired dishes. Don’t miss their weekend brunch.

4. Fu 1088

Housed in a restored villa, Fu 1088 specializes in refined Shanghainese cuisine. Their drunken chicken and crab roe tofu are legendary.

Street Food & Hidden Gems

Yunnan Road Food Street

Just a short walk from The Bund, this street is a paradise for food lovers. Try:
- Shengjianbao (pan-fried pork buns) at Yang’s Fry-Dumpling
- Congyoubanmian (scallion oil noodles) at local stalls

Nanjing East Road Snacks

From candied hawthorns to stinky tofu, this bustling area offers quick bites for explorers.

Rooftop Bars with a View

Bar Rouge

Famous for its killer cocktails and neon-lit parties, Bar Rouge has one of the best views of the Pudong skyline.

The Captain Bar

A more laid-back option, this spot is perfect for sunset drinks without the crowds.

Tips for Foodies Visiting The Bund

  • Timing Matters – Book rooftop bars early for sunset seats.
  • Dress Code – Some high-end restaurants require smart casual attire.
  • Local Etiquette – It’s common to share dishes in Shanghainese meals.

Whether you’re craving luxury or local flavors, The Bund delivers an unforgettable food adventure. So grab your chopsticks (or fork) and dig in!
