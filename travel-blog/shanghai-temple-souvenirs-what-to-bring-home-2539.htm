Shanghai Temple Souvenirs: What to Bring Home

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shanghai is a city where ancient traditions blend seamlessly with modern innovation. Among its many attractions, the city’s temples stand out as serene oases amidst the urban hustle. Whether you’re visiting the iconic Jade Buddha Temple, the tranquil Longhua Temple, or the historic Jing’an Temple, each offers a unique spiritual experience—and an opportunity to take home meaningful souvenirs. From intricately crafted religious artifacts to modern interpretations of traditional crafts, here’s your guide to the best temple souvenirs in Shanghai.

The Spiritual Significance of Temple Souvenirs

Temple souvenirs aren’t just keepsakes; they often carry deep spiritual meaning. In Chinese culture, items blessed by monks or purchased from sacred sites are believed to bring good fortune, protection, and positive energy. When selecting souvenirs, consider their symbolism—whether it’s a charm for prosperity, a statue for peace, or incense for purification.

Jade Buddha Temple: Treasures of Serenity

One of Shanghai’s most famous temples, the Jade Buddha Temple, is renowned for its exquisite jade Buddha statues. While the temple’s main jade Buddha is too precious to take home, you can find smaller replicas and jade carvings in the temple’s gift shop.

Must-Buy Souvenirs:

  • Mini Jade Buddha Statues – These beautifully carved statues, often made from nephrite jade, symbolize peace and protection.
  • Prayer Beads (Mala) – Used in Buddhist meditation, these beads are often made from sandalwood or jade.
  • Incense and Burners – High-quality incense sticks, blessed by monks, are perfect for creating a calming atmosphere at home.

Longhua Temple: Timeless Traditions

As Shanghai’s oldest temple, Longhua Temple is a treasure trove of traditional Buddhist artifacts. The temple’s annual peach blossom festival adds a seasonal charm to its souvenir offerings.

Must-Buy Souvenirs:

  • Wooden Fish (Muyu) – A percussion instrument used in Buddhist chanting, often ornately carved.
  • Calligraphy Scrolls – Featuring Buddhist scriptures or blessings, these make elegant home decorations.
  • Lucky Red Strings – Tied around the wrist for protection and good luck, these are popular among visitors.

Jing’an Temple: Modern Meets Sacred

Located in the heart of downtown Shanghai, Jing’an Temple blends ancient spirituality with contemporary aesthetics. Its souvenir shop offers a mix of traditional and modern items.

Must-Buy Souvenirs:

  • Gold-Plated Amulets – Designed with intricate Buddhist motifs, these are believed to attract wealth and safety.
  • Tea Sets – Handcrafted ceramic sets, often featuring lotus designs, perfect for tea enthusiasts.
  • Modern Buddhist Jewelry – Stylish bracelets and pendants incorporating mantras or sacred symbols.

Unique Finds Beyond the Temples

While temple gift shops are a great starting point, Shanghai’s markets and boutique stores also offer temple-inspired souvenirs.

Tianzifang & Yu Garden Markets

These artsy districts sell hand-painted fans, embroidered silk pouches, and miniature pagodas—great alternatives if you’re looking for something more decorative.

Local Artisan Workshops

Some workshops near temples create custom-made souvenirs, such as personalized name seals (chops) or embroidered tapestries featuring temple scenery.

Tips for Choosing the Perfect Souvenir

  • Authenticity Matters – Buy directly from temple shops to ensure your souvenirs are blessed and genuine.
  • Cultural Respect – Avoid purchasing items that may be considered disrespectful, such as Buddha heads used purely as decor.
  • Practicality – Consider how you’ll display or use the item—incense burners and tea sets are both functional and meaningful.

Whether you’re drawn to the spiritual significance or the artistic craftsmanship, Shanghai’s temple souvenirs offer a beautiful way to remember your journey. Each piece tells a story—one of tradition, faith, and the timeless allure of this incredible city.
