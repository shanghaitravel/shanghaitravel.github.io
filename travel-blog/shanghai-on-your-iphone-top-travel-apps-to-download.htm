Shanghai on Your iPhone: Top Travel Apps to Download

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Forget bulky maps and phrasebooks. The key to unlocking the thrilling, chaotic, and utterly captivating essence of Shanghai now fits in your palm. Your iPhone is more than a camera; it’s your translator, navigator, concierge, and culinary scout in this city of 26 million. From navigating the labyrinth of the Old City to ordering the perfect xiaolongbao, the right apps transform a visit into an immersive experience. Here is your essential guide to curating the ultimate Shanghai travel toolkit.

The Foundational Trio: Navigation, Translation, Payment

Before you even think about finding the best cocktail bar, master these three pillars. They are the non-negotiables for a smooth Shanghai adventure.

1. Didi Chuxing: Your Key to the Streets

While Uber exists, Didi is the undisputed king of Chinese ride-hailing. Its interface is intuitive even for non-Mandarin speakers. The app offers multiple services: Didi Express (affordable cars), Didi Premier (nicer vehicles), and even taxi-hailing. Crucially, it integrates with Apple Pay and international credit cards, solving the payment hurdle many face. Use it to escape the metro crowds, reach far-flung art districts like M50, or get back to your hotel after a long night on the Bund. Pro tip: Have your destination’s name or address in Chinese characters saved on your phone to show the driver, as English mapping can be imprecise.

2. Pleco: More Than a Dictionary

Google Translate is helpful, but Pleco is powerful. This premier Chinese dictionary app is a traveler’s secret weapon. Its killer feature is the live OCR (Optical Character Recognition). Simply point your iPhone’s camera at a menu, a street sign, or a product label, and it will instantly overlay the English translation. No more pointing blindly at pictures! The basic version is incredibly robust, but consider the paid bundle for full-screen OCR and audio pronunciations. It demystifies the world around you, turning indecipherable characters into actionable information.

3. Alipay: Your Digital Wallet

In Shanghai, cash is king no more. Alipay (and its rival WeChat Pay) is the ubiquitous lifeblood of transactions. From sprawling malls to a tiny street vendor selling jianbing (savory crepes), QR code payments rule. Setting up Alipay as a foreigner has become much easier. Link an international credit card to the "Tour Pass" or "Link Card" feature within the app. Suddenly, you can pay for everything, rent a bike, book tickets, and even order food delivery. Not having it means missing out on 90% of the seamless, cashless convenience that defines modern Chinese urban life.

Feasting Like a Local: Food & Dining Apps

Shanghai is a culinary universe. These apps guide you through its dizzying constellations of flavors, from michelin-starred restaurants to hidden alleyway stalls.

Dianping: The Yelp of China, But So Much More

Think of Dianping as Yelp, OpenTable, and TripAdvisor combined, with a fiercely active user base. Even without reading Chinese, the app is navigable. Use the photo-heavy reviews and the powerful map search. Look for restaurants with a high number of reviews and check the user-uploaded pictures to see the real deal. The "Must-Order Dishes" section, often highlighted by users, is a game-changer. You can also book tables, order delivery, and find nearby food options ranked by popularity. It’s the single best tool to avoid tourist traps and eat where the locals eat.

Meituan: The Everything App for Daily Life

If you only download one "super-app," make it Meituan. It began as a Groupon-like service but has evolved into an astonishing all-in-one platform. Yes, you can order food delivery (a massive selection), but you can also book discounted tickets for attractions (like the Shanghai Tower observation deck), reserve hotpot tables, find massage deals, buy movie tickets, and even schedule a karaoke (KTV) room. Its competitive pricing and vast network make it indispensable for the savvy traveler looking to experience the city’s services and entertainment like a resident.

Exploring & Experiencing: Culture and Logistics

Beyond food and transport, these apps help you dive into Shanghai’s art, history, and vibrant scene, and handle practicalities with ease.

SmartShanghai: The Expat & Insider’s Guide

For English-language, up-to-the-minute information, SmartShanghai is unparalleled. Its website is legendary, and the app distills its best content. It’s not just listings; it’s curated guides, honest bar and restaurant reviews, and news on upcoming events, art exhibitions, pop-up markets, and live music. When you’re done with the classic tourist itinerary and want to know about the new speakeasy in Jing’an, the independent film screening in Xintiandi, or the best brunch spots, this is your source. It provides the cultural context that pure navigation apps lack.

MetroMan Shanghai: Master the Subway

Shanghai’s metro system is vast, efficient, and the best way to traverse the city. While Apple Maps and Google Maps offer transit directions, MetroMan is a dedicated, offline-friendly metro map. It allows you to plan routes, see transfer points, calculate fares, and most importantly, identify the correct exit number—a crucial detail in massive, multi-exit stations like People’s Square. It saves time, battery, and cellular data, making you a confident underground navigator.

Air Quality Index (AQI) Apps

While not unique to Shanghai, monitoring air quality is a practical reality. Apps like AirVisual or Plume provide real-time AQI readings and forecasts. They can help you decide if today is the day for that open-top bus tour or if you should pack a mask. It’s a small but significant tool for planning your daily activities and ensuring comfort.

Bonus: Capturing & Sharing the Magic

VSCO & Lightroom: Editing for the Urban Jungle

Shanghai’s aesthetic—neon-lit skyscrapers, misty grey longtang alleyways, the art deco details—demands a nuanced edit. VSCO offers presets that can enhance the moody, cinematic quality of your shots, while Lightroom Mobile provides powerful tools to balance the extreme contrasts of the city’s lighting, from dark cocktail bars to the blinding lights of Lujiazui.

A VPN Service

This is a technical necessity, not a travel app per se, but it underpins everything. Google services (Maps, Gmail, Translate), Instagram, Facebook, WhatsApp, and many Western news sites are blocked in China. A reliable VPN (like ExpressVPN, NordVPN, or Astrill) is essential before you arrive. Install and test it on your iPhone. It restores your digital lifeline, allowing you to access the apps and information you rely on.

Your journey through Shanghai’s past and future begins with these digital keys. They bridge the gap between visitor and temporary local, turning potential frustrations into moments of discovery. With this curated suite of apps, your iPhone becomes more than a device; it becomes your most insightful travel companion in the dazzling, relentless, and unforgettable city of Shanghai. Now, go charge your battery—you’re going to need it.
