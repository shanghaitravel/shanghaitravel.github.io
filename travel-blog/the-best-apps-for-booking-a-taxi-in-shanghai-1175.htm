The Best Apps for Booking a Taxi in Shanghai

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shanghai is a bustling metropolis where getting around efficiently can make or break your travel experience. Whether you're a tourist exploring the Bund, a business traveler rushing to a meeting in Pudong, or a foodie hunting for the best xiaolongbao, having a reliable taxi-booking app is essential. With so many options available, it can be overwhelming to choose the right one. Here’s a breakdown of the best apps for booking a taxi in Shanghai, tailored to different needs and preferences.

Why Taxi Apps Are a Must in Shanghai

Shanghai’s public transport system is impressive, but sometimes you just need the convenience of a taxi. Hailing a cab on the street can be tricky, especially during peak hours or in crowded areas like Nanjing Road. Taxi apps solve this problem by allowing you to book rides instantly, track your driver, and even pay electronically. Plus, they often offer English-language support, making them a lifesaver for non-Mandarin speakers.

Key Features to Look For

  • English-friendly interface
  • Real-time tracking
  • Multiple payment options (cash, credit card, Alipay, WeChat Pay)
  • Competitive pricing and transparency
  • Safety features (driver ratings, SOS buttons)

Top Taxi-Booking Apps in Shanghai

1. Didi Chuxing (滴滴出行)

Didi is the Uber of China and the most popular ride-hailing app in Shanghai. It’s the go-to choice for locals and travelers alike.

Why Choose Didi?

  • Wide availability: Didi has the largest fleet of drivers in Shanghai.
  • Multiple ride options: From budget-friendly "Express" to premium "Luxury" rides.
  • English version: The app now supports English, making it accessible to foreigners.
  • Safety features: Includes ride-sharing details with contacts and an emergency button.

How to Use Didi

  1. Download the app (available on iOS and Android).
  2. Set your pickup and drop-off locations.
  3. Select your preferred ride type.
  4. Confirm and wait for your driver.

Pro tip: Link your Didi account to Alipay or WeChat Pay for seamless payments.

2. Meituan Dache (美团打车)

Meituan is best known for food delivery, but its ride-hailing service is gaining traction in Shanghai.

Why Choose Meituan?

  • Integrated with Meituan’s ecosystem: Book a ride after ordering food or booking a hotel.
  • Discounts and coupons: Frequent promotions for new and existing users.
  • User-friendly interface: Simple and intuitive, even for first-time users.

Drawbacks

  • Fewer drivers compared to Didi, especially during peak hours.

3. Shouqi Limousine & Chauffeur (首汽约车)

If you prefer a more premium experience, Shouqi is the way to go.

Why Choose Shouqi?

  • Professional drivers: Often used for business travel.
  • Luxury vehicles: Options like Mercedes-Benz and Audi available.
  • Fixed pricing: No surge pricing during peak hours.

Best For

  • Business travelers
  • Special occasions (e.g., airport transfers)

4. Caocao Mobility (曹操出行)

Caocao focuses on eco-friendly rides with a fleet of electric vehicles.

Why Choose Caocao?

  • Green travel: All-electric vehicles reduce your carbon footprint.
  • Affordable pricing: Competitive rates compared to standard taxis.
  • Reliable service: Drivers are trained and vetted.

Best For

  • Environmentally conscious travelers
  • Short to medium-distance trips

5. Traditional Taxi Apps (Haibo, Dazhong)

For those who prefer traditional taxis, apps like Haibo and Dazhong connect you with licensed cabs.

Why Choose Traditional Taxi Apps?

  • Metered fares: No surprises with pricing.
  • Familiarity: Some travelers feel safer with licensed taxis.

Drawbacks

  • Limited English support.
  • Harder to book during rush hours.

Tips for Using Taxi Apps in Shanghai

1. Have Your Destination in Chinese

While some apps support English, having your destination written in Chinese (or a map pin) helps avoid miscommunication.

2. Avoid Peak Hours

Rush hours (7-9 AM, 5-7 PM) mean longer wait times and higher prices.

3. Use Multiple Apps

If one app isn’t working, switch to another—Didi, Meituan, and Shouqi are good backups.

4. Cash Is Still King

While most apps accept digital payments, some drivers prefer cash, especially for short rides.

5. Check for Coupons

Apps like Meituan and Didi often offer discounts for first-time users or during promotions.

Final Thoughts

Shanghai’s taxi apps make navigating the city a breeze. Whether you prioritize affordability, luxury, or eco-friendliness, there’s an app for you. Download a couple before your trip, and you’ll never be stuck waiting for a cab again.
