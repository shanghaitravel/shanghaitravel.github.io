Shanghai Winter Itinerary: 5 Days of Adventure

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Winter in Shanghai is a magical time—crisp air, festive lights, and a unique blend of modern energy and traditional charm. Whether you're craving cozy cafes, bustling markets, or cultural gems, this 5-day itinerary will guide you through the best of Shanghai during the colder months.

Day 1: Arrival & Iconic Landmarks

Morning: The Bund & Skyline Views

Start your adventure at The Bund, Shanghai’s iconic waterfront. The winter light casts a golden glow on the colonial-era buildings, making it perfect for photos. Walk along the promenade and admire the contrast between historic architecture and the futuristic skyline of Pudong. For a bird’s-eye view, head to the Shanghai Tower’s observation deck—the world’s second-tallest building—where you can see the city sprawled beneath you.

Afternoon: Yu Garden & Old Town

Warm up with a visit to Yu Garden, a 16th-century classical Chinese garden adorned with pavilions, koi ponds, and intricate rockeries. Nearby, explore the bustling Old Town and its zigzagging alleys filled with souvenir shops and street food stalls. Don’t miss the Nanxiang Steamed Bun Restaurant for their legendary xiaolongbao (soup dumplings).

Evening: Nanjing Road & Holiday Lights

As night falls, stroll down Nanjing Road, one of the world’s busiest shopping streets, now twinkling with winter decorations. Pop into the Shanghai First Food Hall for local snacks or warm up with a cup of ginger tea from a street vendor.

Day 2: Art & Culture Immersion

Morning: Power Station of Art

Kick off your day at the Power Station of Art, Shanghai’s premier contemporary art museum housed in a converted power plant. The industrial-chic space hosts cutting-edge exhibitions, and the rooftop offers stunning river views.

Afternoon: Tianzifang & French Concession

Head to Tianzifang, a labyrinth of artsy boutiques, cafes, and galleries tucked into Shikumen (stone-gate) buildings. Then, wander through the French Concession, where tree-lined avenues and European-style villas create a romantic winter vibe. Stop by Ferguson Lane for a cozy lunch at a café like Baker & Spice.

Evening: Jazz at Peace Hotel

Cap the night with live jazz at the Peace Hotel’s legendary bar. The vintage ambiance and smooth melodies make it a perfect winter evening escape.

Day 3: Day Trip to Zhujiajiao

All Day: Venice of the East

Escape the city with a day trip to Zhujiajiao, a 1,700-year-old water town just an hour from Shanghai. Glide through canals on a gondola, explore ancient bridges like Fangsheng Bridge, and sample local treats like sticky rice dumplings (zongzi). The misty winter air adds a dreamy quality to the cobblestone streets.

Evening: Riverside Dining in Xintiandi

Return to Shanghai and unwind in Xintiandi, a stylish dining and entertainment district. Book a table at Lost Heaven for Yunnan-inspired cuisine or enjoy fondue at The Refinery.

Day 4: Modern Shanghai & Shopping

Morning: Shanghai Disneyland (Winter Edition)

Embrace your inner child at Shanghai Disneyland, which transforms into a winter wonderland during the season. Ride Tron Lightcycle Power Run, meet Mickey in festive gear, and watch the "Ignite the Magic" nighttime castle show.

Afternoon: Luxury Shopping in Pudong

Head to IFC Mall or Super Brand Mall in Pudong for high-end shopping. Warm up with a matcha latte at % Arabica or indulge in afternoon tea at the Ritz-Carlton.

Evening: Huangpu River Cruise

End the day with a Huangpu River cruise, where the city’s skyline dazzles with holiday lights. Opt for a dinner cruise for a luxurious experience.

Day 5: Relaxation & Hidden Gems

Morning: Jing’an Temple & Brunch

Visit Jing’an Temple, a serene Buddhist temple surrounded by skyscrapers. Then, brunch at Egg or Al’s Diner for Western comfort food.

Afternoon: M50 Creative Park

Discover Shanghai’s indie art scene at M50, a graffiti-covered complex of galleries and studios. Pick up unique souvenirs like prints or handmade ceramics.

Evening: Speakeasy Bar Hopping

Toast to your trip at hidden bars like Speak Low (accessed through a faux bookshelf) or Bar No. 3 for craft cocktails.

From historic lanes to futuristic towers, Shanghai in winter is a feast for the senses. Pack your warmest scarf and get ready for an unforgettable adventure.
