What Are the Best Hotels to Stay in Shanghai?

  • Home|
  • Travel Blog|

Shanghai, a city that seamlessly blends traditional Chinese culture with modern cosmopolitan flair, offers an array of accommodation options to suit every traveler's needs. From luxury international chains to boutique hotels with unique character, Shanghai's hospitality industry reflects the city's dynamic energy. Here’s a guide to some of the most worthwhile hotels in Shanghai, categorized to help you find the perfect stay.

1. Luxurious Hotels for the Ultimate Indulgence

The Peninsula Shanghai

  • Location: The Bund
  • Highlights: The Peninsula Shanghai combines Art Deco elegance with state-of-the-art amenities. Its prime location offers stunning views of the Huangpu River and the futuristic skyline of Pudong. Rooms are spacious, with cutting-edge technology, and the rooftop bar provides an unparalleled panoramic view of the city.
  • Why Stay Here: Ideal for travelers who want to experience Shanghai’s opulence and prime location near historic landmarks.

Waldorf Astoria Shanghai on the Bund

  • Location: The Bund
  • Highlights: Housed in a heritage building, the Waldorf Astoria Shanghai features a blend of classic European design and modern luxury. The Long Bar, an iconic destination within the hotel, is a must-visit for its cocktails and historical ambiance.
  • Why Stay Here: Perfect for those seeking a blend of historical charm and luxury, all while staying close to Shanghai’s most famous attractions.

Park Hyatt Shanghai

  • Location: Lujiazui, Pudong
  • Highlights: Occupying the upper floors of the Shanghai World Financial Center, Park Hyatt Shanghai offers breathtaking views of the city from every room. Its minimalist design exudes sophistication, and the infinity pool on the 85th floor is an experience in itself.
  • Why Stay Here: Best for travelers who want unparalleled views and easy access to Shanghai’s financial district.

Bulgari Hotel Shanghai

  • Location: Suhe Creek
  • Highlights: This Italian luxury brand's Shanghai outpost is a haven of elegance, offering world-class dining options, an exquisite spa, and a serene riverside location. The hotel seamlessly combines modern architecture with traditional Chinese elements.
  • Why Stay Here: Perfect for those who value high-end design and tranquil surroundings while staying close to the city center.

2. Boutique Hotels with Unique Character

The Waterhouse at South Bund

  • Location: South Bund
  • Highlights: This boutique hotel is set in a converted warehouse, showcasing industrial chic design with a touch of modern art. Each room is uniquely designed, and the rooftop terrace offers impressive views of Pudong.
  • Why Stay Here: Great for art lovers and those who appreciate non-traditional, stylish accommodations.

Capella Shanghai Jian Ye Li

  • Location: Xuhui District
  • Highlights: Situated in a restored shikumen (stone-gate house) neighborhood, Capella Shanghai offers a rare glimpse into the city’s architectural history. The hotel features luxurious villas, French-inspired dining, and a tranquil spa.
  • Why Stay Here: Ideal for travelers who want a mix of cultural heritage and modern luxury.

Hotel Indigo Shanghai on the Bund

  • Location: The Bund
  • Highlights: Hotel Indigo offers a fresh and vibrant take on boutique accommodation. With eclectic decor inspired by the city’s maritime history, it’s a feast for the senses. The riverside location and rooftop bar add to its charm.
  • Why Stay Here: Perfect for younger travelers and creative types who value a unique, fun hotel experience.

3. Family-Friendly Hotels

Shanghai Marriott Hotel Parkview

  • Location: Zhabei District
  • Highlights: This hotel offers spacious rooms and family-oriented amenities, including babysitting services and a large indoor pool. It’s close to Daning Lingshi Park, providing a peaceful retreat from the bustling city.
  • Why Stay Here: Great for families traveling with children, offering both convenience and relaxation.

Hyatt on the Bund

  • Location: The Bund
  • Highlights: With large family suites and an on-site spa, this hotel caters to parents and kids alike. The panoramic views from the Sky Bar and easy access to both sides of the river make it a convenient choice.
  • Why Stay Here: Perfect for families who want to stay close to Shanghai’s main attractions.

4. Budget-Friendly Options Without Sacrificing Quality

Campanile Shanghai Natural History Museum Hotel

  • Location: Jing’an District
  • Highlights: A clean, modern hotel with a minimalist design and budget-friendly prices. Its proximity to the Natural History Museum and metro stations makes it a convenient choice.
  • Why Stay Here: Best for travelers looking for affordable accommodations with easy access to public transport.

Mingtown Etour Youth Hostel

  • Location: People’s Square
  • Highlights: This charming hostel offers private rooms and dorms, catering to budget-conscious travelers. The cozy courtyard is a lovely spot to relax after exploring the city.
  • Why Stay Here: Ideal for backpackers and solo travelers who value affordability and social opportunities.

5. Hotels for Business Travelers

Four Seasons Hotel Shanghai

  • Location: Jing’an District
  • Highlights: With excellent business facilities, including meeting rooms and a 24-hour business center, the Four Seasons is a top choice for professionals. Its central location ensures easy access to Shanghai’s commercial hubs.
  • Why Stay Here: Perfect for business travelers seeking luxury and convenience.

Kerry Hotel Pudong

  • Location: Lujiazui, Pudong
  • Highlights: Connected to the Kerry Parkside shopping center, this hotel offers excellent facilities for conferences and events. The hotel’s wellness center and dining options make it a comfortable choice for extended stays.
  • Why Stay Here: Ideal for business travelers attending events in the Pudong area.

6. Hotels with Stunning Views

The Ritz-Carlton Shanghai, Pudong

  • Location: Lujiazui, Pudong
  • Highlights: Overlooking the Huangpu River, the Ritz-Carlton boasts some of the best views of the Bund and Shanghai’s skyline. The rooftop Flair Bar is a popular spot for sunset cocktails.
  • Why Stay Here: Perfect for those who prioritize iconic views and impeccable service.

Pudong Shangri-La, East Shanghai

  • Location: Lujiazui, Pudong
  • Highlights: With floor-to-ceiling windows, the Pudong Shangri-La offers mesmerizing views of the Bund. Its luxurious spa and fine dining options add to its appeal.
  • Why Stay Here: Ideal for travelers who want to immerse themselves in Shanghai’s iconic cityscape.

7. Tips for Choosing the Right Hotel in Shanghai

  1. Consider Your Location Needs: If sightseeing is your priority, stay near the Bund or People’s Square. For business, Pudong or Jing’an are more convenient.
  2. Budget: Shanghai offers options across all price ranges, so plan your stay according to your budget.
  3. Special Features: Whether it’s a rooftop bar, cultural design, or family-friendly amenities, consider what features are most important to you.
  4. Book Early: Hotels in popular areas fill up quickly, especially during peak travel seasons.

Shanghai’s vibrant hotel scene ensures that every traveler, from luxury seekers to budget adventurers, can find their perfect place to stay. Choose your accommodation wisely to make your visit to this dynamic city even more memorable.
