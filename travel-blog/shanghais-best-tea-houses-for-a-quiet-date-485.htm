Shanghai’s Best Tea Houses for a Quiet Date

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shanghai is a city of contrasts—where towering skyscrapers meet tranquil gardens, and bustling streets hide serene tea houses perfect for a quiet date. Whether you're looking for an intimate escape or a cultural experience, these tea houses offer the ideal setting for romance, reflection, and relaxation.

The Charm of Traditional Tea Houses

Huxinting Teahouse: A Historic Retreat

Nestled in the heart of Old Shanghai, Huxinting Teahouse is one of the city’s most iconic spots. Built over a pond in the Yuyuan Garden, this Ming Dynasty-style teahouse exudes old-world charm. The wooden bridges, intricate carvings, and soft lantern light create an enchanting atmosphere.

Order a pot of Longjing (Dragon Well) tea and enjoy the view of koi fish gliding through the water below. The slow, deliberate tea service here encourages conversation, making it perfect for a quiet, unhurried date.

Lao She Teahouse: A Cultural Experience

Named after the famous Chinese writer Lao She, this teahouse blends tradition with entertainment. While it’s known for its evening performances, the daytime hours are ideal for couples seeking a peaceful tea session.

Try their Jasmine Pearl tea, served in delicate porcelain cups. The vintage decor—wooden tables, calligraphy scrolls, and soft guzheng music—sets a nostalgic mood.

Modern Tea Houses with a Romantic Vibe

Song Fang Maison de Thé: A Parisian Twist

For those who love a fusion of East and West, Song Fang Maison de Thé is a must-visit. This elegant tea house, inspired by French aesthetics, offers a curated selection of premium Chinese teas.

Their Dahongpao (Rock Oolong) is a standout, with its rich, mineral notes. The cozy upstairs nook, with its soft lighting and plush seating, is perfect for whispered conversations.

Té Company: Minimalist Elegance

Located in the former French Concession, Té Company is a minimalist’s dream. The clean lines, neutral tones, and curated tea menu create a calming environment.

Their Oriental Beauty Oolong is a favorite—sweet, floral, and complex. The quiet ambiance and attentive service make it an excellent choice for a first date or a relaxed afternoon together.

Hidden Gems for Intimate Moments

Han Tang Tea House: A Secret Garden

Tucked away in a quiet lane near Jing’an Temple, Han Tang Tea House feels like a hidden oasis. The interior is adorned with antique furniture, silk drapes, and bonsai trees, transporting visitors to ancient China.

Their Pu’er tea selection is exceptional, with aged varieties that deepen in flavor over multiple infusions. The private tea rooms, separated by wooden screens, offer seclusion for couples.

Ming Cha Tea House: A Zen Escape

If you and your date appreciate mindfulness, Ming Cha Tea House is the place to go. The decor is simple yet refined, with low wooden tables and floor cushions.

Their White Peony tea is light and fragrant, ideal for sipping slowly while enjoying the quiet. The staff are knowledgeable but unobtrusive, allowing you to focus on each other.

Tea Houses with Scenic Views

The PuLi Hotel’s Tea Lounge: Urban Sophistication

For a luxurious tea date, head to The PuLi Hotel’s Tea Lounge. Overlooking a serene courtyard, this space blends modern luxury with traditional tea culture.

Their Silver Needle white tea is a delicate choice, served with artisanal sweets. The plush seating and soft jazz music create a refined yet relaxed atmosphere.

The Bund Tea Room: Skyline Romance

Perched along the Huangpu River, The Bund Tea Room offers breathtaking views of Shanghai’s skyline. The floor-to-ceiling windows make it a stunning spot for sunset tea.

Try their Tieguanyin (Iron Goddess of Mercy), a floral oolong that pairs beautifully with their dim sum selection. The mix of modern design and classic tea service makes it unforgettable.

Tips for a Perfect Tea House Date

  • Timing Matters: Visit during off-peak hours (weekday afternoons) for a quieter experience.
  • Dress Comfortably: Many tea houses have floor seating, so opt for relaxed attire.
  • Engage with the Ritual: Let the tea master guide you—learning about tea together can be a bonding experience.
  • Pair with Light Snacks: Many places offer traditional sweets or savory bites to complement the tea.

Shanghai’s tea houses are more than just places to drink tea—they’re sanctuaries of calm in a fast-paced city. Whether you prefer historic charm, modern elegance, or hidden retreats, there’s a perfect spot for your next quiet date.
