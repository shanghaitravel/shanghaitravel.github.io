Shanghai’s Waterfront Attractions: A Riverside Map Guide

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shanghai’s waterfront is a dazzling fusion of history, modernity, and culture. Stretching along the Huangpu River, the city’s iconic skyline and historic Bund create a mesmerizing contrast that draws millions of visitors each year. Whether you’re a first-time traveler or a seasoned explorer, this guide will help you navigate the best riverside attractions, hidden gems, and local hotspots.

The Bund: Where History Meets Modernity

A Walk Through Time

No visit to Shanghai is complete without strolling along the Bund (Waitan). This famous waterfront promenade is lined with 52 historic buildings showcasing architectural styles from Gothic to Art Deco. Once the financial heart of colonial Shanghai, the Bund now offers breathtaking views of Pudong’s futuristic skyline across the river.

Must-See Spots:
- The Customs House: Home to the iconic clock tower, a relic of 1920s Shanghai.
- Peace Hotel: A Jazz Age landmark with live music and vintage charm.
- Huangpu Park: The city’s first public park, perfect for skyline photos.

Sunset and Night Views

As dusk falls, the Bund transforms into a glittering spectacle. Neon lights from Pudong’s skyscrapers reflect on the river, creating a postcard-perfect scene. For the best experience, take a Huangpu River cruise or grab a cocktail at Bar Rouge, a rooftop lounge with panoramic views.

Pudong’s Riverside Wonders

Lujiazui: The Skyline of Tomorrow

Just across the river, Pudong’s Lujiazui district is a testament to Shanghai’s rapid growth. This financial hub is home to some of the world’s tallest buildings and cutting-edge attractions.

Top Highlights:
- Shanghai Tower: The second-tallest building globally, with an observation deck on the 118th floor.
- Oriental Pearl Tower: Its futuristic spheres house a glass-bottomed skywalk and a revolving restaurant.
- Shanghai World Financial Center: Known as the "Bottle Opener" for its distinctive shape.

Binjiang Avenue: A Scenic Escape

For a quieter riverside experience, head to Binjiang Avenue. This leafy promenade offers jogging paths, art installations, and open-air cafes. It’s a favorite among locals for morning tai chi or evening strolls.

Hidden Gems Along the Waterfront

Cool Docks and Creative Hubs

Beyond the tourist hotspots, Shanghai’s waterfront hides quirky cultural spaces:
- M50 Art District: A repurposed industrial area now filled with galleries and street art.
- 1933 Old Millfun: A former slaughterhouse turned into a maze of shops and cafes.
- The Cool Docks: A trendy dining and nightlife spot with a canalside vibe.

Xuhui Riverside: The New Hotspot

Recently revitalized, Xuhui’s West Bund is a hub for contemporary art and outdoor activities. Don’t miss:
- Long Museum: A private art museum showcasing Chinese and international works.
- West Bund Art Center: Hosts major exhibitions like the Shanghai Biennale.
- Skate Parks and Bike Trails: Perfect for active travelers.

Food and Drink by the River

Rooftop Bars and Waterfront Eateries

Shanghai’s dining scene thrives along the Huangpu. Try these spots:
- Mr & Mrs Bund: French fine dining with Bund views.
- POP at The Peninsula: A chic rooftop bar for sunset cocktails.
- Lost Heaven on the Bund: Yunnan cuisine in a stylish setting.

Street Food and Local Bites

For a more casual experience, explore:
- Fengjing Old Street: Near the river, offering traditional snacks like shengjianbao (pan-fried buns).
- Nanxiang Steamed Bun Restaurant: A century-old spot famous for xiaolongbao.

Practical Tips for Your Visit

Best Times to Go

  • Early Morning: Avoid crowds and catch sunrise over Pudong.
  • Weekdays: Less busy than weekends.
  • Evening: Ideal for light shows and nightlife.

Getting Around

  • Metro: Lines 2 and 10 serve key waterfront areas.
  • Ferries: Cheap and scenic way to cross the river.
  • Bike Rentals: Mobike and Hello Bike are popular for short trips.

Insider Advice

  • Avoid National Holidays: The Bund gets extremely crowded.
  • Combo Tickets: Save money by bundling attractions like observation decks.
  • Local Guides: Consider a walking tour for deeper historical insights.

Shanghai’s waterfront is a dynamic blend of past and future, offering endless discoveries. Whether you’re marveling at skyscrapers, sipping cocktails with a view, or uncovering hidden art spaces, the riverside promises an unforgettable adventure.
