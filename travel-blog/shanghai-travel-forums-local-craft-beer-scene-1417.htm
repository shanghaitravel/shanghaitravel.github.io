Shanghai Travel Forums: Local Craft Beer Scene

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shanghai is a city that never fails to surprise travelers with its dynamic blend of tradition and modernity. Beyond the iconic skyline and historic landmarks, there’s a buzzing subculture that’s capturing the attention of beer enthusiasts worldwide—the local craft beer scene. Whether you're a casual drinker or a dedicated hophead, Shanghai’s craft beer bars and breweries offer a unique way to experience the city’s contemporary vibe.

Why Shanghai’s Craft Beer Scene Stands Out

Unlike many other global cities where craft beer has been mainstream for decades, Shanghai’s scene is relatively young but rapidly evolving. What makes it special is the fusion of international brewing techniques with local flavors, creating beers that reflect Shanghai’s cosmopolitan identity. From IPAs infused with Sichuan peppercorns to stouts aged in baijiu barrels, the creativity here is unmatched.

The Rise of Homegrown Breweries

Shanghai’s craft beer movement gained momentum in the early 2010s, with pioneers like The Brew and Boxing Cat Brewery leading the charge. These breweries not only introduced high-quality craft beer to locals but also became social hubs for expats and travelers. Today, the city boasts over 30 craft breweries, each with its own distinct personality.

Must-Visit Breweries in Shanghai

  1. Boxing Cat Brewery – One of the OGs of Shanghai’s craft beer scene, Boxing Cat is known for its bold flavors and lively taproom. Their Right Hook Helles and TKO IPA are crowd favorites.
  2. Liquid Laundry – A brewpub with a New York loft vibe, offering house-brewed beers alongside gourmet comfort food.
  3. Mikkeller Shanghai – The Danish brand’s outpost in Shanghai brings Scandinavian brewing finesse to the Bund area.
  4. Shanghai Brewery – A great spot for those who enjoy German-style lagers and hearty pub grub.

Hidden Gems: Craft Beer Bars You Shouldn’t Miss

While breweries are fantastic, some of the best craft beer experiences in Shanghai come from specialized bars that curate selections from both local and international brewers.

Beer Lady

A no-frills, no-nonsense spot with an extensive bottle list and rotating taps. It’s a favorite among locals for its laid-back atmosphere and affordable prices.

Cheers Beer & Burger

A small but mighty bar in the French Concession, offering a solid lineup of craft beers alongside juicy burgers—perfect for a casual night out.

The Hop Project

For those who love experimental brews, this tiny bar in Jing’an frequently features limited-edition collaborations with breweries from across China.

Craft Beer and Shanghai’s Nightlife

Shanghai’s nightlife is legendary, and craft beer has become an integral part of it. Areas like The Bund, Jing’an, and the Former French Concession are hotspots for beer lovers. Many bars host tap takeovers, beer-pairing dinners, and even homebrewing workshops, making it easy for travelers to dive deep into the scene.

Seasonal Beer Festivals

If you’re lucky enough to visit during certain times of the year, don’t miss events like:
- Shanghai International Beer Festival – A massive gathering featuring hundreds of beers from around the world.
- Craft Beer Shanghai Expo – A showcase of local and regional breweries, often with exclusive releases.

Pairing Craft Beer with Local Cuisine

One of the best ways to enjoy Shanghai’s craft beer is by pairing it with the city’s diverse food scene. Here are some killer combinations:
- Xiaolongbao & Pilsner – The crispness of a pilsner cuts through the richness of soup dumplings.
- Spicy Hot Pot & IPA – The hoppy bitterness balances the fiery Sichuan flavors.
- Peking Duck & Amber Ale – The maltiness complements the savory-sweet glaze.

Tips for Travelers Exploring Shanghai’s Beer Scene

  1. Use Ride-Hailing Apps – Many craft beer spots are spread out, so DiDi or Meituan cabs will save time.
  2. Follow Local Beer Groups – WeChat groups like Shanghai Craft Beer Enthusiasts often share updates on new releases and events.
  3. Try a Beer Tour – Companies like Untour Food Tours offer guided craft beer crawls, perfect for first-timers.

The Future of Craft Beer in Shanghai

With more microbreweries popping up and local drinkers embracing craft culture, Shanghai’s beer scene shows no signs of slowing down. Innovations like baijiu-infused sours and tea-based ales prove that brewers here are unafraid to push boundaries.

For travelers looking to experience Shanghai beyond the usual tourist trail, diving into its craft beer culture offers a refreshing (and intoxicating) perspective. Whether you’re sipping a hazy IPA in a hidden speakeasy or chatting with brewers at a taproom, every pint tells a story of this ever-evolving city.
