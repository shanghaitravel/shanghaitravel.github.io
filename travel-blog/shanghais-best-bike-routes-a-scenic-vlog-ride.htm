Shanghai’s Best Bike Routes – A Scenic Vlog Ride

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

The hum of the city is a constant soundtrack in Shanghai, a metropolis that moves at the speed of light. But between the towering skyscrapers and the bustling alleyways, there's another rhythm, a slower, more personal pulse best felt on two wheels. To truly understand Shanghai, you need to peel back the layers, and there's no better way to do that than by bike. This isn't just about transportation; it's about immersion. It's about feeling the wind, discovering hidden corners, and capturing moments that a subway ride or a taxi trip would simply blur past. So, charge your camera, strap on your helmet, and join me on a vlog ride through Shanghai's most scenic and unforgettable bike routes. This is your ultimate guide to seeing the city from a fresh, exhilarating perspective.

The Urban Oasis: A Ride Through The Bund and Former French Concession

This route is the quintessential Shanghai experience, perfectly blending the city's iconic skyline with its charming, historical heart. It's a journey through time, from colonial-era romance to futuristic ambition, and it's packed with endless vlog-worthy backdrops.

Starting Point: The Bund Sightseeing Tunnel - Embracing the Futuristic

We begin our adventure on the Pudong side. Instead of taking a ferry, why not enter through the trippy, psychedelic experience that is the Bund Sightseeing Tunnel? It’s a bizarre, light-and-sound show ride under the Huangpu River that will make for a unique and humorous opening to your vlog. Once you emerge on the other side, you'll find bike rental stations readily available. Get your ride, and let's roll onto the Bund Promenade.

As you cycle along the wide, pedestrian-friendly walkway, the full force of Shanghai's architectural dichotomy hits you. On your left, the historic neoclassical buildings of the Customs House and the Peace Hotel stand as grand reminders of the "Paris of the East." On your right, across the river, the skyscrapers of Lujiazui—the Oriental Pearl Tower, the Shanghai Tower, and the Jin Mao Tower—puncture the sky. This is the money shot for any vlogger. Time your ride for the late afternoon; the golden hour light bathes the old buildings in a warm glow, and as dusk falls, you can capture the spectacular transition into the neon-lit night. The synchronized light show on the Pudong skyscrapers is a cinematic masterpiece waiting for your voice-over.

Diving into the Lanes: The Former French Concession

After soaking in the views from the Bund, we turn inland, leaving the tourist crowds behind and diving into the labyrinthine streets of the Former French Concession. This is where the real magic happens. The air changes. The frantic energy of the Bund gives way to a tranquil, almost European serenity. The key here is to get lost on purpose.

Cycle down tree-lined avenues like Fuxing Road and Hunan Road, where plane trees form a majestic canopy overhead, their dappled light creating a perfect, soft-focus filter for your camera. Point your lens at the stunning shikumen (stone-gate) houses and the elegant art-deco villas tucked away behind high walls. Each turn reveals a new story. Stop for a coffee at a hidden courtyard café on Julu Road, filming your quiet moment of respite. Cruise down Xinle Road, a hotspot for boutique shopping and quirky snack stalls—perfect for a quick "mid-ride fuel" segment in your vlog.

Don't miss the charming Fuxing Park, where you can capture a slice of local life: elderly residents practicing tai chi, playing cards, or ballroom dancing in the open air. It’s a vibrant, human-centered scene that provides a beautiful contrast to the city's modernity.

The Waterfront Explorer: Pudong's Riverside Ride

For a completely different vibe, one that feels sleek, modern, and expansive, head to the Pudong Riverside Promenade. This route offers unobstructed, panoramic views of the classic Bund skyline that you just cycled past, providing the "other side of the story."

The Promenade: A Vlogger's Dream of Wide-Open Spaces

Starting from the Lujiazui area, you can easily access the newly developed sections of the promenade. The path is incredibly wide, smooth, and purpose-built for cyclists and pedestrians. There are no cars, no intersections—just you, your bike, and a breathtaking vista. This is the place for those smooth, tracking shots. Mount your camera on your handlebars or get a follow-shot of a riding partner with the iconic Bund skyline as your backdrop.

The scale here is immense. As you glide south, you pass by the magnificent Mercedes-Benz Arena, a structure that looks like a floating pearl, and the futuristic spaceship-like structure of the Aurora Museum. The clean, architectural lines make for fantastic cutaway shots in your vlog. The further you go, the more the landscape opens up, leading towards the Nanpu Bridge and beyond. This route is particularly stunning at sunset, when the western sun sets the historic Bund buildings on fire, creating a silhouette that is pure magic.

Pudong's Artistic Flair: The Gallery Road Detour

A short detour from the main riverside path takes you into the heart of Pudong's cultural scene. Head towards the Binjiang Green Area, where you'll find a cluster of art museums. While you might not have time to go in, the exteriors themselves are worth the trip. The sleek, modern designs against the green parks and blue sky offer a different aesthetic for your vlog's visual portfolio. It’s a great spot to talk about Shanghai's dual identity not just as a financial hub, but as a burgeoning center for arts and culture.

The Cultural & Culinary Crawl: Jing'an to the Old City

This route is for the vlogger who wants to capture the essence of daily Shanghai life, from its spiritual centers to its culinary soul. It's a journey of sensory overload in the best way possible.

Temple of Tranquility: Jing'an to Jade Buddha Temple

Begin in the hyper-modern Jing'an District, right beneath the dazzling skyscrapers. Your first major landmark is the Jing'an Temple itself—a golden, majestic Buddhist temple nestled incongruously between steel and glass towers. The contrast is jarring and utterly fascinating. Capture the serene faces of the temple's statues against the reflection of a modern office building. From here, navigate your way west and north towards the famous Jade Buddha Temple. The ride will take you through a mix of commercial and residential neighborhoods, giving your audience a glimpse of the "real" Shanghai that exists between the tourist spots.

The Jade Buddha Temple, home to two exquisite jade Buddha statues brought from Burma, offers a more intimate and deeply spiritual atmosphere. The vibrant reds and golds of the temple complex provide a rich, warm color palette for your footage.

The Final Stretch: Into the Heart of the Old City

From the temples, we embark on the final leg of our journey, heading south towards the Old City and the iconic Yu Garden. The energy shifts dramatically. The streets narrow, the sounds amplify, and the scents of street food fill the air. This is a challenging but incredibly rewarding ride for a vlogger. Weave through the bustling crowds (walking your bike is often necessary), your camera capturing the vibrant chaos.

Your destination is the Yu Garden Bazaar. The beautiful Ming-dynasty style architecture, the zigzag bridge over the pond, and the stunning Huxinting Teahouse are all legendary film locations. This area is a sensory feast. This is the perfect place for a "foodie" segment. Film yourself trying a xiaolongbao (soup dumpling) from a famous spot like Nanxiang Steamed Bun Restaurant, or biting into a sticky, sweet tanghulu (candied fruit). The dynamic energy, the traditional architecture, and the incredible food make for a captivating finale to your vlog, showcasing the timeless heart of Shanghai that still beats strong amidst the relentless march of progress.

Each of these routes tells a different story of Shanghai. The key to a great vlog is to not just show the path, but to capture the feeling—the wind on the Bund, the quiet of the French Concession, the awe of the Pudong skyline, and the vibrant pulse of the Old City. So get out there, hit record, and let Shanghai unfold before your handlebars.
