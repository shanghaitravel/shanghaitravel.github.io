How to Find Pet-Friendly Taxis in Shanghai

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shanghai is a bustling metropolis known for its vibrant culture, stunning skyline, and pet-loving residents. Whether you're a local or a traveler exploring the city with your furry friend, finding a pet-friendly taxi can be a challenge. Unlike some Western cities where pet-friendly transportation is common, Shanghai’s taxi services have specific rules and limited options for pets. But don’t worry—this guide will walk you through everything you need to know to secure a smooth ride for you and your pet.

Understanding Shanghai’s Taxi Policies for Pets

Before hailing a cab, it’s essential to know the general policies regarding pets in Shanghai taxis. Most traditional taxis (like those operated by Dazhong or Qiangsheng) do not explicitly allow pets due to hygiene concerns. However, some drivers may still accommodate small pets if they’re in carriers. Larger dogs, unfortunately, are often turned away.

Ride-Hailing Apps with Pet Options

Luckily, ride-hailing platforms like Didi Chuxing offer more flexibility. While Didi doesn’t have a dedicated "pet-friendly" filter, you can:
- Message the driver in advance (use the app’s chat feature to confirm if they accept pets).
- Opt for premium services (like Didi Premier or Luxe), where drivers are more likely to accommodate special requests.
- Book a larger vehicle (such as a van or SUV) if traveling with a big dog.

Another option is Meituan Dache, which occasionally allows pet transport if the driver agrees. Always double-check before booking!

Alternative Pet-Friendly Transport Solutions

If taxis aren’t cooperating, consider these alternatives:

Pet-Friendly Car Rentals

Companies like eHi Car Rental or Hertz in Shanghai sometimes allow pets in rented vehicles. Just confirm their pet policy when booking.

Private Drivers with Pet Experience

Some expat-friendly services or private drivers (found via WeChat groups or expat forums) specialize in pet transport. A quick search for "Shanghai pet taxi" in groups like SmartShanghai’s forum can yield helpful contacts.

Public Transport (With Caution)

While Shanghai’s metro and buses officially ban pets, small animals in carriers might slip through if discreet. This isn’t foolproof, though—always have a backup plan.

Tips for a Stress-Free Ride with Your Pet

  1. Use a Carrier or Harness
    Even if the driver allows pets, keeping them secured prevents accidents or distractions.

  2. Bring Cleaning Supplies
    A small towel or pet wipes can quickly address any messes, making drivers more willing to accept future pets.

  3. Avoid Rush Hour
    Less traffic means a calmer ride for your pet—and a happier driver.

  4. Tip Generously
    A little extra cash can go a long way in encouraging drivers to welcome pets.

Final Notes

While Shanghai’s pet-friendly taxi scene isn’t as developed as in some cities, persistence and preparation will help you and your furry companion navigate the city with ease. Whether through ride-hailing tricks, private hires, or rentals, there’s always a way to keep your travels pet-inclusive.

Now, go enjoy Shanghai’s pet-friendly cafes, parks, and attractions—your four-legged friend deserves the adventure too!
