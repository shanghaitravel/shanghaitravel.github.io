Pet-Friendly Destinations in Shanghai

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shanghai, one of China's most vibrant and cosmopolitan cities, is an excellent place for pet owners to explore. With its mix of urban landscapes, serene parks, and pet-friendly cafes, the city offers a variety of destinations where pets are welcome. Whether you're a resident or a visitor traveling with your furry friend, here are some top spots in Shanghai where you can enjoy quality time together.

Dog-Friendly Parks and Open Spaces

Century Park

Located in Pudong, Century Park is one of Shanghai’s largest parks and a popular destination for pet owners. The park’s expansive green spaces and walking paths make it an ideal location for a leisurely stroll with your dog.

  • Features:
    The park includes designated pet-friendly areas where dogs can roam off-leash. It’s also a great spot for a picnic or casual playtime with your pet.
  • Tips:
    Pets must remain on-leash in most areas, and owners are responsible for cleaning up after them.

Gucun Forest Park

Situated in Baoshan District, Gucun Forest Park is known for its scenic beauty and pet-friendly environment. The park’s extensive trails and open fields offer plenty of space for pets to explore.

  • Highlights:
    During the spring, the cherry blossom season attracts visitors, creating a stunning backdrop for photos with your pet.
  • Entry:
    There’s a small admission fee, but the park’s facilities and serene atmosphere make it worth the cost.

Binjiang Forest Park

Located near the Yangtze River, Binjiang Forest Park is another excellent choice for pet owners. This lesser-known park provides a quieter setting, perfect for pets who may feel overwhelmed by crowds.

  • Activities:
    Enjoy a walk along the riverside trails or relax in the park’s shaded areas with your pet.
  • Pet Policy:
    Pets are welcome, but leash laws apply.

Dishui Lake

For those willing to venture a bit further from the city center, Dishui Lake in the Nanhui District offers a peaceful retreat. The area surrounding the lake is spacious and suitable for pets to explore.

  • Best Time to Visit:
    Early mornings or evenings provide cooler weather, ensuring your pet stays comfortable.

Pet-Friendly Cafes and Restaurants

Paw Paw Café

Located in Jing'an District, Paw Paw Café is a haven for pet owners. The café features a welcoming atmosphere where pets are not only allowed but celebrated.

  • Menu:
    Enjoy coffee, light snacks, and even pet-friendly treats for your furry companion.
  • Events:
    The café often hosts pet-themed events, making it a great place to meet fellow pet lovers.

My Coffee

Situated in the Former French Concession area, My Coffee is a chic spot where pets are welcome both indoors and outdoors.

  • Ambiance:
    The cozy setting and pet-friendly staff make this café a favorite among locals and tourists alike.
  • Pet Menu:
    They offer special snacks for pets, ensuring your four-legged friend enjoys the visit as much as you do.

Rio Café

Rio Café in Xuhui District is another fantastic choice for dining with pets. With a spacious outdoor seating area, it’s a perfect spot for brunch or a casual meal.

  • Dog-Friendly Features:
    The café provides water bowls and a relaxed environment for pets.

Pet-Friendly Shopping Experiences

Shanghai Pet Fair

Held annually, the Shanghai Pet Fair is a must-visit for pet enthusiasts. The event features stalls selling pet products, demonstrations, and even competitions for pets.

  • What to Expect:
    From gourmet pet food to the latest accessories, the fair has something for every pet owner.

Pet Shops in Tianzifang

Tianzifang, known for its artistic vibe, is also home to several pet-friendly shops. These stores often stock unique pet accessories and offer a chance to browse with your furry friend.

Pet-Friendly Accommodations

Hyatt Regency Shanghai Jiading

This upscale hotel welcomes pets and offers pet-friendly rooms with amenities like pet beds and food bowls.

  • Nearby Attractions:
    The hotel’s location provides easy access to parks and walking areas.

Campanile Shanghai Bund Hotel

Located near the Bund, this pet-friendly hotel is a convenient option for travelers exploring Shanghai’s historic areas.

  • Pet Policy:
    Pets are allowed with prior notice, and the hotel staff ensures a comfortable stay for you and your pet.

Airbnb Options

Many Airbnb hosts in Shanghai offer pet-friendly accommodations. When booking, ensure the listing explicitly states that pets are allowed.

Tips for Traveling with Pets in Shanghai

  1. Transportation:

    • Taxis and ride-sharing services like Didi often accommodate pets, but it’s courteous to inform the driver in advance.
    • Public transportation, including the metro, typically does not allow pets unless they are in carriers.

  2. Pet Supplies:

    • Bring essentials like water, food, and waste bags. Many parks and cafes provide water bowls, but it’s always good to be prepared.
    • Pet shops are widely available in Shanghai for any supplies you might need.

  3. Health and Safety:

    • Ensure your pet is up-to-date on vaccinations, especially rabies, as it is required by Chinese law.
    • Be mindful of the weather. Shanghai summers can be hot, so plan outdoor activities during cooler parts of the day.

  4. Cultural Sensitivity:

    • While Shanghai is pet-friendly, not everyone is comfortable around animals. Keep your pet under control and respect others’ personal space.

  5. Emergency Vet Care:

    • Have the contact information of a local veterinarian handy. International Pet Hospital and Shanghai Pet Hospital are two reliable options.

Shanghai is an exciting city to explore with your pet, offering a mix of natural beauty, modern amenities, and cultural experiences. By planning ahead and choosing pet-friendly destinations, you can ensure a memorable and enjoyable trip for both you and your furry companion.
