Must-Visit Instagram-Worthy Spots in Shanghai

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shanghai, a bustling metropolis blending historical charm and futuristic modernity, is a treasure trove of photogenic locations. Known for its iconic skyline, charming streets, and artistic spaces, the city offers countless opportunities for creating stunning social media content. From architectural marvels to hidden gems, here are the must-visit wanghong daka di (popular influencer check-in spots) that will elevate your Shanghai experience and make your Instagram feed pop.

The Bund

The Bund, or Waitan, is Shanghai’s most iconic attraction. This waterfront promenade along the Huangpu River boasts breathtaking views of the city's skyline, featuring historic colonial buildings on one side and the futuristic skyscrapers of Pudong on the other.

  • Why It’s Instagram-Worthy:
    The juxtaposition of old and new makes for a stunning backdrop, especially during sunrise, sunset, or at night when the city lights illuminate the skyline.
  • Tips for the Perfect Shot:
    Arrive early to avoid crowds. For a unique perspective, take photos from a rooftop bar or café overlooking the Bund.

Lujiazui Skyline

Across the river from the Bund, Lujiazui is home to Shanghai’s famous skyscrapers, including the Shanghai Tower, Oriental Pearl Tower, and Jin Mao Tower.

  • Why It’s Instagram-Worthy:
    These architectural wonders are perfect for capturing Shanghai’s modern vibe. The Oriental Pearl Tower, with its futuristic design, is a favorite spot for influencers.
  • Tips for the Perfect Shot:
    Visit the Lujiazui pedestrian bridge for a panoramic view. For a dramatic angle, capture the skyline from below the Oriental Pearl Tower.

Tianzifang

Tianzifang is a charming maze of narrow alleys filled with boutique shops, cafés, and art studios, located in the former French Concession.

  • Why It’s Instagram-Worthy:
    The area’s rustic walls, vintage décor, and colorful storefronts make it a delightful location for street photography.
  • Tips for the Perfect Shot:
    Look for quirky details like hanging red lanterns or graffiti murals. Morning visits offer fewer crowds and better natural lighting.

Xintiandi

Xintiandi is a trendy shopping and dining area that combines historic shikumen (stone gate) architecture with modern design.

  • Why It’s Instagram-Worthy:
    The blend of traditional and contemporary styles creates a chic, cosmopolitan aesthetic.
  • Tips for the Perfect Shot:
    Visit in the evening when the lights of the restaurants and shops create a warm, inviting glow. The cobblestone streets add a touch of charm to your photos.

Wukang Road

Wukang Road is a picturesque tree-lined street in the former French Concession, known for its elegant European-style buildings.

  • Why It’s Instagram-Worthy:
    The historic architecture and lush greenery make this street a favorite for lifestyle and fashion photography.
  • Tips for the Perfect Shot:
    Capture the famous Wukang Mansion, a triangular-shaped building that has become an iconic symbol of the area. Early morning visits ensure fewer cars and pedestrians in your shots.

M50 Creative Park

The M50 Creative Park is a hub for contemporary art, featuring galleries, studios, and graffiti-covered walls.

  • Why It’s Instagram-Worthy:
    The industrial setting and bold artwork provide a vibrant and edgy atmosphere for photography.
  • Tips for the Perfect Shot:
    Look for unique graffiti and installations. Don’t be afraid to explore the alleys for hidden gems.

Yu Garden

Yu Garden, or Yuyuan, is a classical Chinese garden that dates back to the Ming Dynasty. Its intricate design includes pavilions, ponds, and rockeries.

  • Why It’s Instagram-Worthy:
    The traditional architecture and serene landscapes make it a perfect spot for capturing Shanghai’s cultural heritage.
  • Tips for the Perfect Shot:
    Visit early in the morning to avoid crowds. Frame your photos with elements like ornate doorways or lotus ponds for added depth.

West Bund Art Center

The West Bund area along the Huangpu River has become a cultural hotspot, featuring museums like the Long Museum and the Yuz Museum.

  • Why It’s Instagram-Worthy:
    The minimalist architecture and riverside views make this area ideal for modern and artistic photography.
  • Tips for the Perfect Shot:
    Explore the Long Museum’s dramatic interiors and staircases for creative compositions. Sunset shots along the river add a golden glow to your photos.

Disneyland Shanghai

For a touch of magic, head to Disneyland Shanghai, home to the world’s largest Disney castle.

  • Why It’s Instagram-Worthy:
    The vibrant attractions, colorful parades, and enchanting castle make this park a dream for Instagrammers.
  • Tips for the Perfect Shot:
    Arrive early to capture the castle without crowds. The evening fireworks provide a spectacular photo opportunity.

Qibao Ancient Town

Located in the Minhang District, Qibao Ancient Town offers a glimpse into traditional Chinese life with its historic architecture, narrow waterways, and local snacks.

  • Why It’s Instagram-Worthy:
    The canals, arched bridges, and lantern-lit streets create a picturesque setting reminiscent of ancient China.
  • Tips for the Perfect Shot:
    Visit during festivals like the Lantern Festival for even more vibrant scenes. Bring a wide-angle lens to capture the bridges and reflections in the water.

Jing’an Temple

Jing’an Temple, a golden Buddhist temple amidst modern skyscrapers, is one of Shanghai’s most photogenic religious sites.

  • Why It’s Instagram-Worthy:
    The temple’s striking gold roofs and intricate details provide a stunning contrast against the urban skyline.
  • Tips for the Perfect Shot:
    Focus on the ornate carvings and golden Buddha statues. Morning light enhances the temple’s glow.

Century Park

Century Park is a large green space in Pudong, perfect for capturing nature and enjoying outdoor activities.

  • Why It’s Instagram-Worthy:
    The park’s serene lakes, cherry blossoms (in spring), and modern sculptures offer diverse photo opportunities.
  • Tips for the Perfect Shot:
    Visit during sunrise or sunset for soft lighting. The bridge over the lake is a particularly photogenic spot.

Duolun Road Cultural Street

Duolun Road is a pedestrian street filled with historical buildings, vintage shops, and quaint cafés.

  • Why It’s Instagram-Worthy:
    The street’s nostalgic charm and artistic vibe make it a hidden gem for photography.
  • Tips for the Perfect Shot:
    Look for old bookstores, vintage signs, and quiet corners for unique compositions.

1933 Laochangfang

This former slaughterhouse turned creative space is an architectural marvel with a maze of bridges, ramps, and spiral staircases.

  • Why It’s Instagram-Worthy:
    The industrial chic design is perfect for minimalist and abstract photography.
  • Tips for the Perfect Shot:
    Play with shadows and angles to highlight the geometric patterns of the structure.

Shanghai is a city that never ceases to inspire, offering endless backdrops for memorable photos. Whether you’re drawn to historical streets, modern architecture, or artistic spaces, these locations ensure your trip to Shanghai will be as picturesque as it is unforgettable.
